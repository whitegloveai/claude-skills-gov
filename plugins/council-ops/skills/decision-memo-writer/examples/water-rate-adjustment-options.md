# Example: Decision Memo — Water Rate Adjustment Options for Cedar Ridge

This is a worked example for the decision-memo-writer skill. It is set in the fictional City of Cedar Ridge and presents three options for a water rate adjustment to take effect October 1, 2026.

## Input

- Topic: Water rate adjustment for FY2027
- Author: Inez Rivera, Finance Director, with input from Public Works Director Raj Patel
- Triggers: rising treatment chemical costs (+18% YoY), debt service on the 2024 water plant expansion bonds, and pending TCEQ compliance work on lead service line inventory
- Options: (1) no rate change; (2) inflation-only adjustment; (3) full cost-of-service adjustment per the recently completed rate study
- Staff recommends Option 3 with a phase-in

## Output

```
TO:        Mayor Castillo and Members of the City Council
FROM:      Inez Rivera, Finance Director
           with Raj Patel, Public Works Director
DATE:      August 14, 2026
SUBJECT:   FY2027 Water Rate Adjustment — Options and Recommendation

BACKGROUND

The City's water utility is funded through enterprise rates that must
recover the full cost of producing and delivering treated water,
maintaining the system, and servicing utility debt. The current rate
structure, adopted in October 2022, was built on a 2021 cost-of-service
study with assumptions that have since been overtaken by three
developments:

  1. Chemical and energy costs at the Cedar Ridge Water Treatment
     Plant have risen 18% year-over-year, driven primarily by sodium
     hypochlorite and ferric sulfate contracts that re-bid in March
     2026.
  2. Annual debt service on the 2024 water plant expansion bonds
     ($82.4M, 25-year tax-exempt) of $5.6M begins in FY2027. This was
     contemplated in the 2021 study but at lower interest rates.
  3. The Texas Commission on Environmental Quality (TCEQ) lead service
     line inventory and replacement program, codified in 30 TAC §
     290.117, requires accelerated replacement of identified lead
     service lines beginning in FY2027. The City's preliminary
     inventory identified 312 likely lead service lines, with
     estimated replacement cost of $4.7M over five years.

Public Works engaged Stantec for an updated rate study in March 2026.
The study recommends a full cost-of-service rate increase of 11.4%
across all classes for FY2027 with a graduated tier structure that
shifts more cost recovery onto high-volume residential users.

The Utility Advisory Committee reviewed the study on July 9 and voted
4–1 to forward it to council without modification. One public open
house was held July 22 with approximately 35 attendees; written
comments are summarized in Attachment 3.

ISSUE

Should the City Council adopt no rate change, an inflation-only
adjustment, or the full cost-of-service adjustment recommended in the
2026 rate study, effective October 1, 2026?

OPTIONS

Option 1: No Rate Change

Description: Maintain current FY2026 water and sewer rates through
FY2027.

Pros:
  - No immediate impact on residential and commercial customers.
  - Avoids the political cost of a rate increase in an election year.

Cons:
  - Water Fund unrestricted reserves projected to fall from $11.2M
    (2.7 months of operations) at FY2026 close to $4.1M (1.0 month) at
    FY2027 close, below the City's adopted 90-day reserve policy.
  - Insufficient revenue to cover full debt service plus required
    capital cash transfers; would force a $2.6M draw from reserves.
  - Defers the lead service line replacement program; the City would
    not be in a position to begin replacements at the TCEQ-required
    pace.

Fiscal Impact: $0 in new revenue. $2.6M draw from reserves in FY2027.
Reserve policy violation by Q3 FY2027.

Legal/Regulatory Considerations: TCEQ enforcement action plausible by
late FY2027 if lead replacement program is not initiated. Bond
covenants under the 2024 issuance require rates sufficient to maintain
1.20x debt service coverage; FY2027 projected coverage at current
rates is 1.06x, which does not meet covenant.

Implementation Timeline: No action required.

Option 2: Inflation-Only Adjustment (4.5%)

Description: Apply a uniform 4.5% increase to all water and sewer rate
classes effective October 1, 2026, indexed to the Bureau of Labor
Statistics' CPI-U for the South Region (most recent 12-month change).

Pros:
  - Modest, predictable rate change; under the 5% threshold many
    residents anchor on.
  - Generates approximately $1.9M in new annual revenue.
  - Maintains 1.18x debt service coverage in FY2027.

Cons:
  - Does not fund the lead service line replacement program at the
    TCEQ-required pace.
  - Does not align rates with the 2026 cost-of-service study; defers
    structural rate redesign and would likely require another increase
    in FY2028.
  - Falls just below the 1.20x bond covenant; modest deterioration in
    operating conditions would put the City out of compliance.

Fiscal Impact: $1.9M in new annual revenue. Average residential bill
increases approximately $3.40/month. Reserve balance stable.

Legal/Regulatory Considerations: TCEQ exposure remains. Bond covenant
margin is thin.

Implementation Timeline: Adoption September 2026 → effective October
1, 2026 → first billed in November 2026.

Option 3: Full Cost-of-Service Adjustment (Stantec Recommendation,
11.4%) with Three-Year Phase-In

Description: Adopt the rate structure recommended in the 2026 Stantec
study, with the 11.4% increase phased in over three years (5.0% in
FY2027, 4.0% in FY2028, 2.0% in FY2029) to spread the impact while
funding the full program.

Pros:
  - Funds the lead service line replacement program at the
    TCEQ-required pace.
  - Restores 1.32x debt service coverage in FY2027.
  - Aligns rate structure with current cost of service.
  - Phase-in moderates customer impact in year one.

Cons:
  - Compounded customer impact over three years.
  - Requires council readoption of the FY2028 and FY2029 steps each
    year (the rate ordinance can pre-authorize them, but a future
    council can defer).

Fiscal Impact: Year 1 revenue +$2.1M; Year 2 +$1.7M; Year 3 +$0.9M.
Average residential bill increases approximately $3.80/month in FY2027,
with cumulative increase of approximately $8.50/month by FY2029.
Reserve balance grows to $13.4M by FY2029.

Legal/Regulatory Considerations: Funds the City's TCEQ obligations.
Comfortably exceeds the 1.20x bond covenant. The phased structure must
be embedded in the rate ordinance (Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 552 grants
the authority to set water rates by ordinance).

Implementation Timeline: Adoption September 2026 → effective October
1, 2026 → automatic FY2028 step on October 1, 2027 (subject to council
review) → automatic FY2029 step on October 1, 2028 (subject to council
review).

RECOMMENDATION

Staff recommends Option 3: Full Cost-of-Service Adjustment with
Three-Year Phase-In.

Reason: Option 3 is the only option that funds the City's TCEQ lead
service line obligations on schedule, maintains comfortable bond
covenant compliance, and aligns rates with the actual cost of service.
The phase-in structure addresses the principal weakness of an
all-at-once adjustment without deferring the underlying structural
shortfall.

Caveats:
  - Council should consider pairing adoption with a customer
    assistance program (CAP) for income-qualified residential
    customers. Staff has modeled a CAP at approximately $180,000
    annual cost; this can be funded within the rate increase.
  - Communications plan attached (Attachment 4) — recommend rolling
    the announcement no later than September 1.

REQUESTED COUNCIL ACTION

  "I move to adopt Ordinance 2026-31 amending Chapter 13 of the Code
  of Ordinances regarding water and sewer rates, adopting the rate
  structure described in Exhibit A of the ordinance, with rates
  effective October 1, 2026, and incorporating a three-year phase-in
  as described in the 2026 cost-of-service study."

ATTACHMENTS

  1. Stantec 2026 Cost-of-Service Study (Executive Summary)
  2. Utility Advisory Committee minutes — July 9, 2026
  3. Public open house comment summary — July 22, 2026
  4. Communications plan
  5. Customer Assistance Program model
  6. Five-year forecast under each option

This memo was drafted with the assistance of artificial intelligence
tools and reviewed by the Finance Director and Public Works Director
before distribution.
```

## Notes for the User

- This memo is intended for the public packet. It does not contain attorney-client privileged content. If a parallel privileged memo is needed (e.g., litigation risk under the bond covenant), route it separately.
- The "Caveats" subsection in Recommendation is where staff acknowledges what the recommendation does not solve. Don't bury caveats.
- Motion language should be drafted with the City Attorney to ensure it matches the form council expects.
