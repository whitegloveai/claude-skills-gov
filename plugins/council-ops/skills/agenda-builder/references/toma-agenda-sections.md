# Texas Open Meetings Act — Agenda Section Reference

This is a working reference for the agenda-builder skill. Citations refer to Tex. Gov't Code Chapter 551 unless otherwise noted. Always verify against the current code text before posting; statutes are amended every legislative session.

## Notice and Posting

| Section | Topic | Practical effect |
|---|---|---|
| § 551.041 | General notice requirement | Notice must state time, place, and subject of each item. "Subject" must be specific enough to alert the public to what will be discussed. |
| § 551.0411 | Item placed by member | A member of the governing body may place an item on a future agenda; the city's procedure for doing so should be clear. |
| § 551.042 | Inquiry from public | If a member of the public raises a subject not on the agenda, the body may make a statement of fact, recite existing policy, or propose to put it on a future agenda — but cannot deliberate. |
| § 551.043 | 72-hour posting | Regular and special meetings must be posted at least 72 hours before the scheduled time. |
| § 551.045 | Emergency / urgent matter | Allows posting at least 1 hour before with a supplemental written notice. The emergency or urgent public necessity must be stated on the notice. |
| § 551.046 | Continuation of meeting | A meeting may be continued to a stated time and place without re-posting. |
| § 551.049 | Posted by clerk or secretary | Notice posted on a bulletin board readily accessible to the general public at all times. |
| § 551.050 | Notice for special districts and counties | Different posting rules apply to certain entities; not directly relevant to municipalities but useful when interlocal items are on the agenda. |
| § 551.056 | Internet posting (population 48,000+) | Cities with population 48,000 or more must also post on the entity's internet website. Many smaller cities post online voluntarily. |
| § 551.127 | Videoconferencing | Allows videoconference meetings under specific conditions; requires a quorum present at the location specified in the notice or as the act otherwise provides. |

## Standard Agenda Order

A typical Texas city council agenda runs in this order. Item numbering is local custom.

1. Call to Order
2. Invocation / Pledges of Allegiance (US and Texas flag)
3. Public Comment under § 551.007
4. Consent Agenda
5. Public Hearings (each citing the controlling substantive statute)
6. Regular Business / Individual Consideration (discussion and possible action items)
7. Executive Session (each item citing the § 551.07x exception)
8. Reconvene in Open Session — Action on Executive Session Items
9. Council/Manager Comments and Future Agenda Items
10. Adjournment

## Public Comment Rules — § 551.007

- Reasonable rules can govern public comment, including time limits and registration requirements.
- The body cannot deny comment based on viewpoint.
- The body cannot deliberate or take action on subjects not posted on the agenda.
- Translation accommodation is required if the entity requires English-only comment under certain circumstances; check current statutory text.

## Executive Session Exceptions — Cite Specifically on the Agenda

| Section | Topic | Common use |
|---|---|---|
| § 551.071 | Consultation with attorney | Pending or contemplated litigation, or when ethical duty of confidentiality conflicts with the act. The attorney must be present in person or by phone. |
| § 551.072 | Real property | Deliberation regarding the purchase, exchange, lease, or value of real property where open deliberation would have a detrimental effect on negotiations. |
| § 551.073 | Prospective gifts | Negotiated contracts for prospective gifts or donations. |
| § 551.074 | Personnel matters | Deliberation regarding the appointment, employment, evaluation, reassignment, duties, discipline, or dismissal of a public officer or employee. The officer/employee may request open hearing. |
| § 551.0745 | County advisory body | Specific to counties. |
| § 551.076 | Security | Security personnel or device deployment. |
| § 551.0785 | Medical or psychiatric records | Personally identifiable medical/psychiatric records. |
| § 551.082 | School districts | Specific to school districts. |
| § 551.0821 | Personal information of student | School districts. |
| § 551.083 | Certain school district matters | Consultation with representative of employee group. |
| § 551.084 | Investigation of alleged misconduct | Closed meeting to investigate. |
| § 551.086 | Certain public power utilities | Competitive matters. |
| § 551.087 | Economic development | Deliberation regarding economic development negotiations — incentives offered to a business prospect. |
| § 551.089 | Information security | Deployment of information resources technology, network security. |
| § 551.090 | Certain governing bodies | Specific narrow exceptions. |

## Specific Statutes That Govern Particular Public Hearing Items

- **Budget adoption** — Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 102.006 (city budget hearing notice).
- **Property tax rate** — Tex. Tax Code § 26.05–26.06 (notice of public hearing on tax rate).
- **Zoning amendments** — Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 211, including § 211.006 (notice of zoning hearing).
- **Annexation** — Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 43.
- **Capital improvement plan / impact fees** — Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 395.
- **Special use permits / variances** — local zoning ordinance plus Ch. 211.
- **Solid waste / utility rates** — local code; some require Tex. Health & Safety Code or PUC notice.

## Consequences of TOMA Violations

- Action taken in violation is voidable under § 551.141.
- Criminal penalties apply for knowing violations under §§ 551.143–551.146 (closed meetings, conspiracy, calling, participating).
- Civil enforcement: declaratory and injunctive relief under § 551.142.

## Practical Drafting Checklist

- Each item caption is specific enough that a member of the public could understand what will be considered.
- Each public hearing has a substantive citation.
- Each executive session item has a § 551.07x citation.
- Posting timestamp is before the meeting by the required margin.
- The agenda matches what the body will actually consider; no after-the-fact add-ons.
- Internet posting (where required) goes up at the same time as physical posting.

This reference is informational and is not legal advice. Consult the city attorney before posting any agenda where the application of the act is unclear.
