# Example: Ordinance Tracker — Cedar Ridge Tree Preservation Ordinance

This is a worked example for the ordinance-tracker skill. It tracks a fictional tree preservation ordinance for the City of Cedar Ridge from introduction through adoption.

## Input

- Working title: "An Ordinance Establishing a Tree Preservation Program for the City of Cedar Ridge"
- Ordinance number: TBD at introduction; later assigned 2026-22
- Sponsor: Council Member Sarah Davies and the Planning Department
- Type: Regulatory (zoning-adjacent; touches the development code)
- Effect: Establishes a tree-protection permit, replacement standards, fee schedule, and enforcement provisions for development activity on lots ≥ 1 acre

## Output (the tracker as it appears at three points in time)


## Notes for the User

- This example shows the tracker evolving across three checkpoints. The History section is appended; entries are never deleted.
- The "Effective Date" line appears once the ordinance is adopted with a delayed effective date (here, July 1).
- After codification, the tracker can be marked Closed and filed; outstanding items at that point should be limited to operational rollout.
