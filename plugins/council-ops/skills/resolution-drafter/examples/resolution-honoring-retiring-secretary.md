# Example: Resolution Honoring Retiring City Secretary — Cedar Ridge

This is a worked example for the resolution-drafter skill. It produces Resolution 2026-14, the City of Cedar Ridge resolution honoring City Secretary Helen Marquez upon her retirement. The same resolution is referenced in the agenda-builder, minutes-drafter, and council-packet-prep examples.

## Input

- Type: Recognition / honor
- Subject: Helen Marquez, City Secretary, retiring after twenty-five years of service
- Sponsor: Mayor Elena Castillo
- Resolution number: 2026-14
- Adoption date: June 9, 2026 (regular meeting)
- Key facts:
  - Marquez began service in 2001
  - Held positions of Deputy City Secretary (2001–2008) and City Secretary (2008–2026)
  - Certified Texas Municipal Clerk; served on the TMCA Board 2018–2020
  - Oversaw twelve city elections without irregularity
  - Mentored seven junior staff members who went on to lead clerk's offices in other Texas cities

## Output

```
RESOLUTION NO. 2026-14

A RESOLUTION OF THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE, TEXAS,
RECOGNIZING CITY SECRETARY HELEN MARQUEZ UPON HER RETIREMENT AFTER
TWENTY-FIVE YEARS OF SERVICE TO THE CITY; AND PROVIDING FOR AN
EFFECTIVE DATE.

WHEREAS, Helen Marquez began her service to the City of Cedar Ridge
on August 6, 2001, as Deputy City Secretary, and was appointed City
Secretary by the City Council on November 4, 2008;

WHEREAS, during her twenty-five years of service, Ms. Marquez has
administered twelve municipal elections without irregularity, has
preserved the official records of the City of Cedar Ridge, and has
served as the principal point of contact between the City Council and
the public she serves;

WHEREAS, Ms. Marquez earned the Texas Registered Municipal Clerk
designation in 2006 and the Certified Municipal Clerk designation
shortly thereafter, and has served on the Board of the Texas Municipal
Clerks Association from 2018 to 2020;

WHEREAS, Ms. Marquez has mentored seven members of her staff who have
gone on to serve as principal clerks in other Texas municipalities,
extending the standard of public service she has set in Cedar Ridge
beyond the boundaries of this City;

WHEREAS, the City Council and the residents of Cedar Ridge have
benefited from Ms. Marquez's professionalism, her institutional memory,
and her quiet stewardship of the work that makes municipal government
function;

WHEREAS, Ms. Marquez has announced her retirement effective June 30,
2026, and the City Council wishes to recognize her contributions on the
record;

NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE CITY COUNCIL OF THE CITY OF
CEDAR RIDGE, TEXAS:

Section 1. The City Council expresses its profound appreciation to
Helen Marquez for her twenty-five years of service to the City of
Cedar Ridge.

Section 2. The City Council commends Ms. Marquez for her stewardship
of the City's records, her administration of municipal elections, and
her mentorship of the next generation of Texas municipal clerks.

Section 3. The City Council extends to Ms. Marquez and her family its
best wishes for a long, healthy, and rewarding retirement.

Section 4. The City Secretary's office is directed to provide a copy
of this Resolution to Ms. Marquez and to record this Resolution in the
official records of the City.

Section 5. This Resolution shall take effect immediately upon its
passage.

PASSED AND APPROVED this 9th day of June, 2026, at a regular meeting
of the City Council of the City of Cedar Ridge, Texas, by a vote of
6 ayes, 0 nays, 0 abstentions, with 1 absent.

____________________________________
Elena Castillo, Mayor

ATTEST:

____________________________________
Helen Marquez, City Secretary

APPROVED AS TO FORM:

____________________________________
Theresa Kowalski, City Attorney

This document was drafted with the assistance of artificial intelligence
tools and reviewed by the City Attorney before adoption.
```

## Notes for the User

- For a recognition resolution, three to six whereas clauses is typical. More than that risks losing the reader.
- The City Secretary attests her own retirement resolution because her duty to attest persists until her last day in office; if the city wants the Deputy City Secretary to attest, that's permissible but should be confirmed with the City Attorney.
- Section 4 (directing distribution and recording) is optional in many recognition resolutions; include it when the council wants the resolution physically delivered to the honoree.
- For a position-statement or authorization resolution, the operative sections will be more substantive — cite the underlying authority, identify the official authorized to act, and specify any limits.
