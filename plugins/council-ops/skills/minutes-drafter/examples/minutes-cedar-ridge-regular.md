# Example: Minutes — City of Cedar Ridge Regular Meeting, June 9, 2026

This is a worked example for the minutes-drafter skill. It uses the same fictional Cedar Ridge meeting as the agenda-builder example.

## Input

- The June 9, 2026 agenda (see agenda-builder example)
- Note-taker draft from the City Secretary's office
- Vote tallies for each action item
- Attendance: all 7 members present except Council Member Owens (absent, excused)

## Output

```
CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE, TEXAS
CITY COUNCIL — REGULAR MEETING MINUTES
Tuesday, June 9, 2026 — 6:30 p.m.
Council Chambers, Cedar Ridge City Hall
200 Live Oak Drive, Cedar Ridge, TX 75000

ATTENDANCE
Present:
  Mayor Elena Castillo
  Mayor Pro Tem Marcus Brennan
  Council Member Aditi Patel
  Council Member Diego Ramirez
  Council Member Wen Yu
  Council Member Sarah Davies

Absent:
  Council Member James Owens (excused)

Also Present:
  Daniel Ahn, City Manager
  Helen Marquez, City Secretary
  Theresa Kowalski, City Attorney
  Inez Rivera, Finance Director
  Adaeze Okafor, Planning Director
  Raj Patel, Public Works Director

1. CALL TO ORDER
Mayor Castillo called the meeting to order at 6:31 p.m. and confirmed a
quorum of six members was present.

2. INVOCATION AND PLEDGES OF ALLEGIANCE
Pastor Lee Hwang of Cedar Ridge Community Church gave the invocation.
The Council and audience recited the pledges to the United States flag
and the State of Texas flag.

3. PUBLIC COMMENT
Three speakers addressed the Council:

  - Maria Sandoval (1208 Pecan Street) spoke in favor of the Z-2026-04
    rezoning at 1100 Pecan Street, citing the need for neighborhood
    services within walking distance.
  - Robert Tinsley (1304 Pecan Street) opposed the rezoning, expressing
    concerns about traffic and parking.
  - Linda Yates (Cedar Ridge ISD parent) thanked the Council for the
    school zone signage installed in May.

The Mayor reminded speakers that the Council could not deliberate on
items not posted on the agenda, in accordance with Tex. Gov't Code
§ 551.042.

4. CONSENT AGENDA
On a motion by Council Member Ramirez, seconded by Council Member Yu,
the Council approved consent items 4a through 4c. Vote: Ayes — 6
(Castillo, Brennan, Patel, Ramirez, Yu, Davies); Nays — 0; Absent — 1
(Owens). Motion carried 6–0.

   4a. Minutes of the May 26, 2026 regular meeting.
   4b. Check register for May 1–31, 2026, in the amount of $1,847,302.14.
   4c. Interlocal cooperation agreement with Caddo County for animal
       control services for FY2026, not to exceed $84,000.

5. PUBLIC HEARINGS

   5a. PUBLIC HEARING — FY2026 BUDGET AMENDMENT
   Mayor Castillo opened the public hearing at 6:42 p.m. Finance
   Director Rivera presented the proposed amendment reallocating
   $312,000 from the General Fund contingency reserve to the Street
   Maintenance program, citing higher-than-projected fuel and asphalt
   costs. No members of the public addressed the Council on this item.
   Mayor Castillo closed the public hearing at 6:48 p.m.

   On a motion by Council Member Davies, seconded by Council Member
   Patel, the Council adopted the FY2026 budget amendment. Vote: Ayes — 6
   (Castillo, Brennan, Patel, Ramirez, Yu, Davies); Nays — 0; Absent — 1
   (Owens). Motion carried 6–0.

   5b. PUBLIC HEARING — REZONING CASE Z-2026-04, 1100 PECAN STREET
   Mayor Castillo opened the public hearing at 6:51 p.m. Planning
   Director Okafor presented the staff report and the Planning &
   Zoning Commission's 5–2 recommendation to approve. The applicant,
   Cedar Ridge Holdings LLC, was represented by Marisol Reyes. Three
   members of the public spoke in favor (including Ms. Sandoval, who
   reiterated her earlier comment) and four spoke in opposition,
   primarily citing traffic concerns. Mayor Castillo closed the public
   hearing at 7:14 p.m.

   Council Member Brennan stated that the City Secretary had received
   one written protest under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 211.006(d), and
   that the protest area did not meet the 20% threshold to require a
   three-fourths affirmative vote.

   On a motion by Council Member Patel, seconded by Council Member Yu,
   the Council approved Case Z-2026-04, rezoning 1100 Pecan Street from
   Single-Family Residential (R-1) to Neighborhood Commercial (NC-1),
   subject to the conditions listed in the staff report. Vote: Ayes — 4
   (Castillo, Patel, Yu, Davies); Nays — 2 (Brennan, Ramirez); Absent — 1
   (Owens). Motion carried 4–2.

6. REGULAR BUSINESS

   6a. FY2026 STREET OVERLAY CONTRACT AWARD
   Public Works Director Patel presented the bid tabulation. Three bids
   were received in response to the formal advertisement. Staff
   recommended award to Lone Star Paving in the amount of $1,142,800
   as the lowest responsible bidder.

   On a motion by Council Member Davies, seconded by Council Member
   Brennan, the Council awarded the FY2026 street overlay contract to
   Lone Star Paving in an amount not to exceed $1,142,800, and
   authorized the City Manager to execute the contract upon review by
   the City Attorney. Vote: Ayes — 6; Nays — 0; Absent — 1 (Owens).
   Motion carried 6–0.

   6b. RESOLUTION 2026-14 — RECOGNITION OF CITY SECRETARY HELEN MARQUEZ
   Mayor Castillo read Resolution 2026-14 honoring City Secretary Helen
   Marquez upon her retirement after twenty-five years of service. The
   Mayor and each Council member offered remarks of appreciation.

   On a motion by Mayor Castillo, seconded by Council Member Ramirez,
   the Council adopted Resolution 2026-14. Vote: Ayes — 6; Nays — 0;
   Absent — 1 (Owens). Motion carried 6–0.

7. EXECUTIVE SESSION
The Council recessed into executive session at 7:54 p.m. under Tex.
Gov. Code §§ 551.071 (consultation with attorney regarding pending
litigation in *Smith v. City of Cedar Ridge*, Cause No. 2026-CV-118)
and 551.074 (annual evaluation of City Manager Daniel Ahn).

The Council reconvened in open session at 8:46 p.m.

8. ACTION ON EXECUTIVE SESSION ITEMS
On a motion by Mayor Pro Tem Brennan, seconded by Council Member
Davies, the Council directed the City Attorney to proceed with the
litigation strategy discussed in executive session. Vote: Ayes — 6;
Nays — 0; Absent — 1 (Owens). Motion carried 6–0.

No action was taken regarding the City Manager evaluation.

9. CITY MANAGER AND COUNCIL COMMENTS
City Manager Ahn announced the July 4 community celebration at Pecan
Park. Council Member Yu thanked the Public Works staff for storm
cleanup the prior week. No deliberation occurred.

10. ADJOURNMENT
There being no further business, Mayor Castillo adjourned the meeting
at 8:54 p.m.

____________________________________
Elena Castillo, Mayor

ATTEST:

____________________________________
Helen Marquez, City Secretary

This document was drafted with the assistance of artificial intelligence
tools and reviewed by the City Secretary before publication.
```

## Items Flagged for Reviewer

- Confirm the protest tally on Case Z-2026-04. The note-taker draft said "one written protest" but the staff report references three letters of opposition; verify whether the additional letters were timely written protests under § 211.006(d) or general public comment.
- The City Manager evaluation under § 551.074 produced no action; verify with the Mayor whether council intends to take action at a future meeting and whether contract terms require a vote within a specific window.
- Spelling: confirm "Adaeze Okafor" and "Theresa Kowalski" against personnel records.
