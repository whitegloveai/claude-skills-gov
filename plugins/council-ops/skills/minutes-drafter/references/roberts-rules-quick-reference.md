# Robert's Rules Quick Reference for Council Minutes

This is a working reference for the minutes-drafter skill. Most Texas city councils use Robert's Rules of Order Newly Revised (RONR) as their default parliamentary authority unless local rules say otherwise. Some councils adopt simplified or hybrid rules; check the local rules first.

This reference focuses on the motion language that appears in minutes. It is not a full parliamentary manual.

## Core Motion Vocabulary

- **Motion**: a formal proposal that the council take action.
- **Second**: another member's required indication that the motion should be considered. Most main motions need a second.
- **Mover**: the member who made the motion.
- **Pending motion**: the motion currently before the council.
- **Question**: in parliamentary usage, the same as "motion."
- **Carried**: the motion passed.
- **Failed**: the motion did not pass.
- **Withdrawn**: the mover took the motion back before a vote.

## Types of Motions That Commonly Appear in Minutes

| Motion | What it does | Needs second? | Vote required |
|---|---|---|---|
| Main motion | Proposes a substantive action | Yes | Majority of members present and voting (unless charter or statute requires more) |
| Amend | Modifies a pending motion before the vote | Yes | Majority |
| Substitute motion | Replaces a pending motion with a different one on the same subject | Yes | Majority |
| Table / Lay on the table | Sets the motion aside, intended to be taken up later in the same meeting | Yes | Majority |
| Postpone to a date certain | Defers the motion to a specific future meeting | Yes | Majority |
| Postpone indefinitely | Effectively kills the motion without a direct vote on the merits | Yes | Majority |
| Refer to committee | Sends the matter to a committee for study | Yes | Majority |
| Call the question / Move the previous question | Closes debate and forces a vote | Yes | Two-thirds (RONR) |
| Reconsider | Revisits a vote taken earlier in the same meeting | Yes; mover must have voted on prevailing side | Majority |
| Rescind | Repeals a prior action taken at a previous meeting | Yes | Two-thirds (or majority with notice) |
| Adjourn | Ends the meeting | Yes | Majority |
| Recess | Pauses the meeting briefly | Yes | Majority |
| Point of order | Raises a procedural objection | No | Chair rules |
| Appeal the ruling of the chair | Challenges the chair's procedural ruling | Yes | Majority |
| Suspend the rules | Temporarily sets aside a rule (cannot suspend a statute or charter provision) | Yes | Two-thirds |

## Common Texas Statutory Vote Thresholds

These override RONR's default majorities when applicable. Always check the city charter and the controlling statute.

- **Budget adoption** — majority of governing body (Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 102.007).
- **Tax rate adoption** — depends on whether the rate exceeds the no-new-revenue rate or voter-approval rate; see Tex. Tax Code Ch. 26.
- **Bond election** — varies; see Public Securities provisions.
- **Zoning amendment with valid protest** — three-fourths affirmative vote of all members of the governing body required if 20% of property owners within the area protest, under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 211.006(d).
- **Emergency ordinance** — depends on charter; many home-rule charters require five-sixths or unanimous to suspend the two-reading rule.
- **Executive session entry** — no special vote required, but a quorum must be present and the statutory citation must be on the agenda.

## Standard Motion Language for Minutes

Use this pattern:

> On a motion by [Member], seconded by [Member], the Council [action verb] [item description]. Vote: Ayes — [N] ([list]); Nays — [N] ([list]); Abstain — [N] ([list]); Absent — [N] ([list]). Motion carried [N–N].

Examples:

- *On a motion by Council Member A, seconded by Council Member B, the Council approved Resolution 2026-14. Vote: Ayes — 6 (Mayor, Members A, B, C, D, E); Nays — 0; Abstain — 0; Absent — 1 (Member F). Motion carried 6–0.*
- *On a motion by Council Member C, seconded by Council Member D, the Council postponed Item 6a to the regular meeting of June 23, 2026. Vote: Ayes — 4; Nays — 3 (Mayor, Members A, E). Motion carried 4–3.*

## Things That Should NOT Appear in Open-Session Minutes

- Anything said during executive session (record the entry/exit time and statutory citation only; substantive deliberation goes in the certified agenda or recording maintained under § 551.103).
- Editorial characterization of a speaker's tone, motivation, or demeanor.
- Members' off-the-record asides.
- Internal staff conversations not addressed to council.
- Disputed factual claims stated as if true (paraphrase what was *asserted* by the speaker).

## Things That MUST Appear

- Date, time, and place of meeting.
- Members present and absent.
- All motions made (including those that failed or were withdrawn).
- All votes, including roll-call votes when statute or charter requires.
- Executive session entry, statutory citation, and exit times.
- Any action taken in open session after executive session.
- Adjournment time.

## Approval and Amendment of Minutes

- Minutes are typically approved at the next regular meeting on the consent agenda.
- A member may move to amend the draft minutes; amendments need a second and a majority vote.
- Once approved, minutes are signed by the mayor (or presiding officer) and attested by the city secretary.
- Approved minutes can later be corrected only by motion to amend something previously adopted (RONR §35).

This reference is informational. The city attorney has the final word on whether minutes language meets statutory requirements.
