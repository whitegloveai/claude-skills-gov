# Example: Public Comment Summary — Cedar Ridge FY26 Budget Hearing

This is a worked example for the public-comment-summarizer skill. It is set during the City of Cedar Ridge FY26 budget public hearing held August 18, 2025. Six speakers addressed the Council across four topics.

## Input

- Meeting: Cedar Ridge City Council, August 18, 2025
- Item: Public hearing on the FY2026 proposed budget under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 102.006
- Speakers (in order): Patricia Long, Mark Underwood, Reverend Jonas Whitfield, Christine Ngo, Jay Holmgren, Lisa Park
- Source: Council Chamber recording, time-coded transcript

## Output

```
PUBLIC COMMENT SUMMARY
City of Cedar Ridge — Public Hearing on FY2026 Proposed Budget
August 18, 2025

Total speakers registered: 7
Total speakers heard: 6 (one registered speaker did not appear when called)
Written comments submitted: 2 (filed with the City Secretary the same day)

By Topic:

Property Tax Rate (3 speakers)
Three speakers addressed the proposed property tax rate. Mr. Underwood
and Ms. Park stated that the no-new-revenue rate would be more
appropriate given economic pressure on homeowners and small businesses.
Ms. Long expressed support for the proposed rate, citing the need to
fully fund public safety personnel costs.

Public Safety Funding (2 speakers)
Ms. Long and Reverend Whitfield expressed support for the proposed 4.5%
increase in the Police Department personnel budget. Reverend Whitfield
also requested that the Council consider expanding mental health
co-response funding in next year's cycle.

Parks and Recreation (1 speaker)
Ms. Ngo requested that the Council restore the $48,000 reduction to the
parks summer programs line, noting youth participation gains in the
prior fiscal year.

Code Enforcement (2 speakers)
Mr. Holmgren and Mr. Underwood raised concerns about response times for
property maintenance complaints in the Eastside neighborhood. Mr.
Holmgren requested a dedicated code enforcement officer be funded.

Position Counts on the Posted Item:
  Posted Item: Public hearing on the FY2026 proposed budget.
  4 speakers expressed support for elements of the proposed budget as
  drafted; 3 speakers requested specific modifications (the totals
  exceed 6 because three speakers addressed multiple elements).

Procedural Notes:
  - Each speaker was allotted three minutes. The chair called time on
    one speaker (Mr. Holmgren) at the three-minute mark; he yielded.
  - One registered speaker (S. Mahdavi) was not present when called.
  - Written comments from F. Olivera and J. Tran were filed with the
    City Secretary at 6:25 p.m. and entered into the meeting record.

Speakers (in order heard):
1. Patricia Long, registered topic: budget — public safety
2. Mark Underwood, registered topic: tax rate
3. Reverend Jonas Whitfield, registered topic: public safety
4. Christine Ngo, registered topic: parks and recreation
5. Jay Holmgren, registered topic: code enforcement
6. Lisa Park, registered topic: tax rate

The full recording of the August 18, 2025 public comment and budget
hearing is retained under the City's records management program and
is available through the City Secretary's office under Tex. Gov't Code
Ch. 552.

This summary was drafted with the assistance of artificial intelligence
tools and reviewed by the City Secretary before publication.
```

## Notes for the User

- The position count is intentionally framed by element rather than by overall up/down on the budget — public budget hearings rarely produce clean for/against tallies, and forcing them can distort the record.
- One speaker (Mr. Underwood) addressed two topics; he is counted in both.
- The two written comments are referenced but not paraphrased; they should be attached to the staff report when it goes into the packet so the substance is preserved in full.
