# Example — Cedar Ridge July 4th Festival Sponsorship

This is a worked example for the `sponsorship-proposal-helper` skill. Input scenario followed by the drafted proposal.

## Input scenario

David Ngata, the Cedar Ridge Parks Director, is preparing the sponsorship deck for the 2026 Cedar Ridge July 4th Festival at Live Oak Park. The festival is the city's largest annual event. He gave the skill:

- Target: 2026 Cedar Ridge July 4th Festival, Live Oak Park, Saturday, July 4, 2026
- Audience: Cedar Ridge residents and surrounding communities; multi-generational family event
- Past attendance: 8,200 in 2024, 9,400 in 2025 (per CRPD count)
- Past social impressions: 142,000 across city channels, June–July 2025
- Local media: Cedar Ridge Sentinel (weekly), KCRT 99.5 FM, two regional television stations covered the 2025 fireworks
- Mailing list reach: 14,800 households (city e-newsletter)
- Levels:
  - Title: $15,000, limit 1
  - Premier: $5,000, limit 4
  - Supporting: $1,500, limit 8
  - Friend: $500, no limit
- Renewal: One festival; Title sponsor has right of first refusal for the following year at the same level (no escalator without council action)
- Restrictions: standard exclusions plus no alcohol-brand sponsors at the family stage; alcohol-brand sponsors permitted only at the 21+ tasting tent if alcohol sales are authorized that year
- Approval pathway: Staff up to $2,500; Parks Board $2,501–$10,000; Council above $10,000 or any naming. Title tier requires council action.
- Coordinator: Lin Cervantes, Special Events Coordinator, 512-555-0150, lcervantes@cedarridgetx.gov

## Drafted proposal

```
SPONSORSHIP PROPOSAL
2026 Cedar Ridge July 4th Festival
City of Cedar Ridge Parks and Recreation
Saturday, July 4, 2026 | Live Oak Park

OPPORTUNITY OVERVIEW
The Cedar Ridge July 4th Festival is the city's largest annual event.
The 2025 festival drew 9,400 attendees across the afternoon program,
evening concert, and fireworks finale. The festival runs from 4:00 p.m.
to 10:30 p.m. at Live Oak Park and includes a family activities zone,
food vendors, three stages of live music, a youth bike parade, and a
20-minute fireworks show.

Local sponsors gain visibility with a multi-generational audience of
Cedar Ridge residents and visitors from surrounding communities, with
recognition across on-site signage, the city's digital channels, and
the festival program guide distributed in advance through the Cedar
Ridge Sentinel insert.

SPONSORSHIP LEVELS

Title — $15,000 (limit: 1)
- Festival named "[Sponsor] presents the Cedar Ridge July 4th Festival"
  on all signage, printed materials, and digital channels
- 20'×10' branded activation space at festival entrance
- Logo on main stage banner; verbal recognition at every stage
  introduction
- Premium hospitality area for up to 20 guests with reserved viewing
  for fireworks
- Logo on the front of 15,000 program guides
- Right of first refusal for 2027 (council approval required)

Premier — $5,000 (limit: 4)
- 10'×10' branded activation space in vendor area
- Logo on stage banners and program guide interior pages
- Three verbal recognitions per stage across the event
- Hospitality area for up to 8 guests

Supporting — $1,500 (limit: 8)
- 10'×10' booth space (sponsor provides tent and table)
- Logo on program guide and city festival webpage
- One verbal recognition at the family stage

Friend — $500 (no limit)
- Name listed in program guide
- Name listed on the city festival webpage
- Two festival passes for sponsor staff

REACH AND IMPACT
- 2025 attendance: 9,400 (counted by CRPD entry-point tally)
- 2024 attendance: 8,200
- Social impressions, June–July 2025 (city channels): 142,000
- Local media: weekly coverage in the Cedar Ridge Sentinel; live spots
  on KCRT 99.5 FM; regional TV coverage of fireworks in 2025
- City newsletter reach: 14,800 households

RECOGNITION MECHANISMS
- On-site signage: stage banners, entrance arches, activation booths
- Stage announcements: scripted by the festival emcee
- Printed materials: 15,000 program guides distributed via the Sentinel
  insert and on-site
- Digital channels: city website festival page, three social platforms,
  city e-newsletter (4 sends)
- Press release: sponsor list included in the festival press release
  issued 30 days before the event

TERM AND RENEWAL
One festival. Title sponsor has right of first refusal for the 2027
festival at the same level; any increase requires separate council
action. Other levels open to all eligible applicants each year.

RESTRICTIONS
The City of Cedar Ridge does not accept sponsorship from: tobacco and
vaping products, cannabis (regardless of legal status), sexually
oriented businesses, political candidates and PACs, predatory lending
products, and any product or service inconsistent with adopted City
policy.

Alcohol-brand sponsors are not eligible for visibility at the family
activities zone or family stage. Alcohol-brand sponsors may activate
only at the 21+ tasting tent if alcohol sales are authorized for the
2026 festival.

Sponsor messaging displayed at the festival is subject to the City's
content guidelines and the First Amendment. The City retains editorial
control over how sponsor recognition appears. No sponsor benefit
grants authority over festival programming, vendor selection, or City
staff.

APPROVAL PATHWAY
- Staff acceptance: sponsorships up to $2,500
- Parks Board acceptance: $2,501 to $10,000 (regular meeting consent)
- City Council acceptance: above $10,000, and any sponsorship of the
  Title tier
Title tier acceptance will be placed on the council consent agenda
within 30 days of sponsor commitment.

OUT CLAUSE
Either party may terminate this sponsorship in writing with 30 days
notice. The City may terminate immediately if the sponsor's conduct or
product line becomes inconsistent with the restrictions above; refund
pro-rated to days remaining before the event.

CONTACT
Lin Cervantes, Special Events Coordinator
512-555-0150 | lcervantes@cedarridgetx.gov
City of Cedar Ridge Parks and Recreation | cedarridgetx.gov/festival

This proposal is a solicitation of an offer of sponsorship and not a
binding contract. The sponsorship agreement, if accepted, will be
documented in a separate written agreement signed by the sponsor and
the City following the approval pathway above.

AI-assisted draft, reviewed by David Ngata, Parks Director, and
approved for circulation by City Manager Daniel Ahn.
```

## Why this proposal works

- Reach numbers are documented, not invented. The 2025 attendance count is from the CRPD entry tally; the social impressions are from the city's analytics. A sponsor's marketing director can verify them.
- The Title tier carries a single sponsor limit and triggers council action, which keeps the city from accidentally selling the festival's identity without a public vote.
- The alcohol-brand carve-out is specific: alcohol brands are not at the family stage but are eligible at the 21+ tasting tent if alcohol sales are authorized. That language survives a council briefing.
- The restrictions section pairs the categorical exclusions with the First Amendment editorial-control clause. Both are necessary; one without the other is incomplete.
- The right-of-first-refusal language is bounded — Title sponsor returns at the same level without escalator unless council acts. This prevents creep into long-term naming rights without a public process.
- The disclaimer at the end keeps the proposal from being a binding contract.
