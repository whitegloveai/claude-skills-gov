# Facility Rental Response

> Drafts a complete reply to a facility rental inquiry. Built for parks rental coordinators who need to send a response that names the price, names the policies, and does not accidentally commit the city to a booking before the paperwork lands.

## When to Use

- "Someone wants to rent the city park pavilion for a wedding. Draft my reply."
- "A church is asking about renting the community center gym on Sundays."
- "Quinceañera inquiry for the community center ballroom. Reply."
- "Corporate team wants to rent the athletic field for a kickball tournament."
- Any inquiry needing availability, rates, full policy set, and next-step paperwork.

## Inputs Needed

Required:

- Original inquiry text
- Requested facility
- Requested date(s) and time window
- Event type and approximate headcount
- Whether alcohol service is requested
- Applicant name, organization, contact phone and email
- Current calendar status (open, held, booked)

Optional:

- Resident vs. non-resident status
- Repeat-customer or partner-agency status
- Known accessibility needs of host or guests
- Whether the applicant has rented before

If date and facility are missing or calendar status is unknown, ask before drafting. Do not promise availability you cannot confirm.

## Process

1. Confirm calendar status. If already held or booked, the response says so before naming rates, and offers alternates.
2. Match the facility and headcount to the rate schedule. Use resident and non-resident rates as separate lines.
3. Pull required forms: application, certificate of insurance, liability waiver. Events over a stated headcount (commonly 100) typically also require an event plan and security plan.
4. Determine the alcohol-service rule. If alcohol is requested, cite the city's policy and any TABC permit requirement.
5. State the setup/takedown window. Most rentals carry one hour pre-event and one hour post-event in the rate.
6. State the cancellation policy and deposit refund schedule plainly.
7. Draft the ADA paragraph naming the accessible route and the contact for accommodation requests.
8. Draft the next-steps block: where to submit, certificate-of-insurance limits and additional-insured language, and the deadline to hold the date.
9. Close with the coordinator's contact and a hold expiration note. A hold without signed paperwork is not a reservation.
10. Do not commit the city. The response is an offer subject to signed application and approval, not the agreement itself.

## Output Format

Plain email or letter, scannable, no emoji. The default is email.

```
Subject: [Facility name] rental inquiry — [Date]

Dear [Applicant name],

Thank you for your interest in renting [facility] on [date].

AVAILABILITY
[Open / On hold for 7 days / Already booked — alternates: ...]

RATES
[Facility name], [Date], [Time window]
Resident rate:        $[amount]
Non-resident rate:    $[amount]
Refundable deposit:   $[amount]
[Add-on: pavilion AV, kitchen, scoreboard, etc., as applicable]

REQUIRED PAPERWORK
1. Rental Application — submit at [URL or address]
2. Certificate of Insurance — general liability, minimum $[amount] per
   occurrence, naming the City of [City] as additional insured
3. Liability Waiver (signed by applicant)
[For events over 100 or events with alcohol:]
4. Event Plan — site layout, timeline, vendors
5. Security Plan — for attendance over [threshold]

ALCOHOL SERVICE
[If alcohol requested: city policy, TABC permit requirement, server
requirements, hours of service, open-container boundaries. Omit if not
requested.]

SETUP AND TAKEDOWN
Rental includes a [X]-hour setup window beginning at [time] and a
[Y]-hour takedown window ending at [time]. Damage or extended cleaning
is billed against the refundable deposit.

CANCELLATION
Full refund if cancellation is received in writing at least [N] days
before the event. [Tiered refund schedule]. No refunds within [N] days.

ACCESSIBILITY
The City of [City] complies with Title II of the Americans with
Disabilities Act, 42 U.S.C. § 12131 et seq. [Facility] is wheelchair
accessible. To request a reasonable modification, contact [Name] at
least 7 days before the event.

NEXT STEPS
To hold the date, submit signed application, certificate of insurance,
and refundable deposit by [date]. Hold expires then. Full fee due [N]
days before the event. Reply to this email or call me at [phone].

[Name]
[Title], Parks and Recreation
City of [City] | [city website]

This is an offer to enter a rental agreement subject to signed
application and approval. It is not a reservation until application and
deposit are received.
```

Length: 350 to 600 words. Tone: clear, professional, no hedging. The applicant should be able to forward this email and have every piece of information they need.

## TRAIGA & Compliance Notes

**AI use disclosure — transparency best practice (not a § 552.051 trigger):** Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.051(b)–(d) requires disclosure when a citizen *interacts directly with an AI system* (e.g., a chatbot, automated voice assistant, or AI-driven service interface). The disclosure trigger is *interaction*, not *output*. This skill produces a static document drafted with AI assistance; it does not put a citizen in direct interaction with AI, so § 552.051 is not engaged.

However, transparency about AI assistance in document drafting is sound practice and should align with the city's adopted AI use policy and the GovAI Coalition's guidance on documenting AI tool use. Consider a brief notation on documents distributed publicly or to the governing body: *"AI-assisted draft, reviewed by [staff title and name]."* This is a policy choice, not a statutory mandate.

**Texas Recreational Use Statute (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code Ch. 75):** The statute provides limited liability immunity for governmental units that allow recreational use of public property without charge. Charging a rental fee for exclusive use generally takes the rental outside the statute's protection, which is why insurance and additional-insured language matters. Do not assume Ch. 75 immunity extends to fee-based exclusive-use rentals.

**Alcohol service (Tex. Alco. Bev. Code):** Alcohol service at a rented city facility may require a Mixed Beverage Permit or Beer & Wine Only Permit issued by the Texas Alcoholic Beverage Commission, depending on whether alcohol is sold or served free. The applicant is responsible for the TABC permit. Cities commonly require licensed servers and prohibit self-service.

**Insurance, records, ADA:** A certificate of insurance naming the City as additional insured is standard; typical minimums are $1 million per occurrence, $2 million aggregate (consult the city attorney). Rental applications and event plans are public records under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552 once filed; security plans may be exempt under § 552.108. Cities are public entities under ADA Title II (42 U.S.C. § 12131 et seq.); facilities must be accessible.
