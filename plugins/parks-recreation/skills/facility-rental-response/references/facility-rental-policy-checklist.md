# Facility Rental Policy Checklist

A concise checklist of policy elements that a municipal facility rental agreement should address. Use this as a worksheet when configuring a new facility for rental, or when reviewing whether an existing rental contract is complete.

This is jurisdiction-agnostic. Texas-specific citations are noted where the underlying statute is state law.

## Insurance

- Minimum general liability coverage (typical: $1M per occurrence, $2M aggregate; higher for events with alcohol or attendance over 250)
- Certificate of insurance naming the City as additional insured
- Acceptable carriers (admitted in the state; A.M. Best rating minimum)
- Event-day umbrella requirements for high-risk events
- Workers' compensation requirements where vendors are involved
- Cancellation-of-insurance notice requirement to the city (typical: 30 days)

## Alcohol service

- Mixed Beverage Permit vs. Beer & Wine Only Permit requirements per Tex. Alco. Bev. Code; the applicant is responsible for obtaining the correct TABC permit
- Licensed-server requirement (TABC-certified bartenders)
- Self-service prohibition
- Hours of service window (typical: ends 30 minutes before event end)
- Open-container boundaries on city property (consistent with municipal code and Tex. Alco. Bev. Code Ch. 109)
- BYOB policy — most cities prohibit BYOB at city facilities
- Security requirements when alcohol is served (typically one off-duty peace officer per 100 guests)
- Designated-driver and rideshare information requirements

## Accessibility for the public attending

- ADA Title II compliance (42 U.S.C. § 12131 et seq.) — the city is a public entity and the rented facility must be accessible
- Accessible parking and route from parking to facility entrance
- Accessible restrooms
- Accessible seating area where applicable
- Hearing-loop or assistive-listening device availability for events with amplification
- Accommodation-request channel and deadline (typical: 7 days before event)
- The applicant's responsibility to communicate accessible-route information to their guests

## Hold-harmless and liability

- Hold-harmless clause indemnifying the city for applicant's negligence
- Texas Recreational Use Statute (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code Ch. 75) provides limited immunity for governmental units allowing recreational use of public property without charge; charging a rental fee for exclusive use generally takes the rental outside Ch. 75 protection
- Waiver of subrogation in favor of the city
- Limitation of damages clause referencing applicable governmental immunity (Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code Ch. 101 Texas Tort Claims Act)
- Property damage responsibility (deposit hold and billing for damage beyond deposit)

## Security for high-attendance events

- Attendance threshold for required security (typical: 100+)
- Off-duty officer staffing ratio (typical: one officer per 100 attendees; one per 50 for alcohol-served events)
- Coordination with the city police department for traffic and parking
- Crowd management plan for attendance over 300
- Severe-weather contingency plan for outdoor facilities
- Communication plan and on-site point of contact for emergency response

## Noise, lights, hours

- Municipal noise ordinance compliance with citation to local code
- Amplified-sound shutoff time (typical: 10:00 p.m. weeknights, 11:00 p.m. Friday/Saturday)
- Lighting plan for outdoor evening events
- Quiet-hours residential consideration where the facility abuts residential property
- Fireworks and pyrotechnics — typically require separate fire marshal review and a fire watch

## Public-park reservation procedures

- Reservation lead time minimum (typical: 14 days) and maximum (typical: 12 months in advance)
- Tiered pricing for residents vs. non-residents
- Non-profit and partner-agency discount eligibility and documentation requirements
- Permit-exempt activities (small family gatherings under a stated threshold)
- Closure and reschedule policy for weather, public-health, or city emergencies
- First-come, first-served priority for recurring city programs already on the calendar

## Cancellation and refunds

- Tiered refund schedule by days before event (typical: 100% refund 30+ days out; 50% at 14–29 days; 0% under 14 days)
- City-initiated cancellation full-refund policy (weather, public-health, public-safety)
- Deposit-vs-fee distinction in refund language
- Force majeure language

## Records, signage, and content

- Rental application is a public record under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552 once filed
- Event signage limits (size, content, no political endorsements on city property without separate approval)
- Photo and media restrictions in facilities where children's programs operate concurrently
- Vendor list disclosure requirement for events with food service

## Approvals and signatures

- Staff approval authority threshold (typical: rentals up to $X approved by Parks Director; above that, City Manager; above another threshold, council)
- Signed application required before any hold is converted to a confirmed reservation
- Council approval pathway for naming-rights or sponsorship-tied events
