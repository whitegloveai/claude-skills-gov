# Example — Carmen Velasquez Recognition (Cedar Ridge Community Center)

This is a worked example for the `volunteer-recognition-writer` skill. Single input scenario, three output formats produced together: letter, social post, and spoken remarks.

## Input scenario

David Ngata, the Cedar Ridge Parks Director, is recognizing Carmen Velasquez for completing 200 hours of volunteer service at the Cedar Ridge Community Center over the 2025 calendar year. Carmen has signed the city's media-consent form. She will be recognized at the Parks Board's regular January 2026 meeting.

Inputs supplied to the skill:

- Volunteer: Carmen Velasquez (she/her)
- Role: Volunteer at the Cedar Ridge Community Center supporting two programs — the Senior Nutrition Program (Tuesday and Thursday lunches) and the After-School Homework Club (Monday, Wednesday, Friday afternoons)
- Hours: 200 hours, calendar year 2025
- Specific contributions: Carmen plated meals for an average of 42 seniors per Tuesday and Thursday lunch. She trained two newer volunteers on the kitchen-safety protocol. At the homework club, she worked one-on-one with seven students struggling with math, and helped two students prepare for the state assessment in spring 2025
- Specific impact: Both students Carmen tutored passed the state assessment. The Senior Nutrition Program served 4,200 meals in 2025 — Carmen plated roughly a third of them. The two volunteers she trained are now leading their own kitchen shifts
- Signing authority for letter: David Ngata, Parks Director
- Spoken remarks setting: Parks Board regular meeting, January 14, 2026
- Photo consent: yes
- Format: all three

## Drafted recognition package

### Letter

```
[Cedar Ridge city letterhead]

January 14, 2026

Carmen Velasquez
[Address]

Dear Carmen,

On behalf of the City of Cedar Ridge, thank you for the 200 hours you
gave to the Cedar Ridge Community Center in 2025 — plating meals for our
seniors on Tuesdays and Thursdays, and sitting with our after-school
homework students on Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays.

In a year when the Senior Nutrition Program served 4,200 meals, you
plated about a third of them. The two volunteers you trained on the
kitchen-safety protocol are now leading their own shifts. And both of
the students you tutored in math passed the state assessment last
spring. None of that happens without you.

I hope you'll accept this letter as a small acknowledgment of work that
matters every week, in a building that runs because of people like you.

Sincerely,

David Ngata
Parks Director
City of Cedar Ridge
```

### Social post

```
Carmen Velasquez gave 200 hours to the Cedar Ridge Community Center in
2025 — plating roughly a third of the 4,200 meals served by our Senior
Nutrition Program, training two new kitchen volunteers, and tutoring
seven after-school homework students in math. Two of those students
passed the state assessment last spring.

Carmen, thank you. The building runs because of you.

Interested in volunteering at the community center? Visit
cedarridgetx.gov/parks/volunteer.

#CedarRidge #ParksAndRec

[Photo: Carmen at the community center kitchen, with her consent.]
```

### Spoken remarks (Parks Board meeting, January 14, 2026)

```
Madam Chair, members of the Parks Board, Mayor Castillo, City Manager
Ahn — thank you.

I'm here tonight to recognize Carmen Velasquez for 200 hours of
volunteer service at the Cedar Ridge Community Center in 2025.

I want to tell you about a Tuesday last March. The Senior Nutrition
Program was short-staffed that morning. We had 44 seniors signed up for
lunch, two regular volunteers out sick, and one new volunteer who had
shown up the week before for her first shift. Carmen had already plated
about 60 lunches that year. She told the new volunteer, "Stand next to
me. We're going to figure this out together." They served all 44 meals.
That new volunteer is now running her own shift on Thursdays.

That's who Carmen is. She does the work, and she teaches it.

In 2025, the Senior Nutrition Program served 4,200 meals. Carmen plated
roughly a third of them. She trained two newer volunteers on the
kitchen-safety protocol, and both are now leading shifts. At the
After-School Homework Club, she worked one-on-one with seven students
on math. Two of those students — including one we were genuinely
worried about — passed the state assessment last spring.

Carmen, the City of Cedar Ridge recognizes you tonight for 200 hours of
volunteer service in 2025. The plaque carries the date and the program
names, but it doesn't carry what really matters: the seniors who count
on you, the volunteers you brought up, and the kids who passed because
you sat with them.

Carmen, would you join me up here?

[Hand off to certificate presentation. Applause cue.]
```

AI-assisted drafts, reviewed by David Ngata, Parks Director.

## Why this package works

- The story in the spoken remarks — the Tuesday in March — anchors the recognition in a specific moment. Without that moment, the remarks read like a press release.
- The same three impact numbers (200 hours, 4,200 meals, 2 students passed) appear in all three formats so the recognition is consistent across channels.
- The letter is one page, the social post is under 120 words, and the spoken remarks run a little over 400 words — about 2 minutes 30 seconds at a calm pace.
- The remarks acknowledge Mayor Castillo and City Manager Ahn by name only because they are likely to be at the meeting; if they were not present, those names would be omitted.
- The signing authority on the letter is the Parks Director, not the Mayor, because the volunteer reports functionally to the parks department and a director's signature is more personal than a mayor's.
