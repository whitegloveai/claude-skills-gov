# Example — Cedar Ridge Youth Summer Baseball League

This is a worked example for the `program-flyer-drafter` skill. Input scenario followed by the drafted flyer copy.

## Input scenario

David Ngata, the Cedar Ridge Parks Director, is launching the 2026 summer baseball league at Live Oak Park. He needs flyer copy this week so the graphic designer can lay it out before registration opens. He gave the skill:

- Program: Cedar Ridge Youth Summer Baseball League
- Audience: Boys and girls ages 6–12, split into 6–8 (Rookie), 9–10 (Minors), and 11–12 (Majors) divisions
- Dates: Practices begin Monday, June 1, 2026. Games run Tuesdays and Thursdays, 6:00–8:00 p.m., through Thursday, July 30, 2026
- Location: Live Oak Park, 4400 Live Oak Crossing Drive, Cedar Ridge, TX
- Fee: $45 resident / $65 non-resident. Scholarships available for households at or below 200% of federal poverty level. Sibling discount: $10 off each additional child
- Registration deadline: Friday, May 22, 2026
- Registration mechanism: Online at cedarridgetx.gov/parks/baseball, or in person at the Cedar Ridge Community Center, 200 Civic Plaza
- Capacity: 240 players total across all divisions; waitlist after capacity
- Equipment: Players provide their own glove and cleats. League provides jerseys, hats, bats, and balls
- Coordinator: Marcus Reyna, Athletics Coordinator. 512-555-0142, mreyna@cedarridgetx.gov

## Drafted flyer copy

```
2026 CEDAR RIDGE YOUTH SUMMER BASEBALL

Eight weeks of practices and games at Live Oak Park for kids ages 6 to 12.
Three divisions so every player competes with kids their own age.

WHO: Boys and girls ages 6–12 (Rookie 6–8, Minors 9–10, Majors 11–12)
WHEN: June 1 – July 30, 2026. Games Tuesdays and Thursdays, 6–8 p.m.
WHERE: Live Oak Park, 4400 Live Oak Crossing Drive, Cedar Ridge, TX
COST: $45 resident / $65 non-resident. Scholarships available for
      households at or below 200% of the federal poverty level. $10
      off each additional sibling.

The league runs eight weeks beginning Monday, June 1. Players are placed
into divisions by age. Practices are scheduled by each team's coach.
Games run Tuesday and Thursday evenings at Live Oak Park. The league
provides jerseys, hats, bats, and balls. Players bring their own glove
and cleats. Coaches are background-checked volunteers trained in the
Cedar Ridge youth-sports coaching standard. End-of-season tournament
the week of July 27.

ACCESSIBILITY
The City of Cedar Ridge does not discriminate on the basis of disability.
The City will provide reasonable modifications to programs and services
for participants with disabilities consistent with Title II of the
Americans with Disabilities Act, 42 U.S.C. § 12131 et seq. To request a
modification, an auxiliary aid, sign language interpreter, or large-print
materials, contact Marcus Reyna at 512-555-0142 or
mreyna@cedarridgetx.gov at least 72 hours before the program start.
Live Oak Park is wheelchair accessible. Adaptive equipment available on
request. The City welcomes players of all abilities.

REGISTRATION
Register online at cedarridgetx.gov/parks/baseball or in person at the
Cedar Ridge Community Center, 200 Civic Plaza, by Friday, May 22, 2026.
Payment by credit card, check, or money order. Full refund through May
22; 50% refund through June 1; no refunds after the season begins.

Capacity is 240 players. Waitlist available after capacity is reached.

QUESTIONS
Marcus Reyna, Athletics Coordinator
512-555-0142 | mreyna@cedarridgetx.gov
City of Cedar Ridge Parks and Recreation | cedarridgetx.gov/parks

AI-assisted draft, reviewed by David Ngata, Parks Director.
```

## Why this draft works

- The headline is plain and date-stamped so it doesn't get mistaken for last year's flyer.
- The hook covers the activity, age range, and number of weeks in two short sentences.
- The Key Details Block is scannable. Most parents will read only this block.
- The description paragraph names what the league provides versus what the family brings, which prevents the most common pre-season call to the coordinator.
- The Accessibility Statement names a specific person and a specific channel, and references the correct ADA Title II citation for a public entity.
- The Spanish summary is short enough to fit on the back of the flyer without requiring a full translated layout.
