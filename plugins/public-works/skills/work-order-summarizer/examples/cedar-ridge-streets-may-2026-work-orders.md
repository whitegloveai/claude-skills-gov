## 3. Notable Items

- Three school-zone pothole reports near Pecan Elementary closed within 48 hours of receipt. Two were on the 700 block of Pecan Street, which is the same block surfacing repeatedly in the recurring hot spot below.
- Council District 2 generated 41% of received work orders against 28% of city population, consistent with the prior three months. District 2 also contains the longest stretch of asphalt rated Poor or Failed in the 2025 pavement condition report.
- The crew completed advance milling and marking on 4.2 lane-miles of the FY2026 overlay program corridors to support the contractor's mid-June mobilization.
- One sidewalk hazard report at 312 Magnolia (lifted slab, school-walk route) was abated with temporary asphalt fill on day one and is now in the FY2027 sidewalk replacement candidate list.

## 4. Cycle Time and Backlog

Pothole and dead animal cycle times stayed at one to two days, which is on target. Drainage and culvert backlog is the principal concern. Of nine over-SLA tickets, seven are drainage-related and concentrated in the Old Town quadrant where the storm system predates current standards. Two require crew rebar saw work that has been deferred behind overlay prep.

## 5. Recurring Issues / Hot Spots

- **700 block of Pecan Street** — 4 pothole reports in the trailing 90 days. Base failure suspected. Recommend an investigative core. This block also has an active service request the call center flagged as a duplicate-cluster (see service request 2026-05-0837).
- **Magnolia at 4th Street drainage inlet** — 3 reports in 60 days for standing water after rain. Inlet appears partially blocked; needs vactor truck visit and downstream pipe inspection.
- **Live Oak Drive cul-de-sac** — 3 reports for sign knockdowns at the same yield sign. Recommend reviewing sight lines and considering a relocation.

## 6. Staffing and Equipment Notes

- Two FMLA absences in the patch crew during the second half of the month reduced effective patch crew strength by approximately 25% for ten working days.
- Vactor truck out of service May 18–22 for hydraulic repair. This contributed directly to drainage backlog.
- One temporary worker hired through staffing agency for overlay prep starting May 22.

## 8. Recommendations

1. **Schedule the Pecan Street 700-block investigative core in early June, before the overlay contractor mobilizes.** If base failure is confirmed, that segment should be added to the overlay scope as a change order rather than re-patched.
2. **Move two drainage tickets in Old Town into a single batched vactor day in the second week of June** now that the truck is back in service.
3. **Hold a pre-mobilization coordination meeting with the overlay contractor** the week of June 8 to align traffic control with school transition and the Pecan Street finding.
4. **Add a recurring data point to next month's report:** count of work orders by council district per 1,000 residents, so District 2 concentration is visible at a glance.
