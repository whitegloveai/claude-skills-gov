# Example — Cedar Ridge 2025 Pavement Condition Report

> Cedar Ridge maintains 87 lane-miles of road. Public Works Director Renata Hsu and the consulting engineer's office (Hartwell Engineering, Cedar Ridge office, James Hartwell, P.E. License No. 89421) completed a citywide PCI survey between January and March 2026 using ASTM D6433. This report wraps the engineer's data into the operational and policy narrative for the May 26, 2026 council CIP work session and supports the FY2026 overlay program currently in the proposed budget at $1.85 million.

---

```
CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE
PAVEMENT CONDITION REPORT — CALENDAR YEAR 2025
April 14, 2026

Prepared by: Renata Hsu, Public Works Director
Engineer of record: James Hartwell, P.E. (License No. 89421),
                    Hartwell Engineering
Reviewed by: Renata Hsu, Public Works Director

==============================================================
1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
==============================================================

Cedar Ridge maintains 87.4 lane-miles of paved roadway. A citywide
PCI survey was conducted in January through March 2026 using ASTM
D6433 methodology by Hartwell Engineering under contract.

Distribution by tier:
- Good      (PCI 71-100)   52% — 45.4 lane-miles
- Fair      (PCI 56-70)    23% — 20.1 lane-miles
- Poor      (PCI 41-55)    18% — 15.7 lane-miles
- Failed    (PCI 0-40)      7% — 6.2 lane-miles

Top finding: PCI distribution declined from 58% Good in 2020 to
52% Good in 2025, with the largest decline in the residential
collector tier. Twenty-two lane-miles are at or below PCI 55
(Poor or Failed) and require either rehabilitation or
reconstruction in the next five years to avoid further cost
escalation.

Top recommendation: Continue the FY2026 overlay program at $1.85
million as the right operational response to address the bottom
quartile of the network on a 10-year resurfacing cycle.
Implementing this cycle is projected to stabilize the Good tier
above 50% and reduce the Failed tier below 5% by 2030.

==============================================================
2. ASSET CLASS AND INVENTORY
==============================================================

Cedar Ridge maintains 87.4 lane-miles of city-owned and
city-maintained paved roadway, consisting of:

- Arterials (asphalt over flexible base):      12.3 lane-miles
- Collectors (asphalt over flexible base):     21.0 lane-miles
- Residential collectors (asphalt):            29.6 lane-miles
- Local residential streets (asphalt):         22.1 lane-miles
- Concrete (downtown grid):                     2.4 lane-miles

State highways (US 90, FM 1244) within the city limits are
maintained by TxDOT and are excluded from this report.

Age cohort breakdown:

- Less than 5 years since overlay or reconstruction:  18%
- 5 to 10 years:                                      31%
- 10 to 15 years:                                     27%
- 15 to 20 years:                                     14%
- More than 20 years since major rehabilitation:      10%

==============================================================
3. METHODOLOGY
==============================================================

Survey conducted January 8 through March 21, 2026 by Hartwell
Engineering using ASTM D6433-20 (Standard Practice for Roads
and Parking Lots Pavement Condition Index Surveys).

Methodology elements:
- Combined windshield and on-foot survey.
- Every 100-foot segment scored.
- 19 distress types recorded by severity.
- PCI calculated per ASTM D6433 deduct value model.
- Data captured in GIS, joined to the city's road centerline.
- Quality control: 10% of segments re-scored by a second
  inspector; reproducibility was within plus or minus 4 PCI
  points.

Limitations: Subsurface base condition was not assessed. Coring
and falling weight deflectometer (FWD) testing are recommended
for any segment programmed for reconstruction.

==============================================================
4. FINDINGS BY SUB-AREA
==============================================================

The city is divided into four service zones for public works
operations.

Zone 1 (Downtown and historic core)
  Good 38%  Fair 25%  Poor 22%  Failed 15%
  Note: Concrete grid in poor condition. Largest concentration
  of Failed segments. Many segments date to 1960s reconstruction.

Zone 2 (North residential, post-1990 development)
  Good 71%  Fair 19%  Poor 8%   Failed 2%
  Note: Youngest network. Crack-seal cycle is appropriate.

Zone 3 (South residential and industrial)
  Good 49%  Fair 24%  Poor 20%  Failed 7%
  Note: Mixed-age network. Industrial truck routes (Industrial
  Drive, Commerce Lane) show accelerated wear; reconstruction
  candidates within 3 years.

Zone 4 (West residential and Pecan Pressure Zone area)
  Good 56%  Fair 23%  Poor 17%  Failed 4%
  Note: Median network. Pavement on Pecan Avenue at the recent
  main break site (May 2026) shows utility-cut patching that
  warrants joint-utility coordination.

==============================================================
5. TREND ANALYSIS
==============================================================

Comparison to prior surveys (FY2015 and FY2020):

Year   Good   Fair   Poor   Failed
2015   62%    22%    12%    4%
2020   58%    23%    14%    5%
2025   52%    23%    18%    7%

Drivers identified by the engineer of record:

1. Residential collector tier: most of the network is now in
   the 10-to-15-year age cohort, the typical onset of structural
   rather than surface distress. Deferral compounds cost.
2. Utility cut patching: the engineer observed inconsistent
   patch quality at locations of recent utility work; this
   accelerates deterioration locally and is addressable through
   tightened utility-cut standards.
3. Funding: the FY2021 through FY2024 overlay budgets averaged
   $1.2 million, below the $1.6 million needed to maintain a
   10-year resurfacing cycle on the residential collector and
   local tier. The proposed FY2026 overlay budget of $1.85
   million restores the funding pace.

==============================================================
6. IMPLICATIONS FOR CIP
==============================================================

Capital programs supported by these findings:

- FY2026 Overlay Program ($1.85M, currently in proposed
  budget). Recommended target lane-miles: 8 to 9, concentrated
  in the Poor tier residential collectors in Zones 1 and 3.
- FY2026-2028 Industrial Truck Route Rehabilitation. Industrial
  Drive and Commerce Lane (1.7 lane-miles) are reconstruction
  candidates. Engineering should begin in FY2026; construction
  in FY2027 or FY2028. Preliminary cost order of magnitude:
  $4.5 to $5.5 million pending coring and FWD analysis.
- Downtown Concrete Grid Rehabilitation Study. The 2.4
  lane-miles of concrete in Zone 1 (Failed share 15%) warrants
  a stand-alone study with funding decision in FY2027 budget.
  Recommended budget: $80,000 for the study.

The decision on capital programming is the council's. This
report identifies the conditions and the operational response
options the engineer recommends.

==============================================================
7. IMPLICATIONS FOR O&M
==============================================================

Adjustments recommended:

- Crack-seal cycle: shift from a 5-year to a 4-year cycle on
  Good-tier segments in Zone 2 to extend life into the next
  decade.
- Utility-cut standards: tighten the patch specification and
  inspection requirement; coordinate with city water, sewer,
  and franchise utilities. Estimated implementation: revised
  standard detail by end of FY2026.
- Joint-utility coordination: when a water or sewer main
  reaches end of life on a segment programmed for overlay,
  prioritize utility replacement first (a "dig once" policy
  for the Pecan, Magnolia, and Live Oak corridors).
- Inventory and GIS: continue the post-survey GIS update so
  the next survey (FY2030) can leverage the same baseline.

==============================================================
8. RECOMMENDED NEXT INSPECTION
==============================================================

Citywide PCI survey in FY2030, using ASTM D6433. Interim
spot-survey of the Failed tier annually to identify any
accelerated deterioration. FWD testing on reconstruction
candidates in advance of FY2027 budget.

==============================================================
9. CAVEATS AND LIMITATIONS
==============================================================

- This report addresses surface pavement condition only. Base
  and subgrade condition were not assessed.
- Hydraulic capacity, stormwater drainage adequacy, and ADA
  ramp compliance are not within the scope.
- Cost order-of-magnitude figures in Section 6 are preliminary
  planning estimates. Final engineer's opinion of probable
  construction cost requires design-level analysis.
- TxDOT-maintained state highways are excluded.
```
