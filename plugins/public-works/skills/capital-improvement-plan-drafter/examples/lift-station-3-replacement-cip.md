CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE CAPITAL IMPROVEMENT PLAN
WW-2027-01 — LIFT STATION 3 (INDUSTRIAL PARK) REPLACEMENT

Asset class: Wastewater collection
Council district / service area: District 4 (Industrial Park basin; downstream sewershed serves Districts 2 and 4)
Project manager: Adelina Cortez, Wastewater Superintendent
Design engineer of record: To be selected via Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 2254 (Professional Services) procurement

*AI-assisted draft, reviewed by Renata Hsu, Public Works Director.*

## 1. Scope

Full replacement of Wastewater Lift Station 3 at its existing site on Industrial Boulevard. The replacement includes a new wet well sized to current peak wet-weather flow with a 20-year planning horizon, two new submersible pumps with VFDs, a new control panel with SCADA integration matching the city's standard platform, new force main connection to the existing 8-inch discharge main, a backup power connection compatible with the city's portable generator fleet, and site improvements including new fencing, lighting, and a paved drive aisle.

The existing station was commissioned in 1998 with a 25-year design life. Sizing and pump selection in the replacement will reflect updated peak flow calculations to be developed in the preliminary engineering report (PER). The PER and design will be sealed by a licensed Texas P.E.

## 2. Problem Statement

The FY2025 wastewater lift station maintenance analysis documents the following at LS-3:

- 12 pump clog/bind events (38% of FY2025 corrective work across all six stations)
- 6 control panel faults; OEM end-of-support for the 1998 relay rack since 2021
- Mean time between failures of 22 days in FY2025, down from 31 in FY2024
- 5-year cumulative maintenance cost equal to 31% of replacement cost
- Two sanitary sewer overflow near-misses in FY2025 tied to control panel faults during wet weather

The Industrial Park basin has no upstream gravity capacity to absorb a multi-hour LS-3 outage. A wet-weather failure with both pumps offline is a credible scenario based on FY2025 history and is the principal availability and TPDES compliance risk in the collection system.

## 3. Goals and Outcomes

1. Eliminate LS-3 from the top of the corrective-work distribution; target less than 8% of citywide lift station corrective work post-completion.
2. Achieve a 12-month run with zero TPDES noncompliance events attributable to LS-3 within 18 months of commissioning.
3. Reduce LS-3 annual O&M cost from the FY2025 baseline of $118,400 to under $20,000 within two years post-commissioning.
4. Integrate the new control panel with the SCADA standard used at LS-4 and LS-5.
5. Build to the current 30 T.A.C. Ch. 217 design criteria and the FY2026 wet-weather peak flow projection.

## 8. Environmental and Regulatory Considerations

- 30 T.A.C. Ch. 217 design criteria applicable to wastewater collection system facilities.
- 30 T.A.C. Ch. 305 / Cedar Ridge TPDES permit conditions — coordinate with TCEQ Region 11 on temporary bypass plan during construction.
- Existing site disturbance only; no new ROW or easement acquisition expected.
- No FEMA Special Flood Hazard Area; LS-3 is on a fill pad above the 100-year floodplain. PER will verify.
- Construction stormwater coverage under TCEQ TPDES general permit TXR150000.
- Site is not within a recognized archaeological zone; no THC consultation expected, but the PER will confirm.

## 9. Operational Impact During Construction

Sewer flows from the Industrial Park basin will be maintained via a temporary pumping arrangement during the cutover window. Traffic impact on Industrial Boulevard is limited to a short partial closure for force main tie-in. Adjacent industrial customers will be notified at least 30 days in advance of the cutover.

## 10. Operating Cost Impact (Post-Completion)

Annualized O&M expected to decrease by approximately $98,000 versus FY2025 baseline (corrective spending drops, PM cost rises slightly). Energy use expected to decrease 15% from VFD-controlled pumping versus current across-the-line starting. No new FTE required.
