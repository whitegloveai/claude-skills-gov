# CIP Funding Source Summary

Concise reference for the funding sources most commonly used in Texas municipal CIP entries. Cite the specific statute in the entry's Funding Strategy section.

## Local Tax-Supported Debt

**General obligation bonds (GO) — Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 1331.** Require voter approval. Backed by the city's ad valorem tax. Used for general government projects (streets, drainage, parks, public safety facilities). Long maturity (typically 20–30 years).

**Certificates of obligation (CO) — Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 271.045 et seq.** Council-authorized, no election unless a 5% petition is filed under § 271.049. Backed by ad valorem tax and (optionally) revenues. Common for routine, non-controversial capital. Subject to public notice and petition window.

**Tax notes — Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 1431.** Short-term (up to 7 years). Useful for equipment and small projects where CO debt service would be excessive.

## Utility Revenue Debt

**Revenue bonds — city charter authority and Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 1502.** Backed by net utility revenues, not the tax rate. Standard vehicle for water, wastewater, and stormwater capital. Requires a parity coverage test in the bond ordinance.

**TWDB Clean Water State Revolving Fund (CWSRF) — Tex. Water Code Ch. 15, 30 T.A.C. Ch. 375.** Below-market loans for wastewater, reuse, and stormwater. Application through the TWDB Intended Use Plan cycle. Often paired with principal forgiveness for disadvantaged communities.

**TWDB Drinking Water State Revolving Fund (DWSRF) — Tex. Water Code Ch. 15, 30 T.A.C. Ch. 371.** Below-market loans for drinking water. Same cycle.

## Impact Fees and Special Districts

**Impact fees — Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 395.** Water, wastewater, roadway, and drainage. Require Land Use Assumptions, Capital Improvements Plan, and a defensible cost-of-service study. Restricted to growth-attributable capacity.

**Tax Increment Reinvestment Zones (TIRZ) — Tex. Tax Code Ch. 311.** Capture incremental property tax for project costs within the zone.

**Public Improvement Districts (PID) — Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 372.** Assessments on benefited properties for defined improvements.

## Sales Tax

**Type A / Type B economic development sales tax — Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 504 and Ch. 505.** Project eligibility is statute-defined. Type B allows broader quality-of-life capital (parks, sports venues, infrastructure for new businesses).

## State and Federal Grants

**TxDOT Transportation Alternatives (TASA) and BIL formula programs.** Sidewalks, trails, safety. Local match typically 20%.

**TxDOT Highway Safety Improvement Program (HSIP).** Safety countermeasures with crash data justification.

**CDBG infrastructure set-asides (TxCDBG for non-entitlement; entitlement directly from HUD).** Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 2306 administration. Income qualification required.

**FEMA Hazard Mitigation Assistance — BRIC, FMA.** Pre-disaster mitigation. State administered through TDEM.

**EPA Clean Water Act § 319 nonpoint source.** Pass-through TCEQ.

**Federal infrastructure programs under the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act.** Specific program citations vary; check the active Notice of Funding Opportunity.

## Operating Sources

**Pay-as-you-go from operating funds.** Smaller projects, equipment, recurring capital. No debt issuance cost.

**Utility rate reserves.** When a utility has been intentionally building capital reserves through its rate model.

## How to Cite in a CIP Entry

In the Funding Strategy section, name the source, the share, and the statute. Example: "Project funded 60% by TWDB Clean Water State Revolving Fund loan (Tex. Water Code Ch. 15) and 40% by wastewater utility rate revenue."
