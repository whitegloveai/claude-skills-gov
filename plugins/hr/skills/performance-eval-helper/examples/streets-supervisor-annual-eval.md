# CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE — PERFORMANCE EVALUATION

**Employee:** Marcus Tilden
**Position:** Streets Supervisor, Public Works Department
**Supervisor:** Renata Hsu, P.E., Public Works Director
**Review Period:** May 1, 2025 – April 30, 2026
**Review Type:** Annual
**Date Prepared:** May 10, 2026

## GOAL ACHIEVEMENT

**Goal 1:** Ramp the FY2026 street overlay program from 6 lane-miles to 10 lane-miles.
**Status:** Achieved.
**Evidence:** The Streets Division completed 11.2 lane-miles of overlay during the program window, 12 percent above target. The completion rate was supported by Mr. Tilden's coordination with the asphalt vendor on delivery windows, weather-aware scheduling, and a redesigned daily start-of-shift huddle that reduced lost time at the beginning of each crew day.

**Goal 2:** Reduce the open work-order backlog from 142 to fewer than 75.
**Status:** Achieved.
**Evidence:** The backlog closed the period at 63 open work orders, a 56 percent reduction. The reduction is attributable to a rebalanced crew assignment, the addition of a backlog-only Friday rotation through the third quarter, and clearer prioritization triage at the supervisor level.

**Goal 3:** Lead the crew through CDL recertification with zero gaps.
**Status:** Achieved.
**Evidence:** All eight FTEs were recertified on schedule, with no operational gap. Mr. Tilden coordinated the training calendar with the City's third-party CDL trainer and worked with HR on backfill scheduling.

**Goal 4:** Improve weekly work-order documentation completeness from 72 percent to 95 percent.
**Status:** Partial.
**Evidence:** Documentation completeness improved to 84 percent — a 12-point gain over the prior period, but 11 points short of target. The improvement is real and meaningful; the gap reflects a pattern of completing field work before closing out the corresponding work-order narrative in the system, particularly on storm-response days.

## COMPETENCY RATINGS

**Job Knowledge — Exceeds Expectations.** Mr. Tilden has deep operational knowledge of asphalt operations, drainage maintenance, ROW issues, and the City's street network. Engineering Division leads regularly consult him on field conditions during design review for paving projects, which is unusual for a supervisor-level role.

**Work Quality — Exceeds Expectations.** Crew output during the overlay program window passed independent inspection on the first walk in 9 of 11 segments. The two segments requiring rework were both addressed within the same week.

**Productivity — Exceeds Expectations.** Lane-mile completion at 11.2 of a 10 goal and a 56 percent reduction in backlog with the same FTE count indicates strong throughput. Mr. Tilden manages crew time and equipment rotation effectively.

**Dependability — Exceeds Expectations.** Mr. Tilden led the November 14–15 ice event de-icing operation through a 36-hour activation, including overnight coverage and crew rotation. Zero injuries, no equipment damage, and continuous coverage of designated routes.

**Communication — Meets Expectations.** Mr. Tilden communicates clearly and respectfully with crew members and with the Engineering Division. Communication with the public, when it occurs, is professional. Upward communication on operational issues is generally timely; HR has noted that documentation-related communication lags during high-tempo weeks.

**Judgment and Decision-Making — Exceeds Expectations.** Field judgment during the ice event and during the August storm response was sound and decisive. Resource trade-offs during the overlay ramp were appropriate to scope and budget.

**Teamwork — Exceeds Expectations.** Crew morale has visibly improved during the period. Coordination with Water Utilities on shared right-of-way work was effective and reduced rework. Coordination with Fleet on equipment readiness was strong.

**Customer Service — Meets Expectations.** Mr. Tilden handles resident inquiries directly when they reach him and follows up. The Streets Division did not receive any escalated complaints during the period.

**Safety — Exceeds Expectations.** Zero lost-time injuries on a crew of eight performing high-exposure work, with no near-miss incidents reported above threshold. Pre-task safety briefings are consistent and documented.

**Supervisory Effectiveness — Meets Expectations.** Crew assignment, scheduling, and time-off coordination are handled effectively. Coaching of newer crew members on equipment operation is strong. The administrative dimension of supervision — work-order documentation, payroll review timeliness — is improving but is the principal area for development.

## STRENGTHS

- Operational leadership in high-tempo and emergency response settings, demonstrated during the November ice event and the August storm response.
- Throughput and execution on the overlay program ramp, exceeding lane-mile target by 12 percent while reducing the work-order backlog by 56 percent.
- Crew development and morale, evidenced by training completion, low turnover, and observed engagement during morning huddles.

## AREAS FOR DEVELOPMENT

**Documentation completeness during and after high-tempo periods.** Field execution is excellent; the closeout of work-order narratives, especially on storm-response days, lags. The 72 percent to 84 percent improvement in the period is meaningful but does not reach the 95 percent target.

**Plan:** Beginning in May 2026, Mr. Tilden will use the field tablet's voice-to-text feature for end-of-shift work-order closeout, with HR-supported coaching from the Public Works Administrative Coordinator twice a month for the first quarter. The Public Works Director will review the documentation completeness metric monthly through Q1 of FY2027. Target: 95 percent monthly documentation completeness by end of Q2 FY2027.

## GOALS FOR NEXT PERIOD (FY2027)

1. Deliver the FY2027 street overlay program (12 lane-miles per the adopted CIP) on schedule and within the asphalt unit-cost budget.
2. Achieve and sustain 95 percent weekly work-order documentation completeness, measured monthly.
3. Lead a cross-training rotation that qualifies at least two crew members on the larger broom and the patch truck, reducing single-operator exposure.
4. Complete an APWA Public Works Supervisor certificate course or equivalent during the period.
5. Maintain zero lost-time injuries.

## OVERALL RATING: Exceeds Expectations

**Rationale:** Mr. Tilden delivered above-target outcomes on three of four annual goals and made meaningful progress on the fourth. His leadership during the November ice event and the throughput on the overlay program are concrete contributions to the City's service delivery. The documentation gap is real but is the only material development area in the period, and a workable plan is in place. The overall rating reflects strong execution and the leadership the City needs in this role.
