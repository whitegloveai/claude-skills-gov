# ADA Accommodation Drafter

> Drafts the documents in the ADA interactive process: initiation letters, accommodation analysis memos, and accommodation agreements. Built for HR generalists handling accommodation requests that will be reviewed by counsel and signed by the city. The skill **structures the analysis** for the HR signer to evaluate; the recommendation belongs to the qualified HR official, not to the skill.

## When to Use

- "Draft the initiation letter for the interactive process. Employee disclosed a back injury yesterday."
- "I have the medical limitations and the essential functions. Walk me through the accommodation analysis memo."
- "We've agreed on the accommodation. Draft the agreement for signature."
- "Compare a modified-duty rotation to a reassignment to plan review for this Building Inspector."
- "We're at undue hardship on a specific accommodation. Draft the analysis."
- An accommodation request has come in and the HR director needs a defensible written record of the interactive process.

## Inputs Needed

Required:

- Employee name and position
- Date of accommodation request and how it was made (verbal, written, supervisor reported)
- The limitation as disclosed by the employee or treating provider (not a diagnosis — limitations and restrictions)
- The position's essential functions
- The accommodation(s) under consideration
- Document type needed: initiation letter, analysis memo, or accommodation agreement

Optional:

- Medical documentation status and the scope of any pending medical inquiry
- Undue hardship factors specific to the position or department
- Prior accommodation history for this employee or this position
- Whether the employee has FMLA leave available or pending
- Whether the position is civil service under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 143 or Ch. 158
- Workers' compensation status if the limitation arose from a work injury

If the user has not provided the position's essential functions or the limitation as disclosed, ask before drafting. The accommodation analysis without essential functions is not an analysis.

## Process

1. Identify the document type needed: initiation letter, accommodation analysis memo, or accommodation agreement.
2. Confirm the legal framework: ADA/ADAAA (42 U.S.C. § 12101 et seq.), 29 C.F.R. Part 1630, EEOC enforcement guidance, and Tex. Lab. Code Ch. 21. The "qualified individual" definition at 42 U.S.C. § 12111(8) is the gate. See `references/ada-interactive-process-flow.md`.
3. Scope medical inquiry. Inquiries must be job-related and consistent with business necessity. Ask for limitations, not diagnoses. Use GINA (42 U.S.C. § 2000ff et seq.) safe-harbor language.
4. Map essential functions to the limitation. Identify which essential functions are affected and which accommodations could enable performance.
5. Structure the accommodation options matrix on effectiveness, cost, operational impact, and safety. The employee's preference is considered, but the employer chooses among effective accommodations.
6. Surface the undue hardship factors — significant difficulty or expense considering the city's resources, operation, and impact. Do not invoke lightly.
7. Structure the recommendation section for the HR signer to complete. The recommendation belongs to the signing official, not the skill. For agreements, structure a signable document; for initiation letters, structure a follow-up meeting proposal.
8. Flag items for the city attorney. Direct threat, leave-as-accommodation, reassignment, and denials need counsel review.

## Output Format

Plain markdown. Tone: professional, neutral, supportive. Avoid clinical or legalistic phrasing where plain words work. Length: 600 to 1,300 words depending on document type.

**Initiation Letter** — letter format with a clear subject line, the request for interactive process, an appropriately scoped medical-inquiry section, a meeting proposal, and contact information.

**Analysis Memo** — memo format with Issue, Background, Essential Functions, Limitation, Accommodation Options Matrix, Undue Hardship Analysis, Recommendation, and Next Steps.

**Accommodation Agreement** — agreement format with the accommodation described specifically, duration, review schedule, employee acknowledgment, and signatures (employee, supervisor, HR, and where appropriate the department head and city attorney's signature block for legal review of record).

## TRAIGA & Compliance Notes

**AI use disclosure — transparency best practice (not a § 552.051 trigger):** Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.051(b)–(d) requires disclosure when a citizen *interacts directly with an AI system* (e.g., a chatbot, automated voice assistant, or AI-driven service interface). The disclosure trigger is *interaction*, not *output*. This skill produces a static document drafted with AI assistance; it does not put a citizen in direct interaction with AI, so § 552.051 is not engaged.

However, transparency about AI assistance in document drafting is sound practice and should align with the city's adopted AI use policy and the GovAI Coalition's guidance on documenting AI tool use. Consider a brief notation on documents distributed publicly or to the governing body: *"AI-assisted draft, reviewed by [staff title and name]."* This is a policy choice, not a statutory mandate.

For accommodation correspondence specifically, AI-assisted drafts are starting points only. The HR professional and likely the city attorney sign off on anything that goes to the employee.

**EEOC AI guidance:** Where the city uses AI in screening or evaluation, the EEOC's 2023 technical assistance requires the accommodation process to address the AI tool itself. An employee with a disability may need an accommodation to the AI-mediated process.

**ADA/ADAAA framework:** 42 U.S.C. § 12101 et seq.; 29 C.F.R. Part 1630. Reasonable accommodation includes job restructuring, modified schedules, vacant-position reassignment, equipment, and qualified readers or interpreters. Undue hardship considers cost, overall resources, and operational impact.

**Medical inquiries:** Limited to what is necessary. Seek limitations and duration, not diagnoses. Use GINA safe-harbor language.

**Direct threat:** A significant risk of substantial harm that accommodation cannot reduce. Fact-intensive; needs medical input and counsel.

**Reassignment:** Last-resort accommodation to a vacant position for which the employee is qualified. The employer need not displace or create. Civil service (Ch. 143/158) interacts with classification.

**Leave as accommodation:** Reasonable when finite. Indefinite leave generally is not. Coordinate with FMLA (29 U.S.C. § 2601 et seq.).

**Workers' comp:** Work-injury limitations trigger Tex. Lab. Code Title 5 obligations in parallel with ADA; the two are separate.

**Confidentiality:** Maintain medical information separately from the personnel file (29 C.F.R. § 1630.14).

**Public records:** Accommodation files contain medical information exempt under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.117. Note: TPIA Ch. 552 (Gov't Code) is separate from TRAIGA Ch. 552 (Bus. & Comm. Code).

**Retention:** Per TSLAC Schedule GR.

**Counsel review:** Direct threat, extended leave, reassignment, and denials require city attorney review before issuance.
