# MEMORANDUM

**TO:** Daniel Ahn, City Manager
**FROM:** Dr. Karen Asher, HR Director
**DATE:** May 10, 2026
**RE:** ADA Reasonable Accommodation Analysis — Theo Marchetti, Building Inspector II
**STATUS:** Privileged and Confidential — Attorney-Client communication on legal questions; medical information segregated per 29 C.F.R. § 1630.14

## I. ISSUE

Whether the City can provide a reasonable accommodation under the ADA (42 U.S.C. § 12101 et seq.) that enables Mr. Marchetti to perform the essential functions of the Building Inspector II position, and if so, which accommodation among those under consideration is appropriate.

## II. BACKGROUND

Mr. Marchetti has served as a Building Inspector II in the Planning Department for four years and as a Building Inspector I for two years before that. His evaluations have consistently rated at "exceeds expectations." On April 28, 2026, Mr. Marchetti notified his supervisor and HR that he had sustained a non-occupational back injury and was beginning physical therapy. He provided functional capacity documentation from his treating provider on May 1, 2026. The interactive process was initiated by HR letter the same day. Mr. Marchetti met with HR on May 6, 2026 to discuss accommodation options.

The functional limitations relevant to the workplace, as disclosed by the treating provider, are: lifting limited to 25 pounds; no repeated ladder climbing; no prolonged trunk flexion or rotation; vehicle ingress/egress limited to 30 per shift. Expected duration is 90 to 120 days with re-evaluation. Permanency depends on physical therapy outcomes.

This is not a workers' compensation case. The analysis proceeds under the ADA and Tex. Lab. Code Ch. 21.

## III. ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS

The Building Inspector II job description identifies the following essential functions affected by the limitations:

1. Conduct on-site inspections of residential and commercial construction, including walking job sites and accessing all construction areas to verify code compliance — **affected** (mobility on uneven terrain, vehicle ingress/egress frequency).
2. Climb ladders to inspect framing, roof structures, and elevated mechanical equipment — **affected** (no repeated ladder climbing).
3. Access crawlspaces and confined spaces for foundation, plumbing, and electrical inspections — **affected** (trunk flexion).
4. Operate a city-assigned inspection vehicle — **partially affected** (ingress/egress frequency).
5. Document inspections, communicate findings, and post correction notices through the permitting system — **not affected**.
6. Communicate with contractors, homeowners, and architects — **not affected**.

Marginal functions (e.g., representing the department at industry meetings) are not affected and do not enter the analysis.

## IV. LIMITATION SUMMARY

Lifting up to 25 pounds is consistent with most inspection tasks, but ladder climbing, prolonged trunk flexion (crawlspaces), and high-frequency vehicle ingress/egress are not. The duration is 90 to 120 days with re-evaluation.

## V. ACCOMMODATION OPTIONS MATRIX

### Option 1 — Modified Field-Duty Rotation (Paired Inspections)

**Description:** Mr. Marchetti continues field inspections, but ladder and crawlspace components of inspections are performed by a paired second inspector or by the assigned trade-specialty inspector. Mr. Marchetti's daily inspection load is reduced from 10–12 to 6–8 to accommodate reduced vehicle ingress/egress.

**Effectiveness:** Enables Mr. Marchetti to perform the essential functions he can perform; the affected functions are covered by a second inspector. Documentation, communication, and walk-through remain primary.

**Operational Impact:** Building Division has four inspectors. Pairing for ladder/crawlspace work increases inspector-day cost on those inspections. Estimated 10–15 percent additional inspector hours per week. Permit-throughput impact is mitigable through scheduling but is not zero.

**Cost:** Inspector overtime if backfill is needed; no capital cost.

**Safety:** Reduces re-injury risk.

### Option 2 — Ergonomic Vehicle Modifications

**Description:** Lumbar support, swivel seat, grab handle, and step-bar modifications to the assigned inspection vehicle, plus a lighter inspection equipment kit (under 15 lb total) that reduces lifting frequency.

**Effectiveness:** Reduces vehicle ingress/egress strain. Does not address ladder climbing, crawlspace access, or the underlying 30-per-shift ingress/egress cap; useful only as a supplemental measure.

**Operational Impact:** Minimal.

**Cost:** Approximately $1,800 for vehicle modifications and equipment kit, sourced from the Planning Department operating budget.

**Safety:** Improves ergonomics; not a standalone solution.

### Option 3 — Temporary Reassignment to Plan Review

**Description:** Temporary reassignment to a Plan Review function within the Planning Department for the 90- to 120-day duration of the limitations. Plan review duties include reviewing submitted construction documents for code compliance, coordinating with the City Engineer, and corresponding with permit applicants — all sedentary office work.

**Effectiveness:** Eliminates all affected essential functions. Plan review is a known function in the department; the City is currently behind on plan review turnaround, so the reassignment fills an operational need.

**Operational Impact:** Positive in the near term — improves plan review throughput. On return, field inspector capacity is restored.

**Cost:** No new cost. Mr. Marchetti's salary continues at his current grade.

**Civil Service / Classification Considerations:** The Building Division is not subject to Ch. 143 civil service. The temporary reassignment is within the Planning Department and at the same pay grade; it does not require a classification action.

**Safety:** No exposure.

### Option 4 — Leave as Accommodation

Considered and not recommended. The duration of the limitations is finite, but the City has effective alternatives that allow Mr. Marchetti to continue working. Leave is not the least restrictive accommodation here.

## VI. UNDUE HARDSHIP ANALYSIS

Considering the size and resources of the City of Cedar Ridge (population ~32,000; Planning Department staffing of 11 FTE including 4 inspectors), the nature of the operation, and the impact on operations:

- **Option 1** is feasible with manageable scheduling impact. Not undue hardship.
- **Option 2** is low-cost and feasible. Not undue hardship.
- **Option 3** is feasible and actually improves a backlogged function. Not undue hardship.

No option under consideration rises to undue hardship.

## VII. RECOMMENDATION

A combined approach for the 90- to 120-day period:

1. **Primary:** Temporary reassignment to Plan Review in the Planning Department at the current pay grade. This eliminates the affected essential functions and addresses a department backlog.
2. **Secondary upon return:** Implement ergonomic vehicle modifications (Option 2) and a modified field-duty rotation (Option 1) on a tapered basis as the treating provider releases restrictions.
3. **Review point:** 60 days from the start of the accommodation, with treating provider re-evaluation status.

If physical therapy outcomes are positive and restrictions are lifted within the period, Mr. Marchetti returns to full field duties with Option 2 modifications in place. If restrictions become permanent or substantially long-term, the City will re-engage the interactive process to evaluate whether reassignment to Plan Review on a permanent basis is the appropriate accommodation, including a review of the Plan Review position's classification, pay grade, and any civil service implications.

## VIII. NEXT STEPS

1. HR to meet with Mr. Marchetti to confirm the recommended approach and that it is acceptable to him as the effective accommodation.
2. Planning Director to confirm the Plan Review assignment scope and supervisor.
3. Fleet to schedule the ergonomic vehicle modifications.
4. Draft the Accommodation Agreement reflecting the approach, with a 60-day review.
5. Submit to City Attorney for legal review before issuance.
6. Maintain medical documentation separately from the personnel file per 29 C.F.R. § 1630.14.
