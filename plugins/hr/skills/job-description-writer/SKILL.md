# Job Description Writer

> Drafts a job description that holds up to the FLSA, the ADA, and Title VII. Built for HR staff and hiring managers who need a defensible, plain-language posting that also functions as the position's foundation document.

## When to Use

- "Draft a job description for a Senior Accountant in our Finance Department."
- "I need a JD for a new Building Inspector II position before we post it."
- "Refresh the Public Works Foreman job description. The duties have changed since 2018."
- "Help me write the essential functions section for this Code Enforcement Officer role."
- "We're reclassifying this position from non-exempt to exempt. Draft the JD with the right FLSA analysis."
- A position is going to council for classification action and needs a clean JD attached to the personnel resolution.

## Inputs Needed

Required:

- Job title and proposed classification
- Department and supervisor's title
- Proposed FLSA status and basis (executive, administrative, professional, computer, or outside sales — 29 C.F.R. Part 541)
- Salary range or pay grade
- Primary duties — five to ten functions the position actually performs
- Minimum qualifications (education, experience, licenses, certifications)
- Physical demands and work environment

Optional: preferred qualifications, security clearance and background check requirements, specific certifications (TCOLE, TCFP, ICC, CGFO), travel and on-call expectations, bilingual preference and pay differential.

If the user has not provided the FLSA basis or the position's essential functions, ask before drafting.

## Process

1. Confirm the FLSA classification and document the rationale. Review the salary basis test and the duties test for the claimed exemption — see `references/flsa-exemption-test-summary.md`.
2. Distinguish essential from non-essential functions. Essential functions are the fundamental duties — what the role exists to do. This distinction controls the ADA reasonable accommodation analysis at 42 U.S.C. § 12111(8).
3. Draft the Job Summary in two to four sentences. State the purpose of the role, the supervision exercised and received, and the primary outcomes.
4. List Essential Functions as action-verb statements, each one a discrete duty. Avoid bundling. Use present tense.
5. Draft the KSAs. Tie each back to an essential function.
6. Write Minimum Qualifications tightly. Overstated minimums create Title VII disparate-impact exposure. Preferred qualifications go in a separate section.
7. Document Physical Demands and Work Environment in neutral, descriptive language. Specify lifting weights, standing or sitting durations, exposure to weather or hazardous materials, and required PPE.
8. Append the EEO Statement and the ADA reasonable accommodation notice.

## Output Format

Plain markdown. Length: 600 to 1,100 words depending on position complexity. Tone: factual, plain, neutral.

```
[CITY OF X]
JOB DESCRIPTION

Position: [Title]
Department: [Department]
Reports To: [Supervisor Title]
FLSA Status: [Exempt — basis / Non-Exempt]
Pay Grade: [Grade] | Salary Range: $[low] – $[high]
Date: [Adoption / revision date]

JOB SUMMARY
[2–4 sentences.]

ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS
The following are the essential functions of this position. Reasonable
accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to
perform these functions.
1. [Action-verb statement.]
2. [Action-verb statement.]
3. [Action-verb statement.]

NON-ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS
1. [Task that could be reassigned.]
2. [Task that could be reassigned.]

KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS, AND ABILITIES
- Knowledge of [domain area tied to an essential function].
- Skill in [technique or system].
- Ability to [outcome].

MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS
- Education: [requirement].
- Experience: [requirement].
- Licenses/Certifications: [requirement, with timing — e.g., "TCOLE
  Peace Officer license required at time of hire."]

PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS
- [Value-add credential or experience].

PHYSICAL DEMANDS
[Lifting, standing, sitting, climbing, reaching, vision, hearing.]

WORK ENVIRONMENT
[Office, field, mixed; weather, noise, hazard exposure; PPE.]

EQUAL EMPLOYMENT OPPORTUNITY
The City of [X] is an equal opportunity employer. The City does not
discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, sex (including
pregnancy, sexual orientation, and gender identity), national origin,
age (40 or older), disability, genetic information, or veteran status,
in accordance with Title VII of the Civil Rights Act of 1964, the Age
```

## TRAIGA & Compliance Notes

**AI use disclosure — transparency best practice (not a § 552.051 trigger):** Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.051(b)–(d) requires disclosure when a citizen *interacts directly with an AI system* (e.g., a chatbot, automated voice assistant, or AI-driven service interface). The disclosure trigger is *interaction*, not *output*. This skill produces a static document drafted with AI assistance; it does not put a citizen in direct interaction with AI, so § 552.051 is not engaged.

However, transparency about AI assistance in document drafting is sound practice and should align with the city's adopted AI use policy and the GovAI Coalition's guidance on documenting AI tool use. Consider a brief notation on documents distributed publicly or to the governing body: *"AI-assisted draft, reviewed by [staff title and name]."* This is a policy choice, not a statutory mandate.

**EEOC AI guidance:** The EEOC's 2023 technical assistance on AI in employment decisions cautions that AI-assisted screening can produce disparate impact under Title VII. A JD does not select candidates, but its qualifications and KSAs become the input to downstream screening tools. Keep minimums tight and job-related.

**FLSA (29 U.S.C. § 207; 29 C.F.R. Part 541):** Misclassification is the most common public-sector wage-and-hour exposure. The 2024 salary threshold rule has been subject to litigation; counsel should confirm the controlling threshold before finalizing exempt classifications.

**ADA (42 U.S.C. § 12101 et seq.):** Essential functions in the JD are the foundation of any accommodation analysis.

**Title VII, ADEA, GINA, USERRA:** Reflected in the EEO statement.

**Texas-specific:** Civil-service positions under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 143 or Ch. 158 have additional procedural requirements. Peace officer JDs must align with TCOLE rules.

**Public records:** Adopted JDs are public records under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552. Drafts may be exempt under § 552.111.

**Retention:** Per Texas State Library Local Government Retention Schedule GR.
