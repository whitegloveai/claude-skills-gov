# CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE, TEXAS
## JOB DESCRIPTION

**Position:** Senior Accountant
**Department:** Finance
**Reports To:** Assistant Finance Director
**FLSA Status:** Exempt (administrative exemption, 29 C.F.R. § 541.200)
**Pay Grade:** 18 | **Salary Range:** $68,400 – $92,300
**Date:** Adopted with FY2026 Pay Plan

### JOB SUMMARY

The Senior Accountant performs advanced governmental accounting work supporting the City's general ledger, monthly close, grant accounting, fixed assets, and annual audit. The position exercises independent judgment on accounting treatment, reconciliation, and audit support, and provides technical guidance to Accountant I and II staff. Work is performed under the general supervision of the Assistant Finance Director.

### ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS

The following are the essential functions of this position. Reasonable accommodations may be made to enable individuals with disabilities to perform these functions.

1. Manages the monthly general ledger close, including journal entry review, account reconciliations, and accrual analysis.
2. Maintains the fixed-asset accounting subledger, including capitalization decisions in line with the City's capitalization policy and GASB 34 reporting.
3. Performs grant accounting, including draw requests, federal financial reporting, and Single Audit (Uniform Guidance) coordination.
4. Prepares and reviews schedules supporting the Annual Comprehensive Financial Report (ACFR), including the Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards.
5. Coordinates with external auditors during interim and year-end fieldwork, providing requested workpapers and analyses.
6. Reviews accounts payable and accounts receivable transactions for accuracy, coding, and policy compliance, and resolves escalated reconciliation issues.
7. Recommends and implements improvements to accounting processes, internal controls, and chart-of-accounts structure.
8. Provides technical guidance and review for Accountant I and Accountant II staff and serves as backup to the Assistant Finance Director on close and reporting tasks.

### NON-ESSENTIAL FUNCTIONS

1. Represents the Finance Department at internal cross-departmental committees.
2. Attends Government Finance Officers Association (GFOA) and Texas Municipal League (TML) conferences and trainings.

### KNOWLEDGE, SKILLS, AND ABILITIES

- Knowledge of generally accepted accounting principles for state and local governments, including the GASB Codification.
- Knowledge of federal Uniform Guidance (2 C.F.R. Part 200) requirements for grant accounting.
- Knowledge of Texas budget law under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 102 and truth-in-taxation under Tex. Tax Code Ch. 26 as it relates to accounting tie-out.
- Skill in operating ERP financial systems and producing analytical schedules in Excel.
- Ability to exercise independent judgment on matters of accounting significance, including capitalization, accrual, and grant cost-allowability determinations.
- Ability to communicate technical accounting matters in plain language to department directors and to elected officials.

### MINIMUM QUALIFICATIONS

- **Education:** Bachelor's degree from an accredited institution in accounting, finance, or a closely related field.
- **Experience:** Four years of progressively responsible accounting experience.
- **Licenses/Certifications:** None required at hire.

### PREFERRED QUALIFICATIONS

- Governmental accounting experience, particularly with a Texas home-rule or general-law city.
- Certified Public Accountant (CPA) license or Certified Government Finance Officer (CGFO) certification.
- Experience with Tyler Munis, Workday Financials, or similar ERP financial systems.
- Experience supporting an unqualified ACFR opinion and a Single Audit.

### PHYSICAL DEMANDS

Work is primarily sedentary in an office environment. The position requires the ability to sit at a desk and operate a computer for extended periods, occasionally lift and carry files or supplies up to 25 pounds, and travel between City facilities for meetings.

### WORK ENVIRONMENT

Office environment at Cedar Ridge City Hall, 100 Main Street. Occasional after-hours work during budget adoption, year-end close, and audit fieldwork. Limited travel for training.
