# Interlocal Agreement Drafter

> Drafts an interlocal cooperation agreement under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 791. Built for city attorneys and contract administrators preparing an interlocal for council approval by resolution.

## When to Use

- "Draft the interlocal for animal control services with the county for FY2026."
- "We need a Ch. 791 agreement for dispatch services with the neighboring city."
- "Convert this scope of work into an interlocal cooperation agreement."
- "Build me a mutual aid interlocal for public works equipment sharing."
- A city is sharing a governmental function with another political subdivision (city, county, special district, school district, COG) and needs a Ch. 791 agreement both governing bodies will approve by resolution.

## Inputs Needed

Required:

- Parties — legal names.
- Governmental function shared. Ch. 791 permits administrative functions, police, fire, public health, parks, library, animal control, transportation, public works, jail, dispatch, GIS, IT, joint purchasing.
- Service description — what is delivered, to whom, where, how often.
- Cost-sharing — fixed fee, per-call, percentage, or hybrid.
- Term, renewal, and termination rights.
- Governance — single party administers, joint committee, or staff coordination.

Optional:

- Insurance (each party usually maintains its own coverage or TML pool participation).
- Facility or equipment use terms.
- Data-sharing terms if records (police, court, health) are exchanged.
- Performance metrics and reporting cadence.
- Whether each party has adopted the authorizing resolution.

If the function is not a governmental function under § 791.003, or if either party lacks current-revenue funding, ask before drafting.

## Process

1. Confirm the function qualifies as a governmental function under § 791.003 and that each party has independent authority to perform it.
2. Confirm funding. Each party must pay from current revenues legally available for the function under § 791.011(d).
3. Draft the Preamble — parties, Ch. 791 authority, purpose.
4. Draft Recitals — each party's authority, function shared, public interest served, § 791.011(d) current-revenues finding, resolution-condition recital.
5. Draft Definitions for operational terms (Services, Service Area, Operating Hours, Calls for Service, Direct Costs).
6. Draft Term and Termination — initial term, renewal, termination for convenience with notice, termination for breach with cure.
7. Draft Description of Services — scope, service standards, response times, coverage hours, exclusions.
8. Draft Cost Allocation and Payment — methodology, invoicing cadence, Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 2251 payment terms, end-of-year true-up.
9. Draft Governance — day-to-day point of contact, escalation path, annual review.
10. Draft Liability and Insurance — each party maintains its own coverage; sovereign immunity preserved.
11. Draft Indemnification carefully. Texas cities cannot indemnify third parties beyond statutory authorization. Use "each party responsible for its own acts and omissions." Avoid broad cross-indemnity.
12. Draft Records and Audit — each party retains its own records; both subject to Ch. 552.
13. Draft Public Information Cooperation — each party processes its own TPIA requests; reasonable cooperation on shared-function requests.
14. Draft Open Meetings Act Compliance — each party complies with Ch. 551.
15. Draft Sovereign Immunity Preservation — express recital under § 791.029.
16. Draft Governing Law and Venue — Texas law; venue in a named county.
17. Draft Counterparts and Signature Blocks — authorized officers, attest by city secretary or county clerk, resolution number, effective date.

## Output Format

A signature-ready interlocal cooperation agreement, eight to fifteen pages. Plain markdown.

```
INTERLOCAL COOPERATION AGREEMENT
FOR [GOVERNMENTAL FUNCTION] SERVICES
BETWEEN [PARTY A] AND [PARTY B]

This Interlocal Cooperation Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into
under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 791 by and between [Party A] and [Party B],
effective [Effective Date].

RECITALS
[Authority, function, public interest, current-revenues finding,
resolution conditions, statutory citations.]

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree:

ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS
ARTICLE 2 — TERM AND TERMINATION
ARTICLE 3 — DESCRIPTION OF SERVICES
ARTICLE 4 — COST ALLOCATION AND PAYMENT
ARTICLE 5 — GOVERNANCE AND POINT OF CONTACT
ARTICLE 6 — LIABILITY AND INSURANCE
ARTICLE 7 — INDEMNIFICATION; SOVEREIGN IMMUNITY PRESERVED
ARTICLE 8 — RECORDS AND AUDIT
ARTICLE 9 — PUBLIC INFORMATION COOPERATION
ARTICLE 10 — OPEN MEETINGS ACT COMPLIANCE
ARTICLE 11 — GOVERNING LAW AND VENUE
ARTICLE 12 — NOTICES
ARTICLE 13 — COUNTERPARTS; ELECTRONIC SIGNATURES
ARTICLE 14 — ENTIRE AGREEMENT

SIGNATURE BLOCKS
[Party A authorized officer; Party B authorized officer]
ATTEST: [City Secretary / County Clerk]
APPROVED AS TO FORM: [City Attorney / County Attorney]
Resolution No. [____] adopted [Date].
```

Tone: plain transactional drafting. Defined terms in initial caps. No marketing language, no operative provisions buried in recitals.

## TRAIGA & Compliance Notes

**AI use disclosure — transparency best practice (not a § 552.051 trigger):** Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.051(b)–(d) requires disclosure when a citizen *interacts directly with an AI system* (e.g., a chatbot, automated voice assistant, or AI-driven service interface). The disclosure trigger is *interaction*, not *output*. This skill produces a static document drafted with AI assistance; it does not put a citizen in direct interaction with AI, so § 552.051 is not engaged.

However, transparency about AI assistance in document drafting is sound practice and should align with the city's adopted AI use policy and the GovAI Coalition's guidance on documenting AI tool use. Consider a brief notation on documents distributed publicly or to the governing body: *"AI-assisted draft, reviewed by [staff title and name]."* This is a policy choice, not a statutory mandate.

**Statutory authority (Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 791):**

- § 791.003 — Definitions of governmental function and political subdivision.
- § 791.011 — Authority to contract; required findings; § 791.011(d) current-revenues funding and resolution approval by each governing body.
- § 791.014 — Joint administration authority.
- § 791.029 — Sovereign immunity not waived by entering into an interlocal.

**Council approval:** Both parties must approve by resolution before effectiveness. Recitals note the condition; signature block references the resolution number.

**Indemnification limits:** A Texas city cannot indemnify a third party without statutory authority. "Each party responsible for its own acts and omissions" is the standard formulation. Broader cross-indemnity must be reviewed by the city attorney.

**Sovereign immunity:** § 791.029 preserves each party's immunity. Recite expressly; avoid waiver language.

**Public information:** Executed interlocals are public under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552. Drafts may be exempt under § 552.111 until executed.

**Retention:** Permanently per Texas State Library Local Government Retention Schedule GR Item 1050-25.
