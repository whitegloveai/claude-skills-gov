# Example — Cedar Ridge / Caddo County Interlocal Cooperation Agreement for Animal Control Services

> The City of Cedar Ridge currently operates a small municipal animal shelter at half-capacity. Caddo County operates the regional shelter and animal control field services for the unincorporated area. City Manager Daniel Ahn and the Caddo County Commissioners Court agreed in February 2026 to consolidate field response and sheltering under the county's existing program, with the city paying a not-to-exceed $84,000 for FY2026 (Oct 1, 2026 to Sept 30, 2027). This is the draft agreement prepared by City Attorney Patricia Mendoza's office for the May 26, 2026 Cedar Ridge City Council consent agenda Item 4c and for parallel approval by Caddo County Commissioners Court on May 27, 2026.

---

```
INTERLOCAL COOPERATION AGREEMENT
FOR ANIMAL CONTROL AND SHELTER SERVICES
BETWEEN THE CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE, TEXAS
AND CADDO COUNTY, TEXAS

This Interlocal Cooperation Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into
under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 791 by and between the CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE,
TEXAS, a Texas home-rule municipal corporation ("City"), and CADDO
COUNTY, TEXAS, a political subdivision of the State of Texas
("County"), effective October 1, 2026 ("Effective Date").

RECITALS

WHEREAS, City and County are each "local governments" within the
meaning of Tex. Gov't Code § 791.003(4) and each is authorized to
enter into interlocal cooperation agreements under Tex. Gov't Code
Ch. 791;

WHEREAS, animal control, sheltering, and humane disposition are
governmental functions within the meaning of Tex. Gov't Code §
791.003(3)(A) and each Party has independent authority to perform
such functions within its jurisdiction;

WHEREAS, County operates the Caddo County Animal Services facility
located at 2400 Industrial Drive, Caddo, Texas, and provides field
response and sheltering services in the unincorporated area of the
County;

WHEREAS, City operates a small municipal animal shelter at 612 Live
Oak Avenue, Cedar Ridge, Texas, that is at or near operational
capacity, and City desires to consolidate field response and
sheltering services with the County's regional program in the public
interest of cost efficiency, animal welfare, and improved service
levels for City residents;

WHEREAS, each Party finds that payments required of it under this
Agreement will be made from current revenues legally available to
that Party as required by Tex. Gov't Code § 791.011(d);

WHEREAS, this Agreement is conditioned on adoption by each Party's
governing body by order, ordinance, or resolution as required by
Tex. Gov't Code § 791.011(d), with the City Council acting on
Resolution No. 2026-R-038 and Caddo County Commissioners Court
acting on Order No. 2026-CO-091; and

WHEREAS, neither Party intends to waive any sovereign or
governmental immunity by entering into this Agreement, and Tex.
Gov't Code § 791.029 provides that no waiver results;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants set forth
below, the Parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS

1.1 "Animal Control Officer" means a peace officer or civilian
    officer of the County trained and certified under Tex. Health &
    Safety Code Ch. 829.
1.2 "Calls for Service" means each dispatched response to a citizen
    request for animal control within the City limits.
1.3 "City Limits" means the corporate boundaries of the City of
    Cedar Ridge as of the Effective Date, as may be amended by
    annexation, with annexed areas added on the first day of the
    fiscal year following annexation.
1.4 "Direct Costs" means the County's allocated cost per call,
    inclusive of personnel, vehicle, fuel, kennel days, and
    veterinary medical, calculated annually under § 4.2.
1.5 "Services" means the animal control field response, sheltering,
    humane disposition, adoption, and reclaim services described in
    Article 3.

ARTICLE 2 — TERM AND TERMINATION

2.1 Initial Term. This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and
    terminates on September 30, 2027.
2.2 Renewal. The Agreement renews for successive one-year terms
    aligned with the County fiscal year unless either Party gives
    written notice of non-renewal not less than ninety (90) days
    before the renewal date.
2.3 Termination for Convenience. Either Party may terminate this
    Agreement for any reason on one hundred eighty (180) days
    written notice.
2.4 Termination for Breach. Either Party may terminate for material
    breach on thirty (30) days written notice if the breaching Party
    fails to cure within that period.
2.5 Effect of Termination. Animals in County custody at the time of
    termination remain in County custody under County policy. City
    is responsible for Direct Costs incurred through the effective
    termination date.

ARTICLE 3 — DESCRIPTION OF SERVICES

3.1 Field Response. County will dispatch an Animal Control Officer
    in response to all Calls for Service within the City Limits
    under the following targets: emergency calls (bite, injured
    animal, aggressive loose animal, livestock at large on a state
    highway), response within ninety (90) minutes; routine calls,
    response within one (1) business day.
3.2 Sheltering. County will accept all animals impounded within the
    City Limits at the County shelter and will provide kennel,
    veterinary medical, vaccination, and microchip services per
    County policy.
3.3 Humane Disposition. County will perform humane disposition under
    Tex. Health & Safety Code Ch. 821 and Ch. 822 and applicable
    County policy. City does not direct disposition decisions.
3.4 Adoption and Reclaim. County operates the adoption and reclaim
    program and retains all related fees collected.
3.5 Reporting. County will provide the City Manager a quarterly
    report including total Calls for Service in the City, response
    times, intakes by category, outcomes, and any incident report
    flagged for follow-up.
3.6 Exclusions. Services do not include: (a) wildlife control in
    private structures; (b) deceased domestic animal pickup from
    private property; (c) dangerous dog adjudication proceedings
    (the City retains its responsibility under Tex. Health & Safety
    Code Ch. 822).

ARTICLE 4 — COST ALLOCATION AND PAYMENT

4.1 Not-to-Exceed FY2026. The City's payment obligation for the
    initial term shall not exceed eighty-four thousand dollars
    ($84,000), inclusive of all Direct Costs.
4.2 Methodology. The not-to-exceed amount is calculated as the
    County's projected Direct Cost per call multiplied by the
    five-year rolling average of Calls for Service originating in
    the City Limits (approximately 420 calls per year at $200 per
    call), with a 10% contingency.
4.3 Invoicing. County will invoice the City monthly on the tenth day
    for the prior month's actual Calls for Service and Direct Costs.
4.4 Payment. Payment is due within thirty (30) days of receipt of an
    undisputed invoice, consistent with Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 2251.
4.5 Annual True-Up. At the end of each fiscal year the Parties will
    reconcile actual Direct Costs to the not-to-exceed amount. If
    actual Direct Costs are below the not-to-exceed amount, no
    refund is due; if actual Direct Costs exceed the not-to-exceed
    amount, County may submit a written request for additional
    consideration, which is subject to City Council appropriation.
4.6 Renewal-Year Pricing. The not-to-exceed amount for each renewal
    year shall be set by written addendum executed by both Parties
    no later than August 1 of the preceding fiscal year.

ARTICLE 5 — GOVERNANCE AND POINT OF CONTACT

5.1 City Point of Contact: Cedar Ridge Police Chief Eduardo Vega or
    designee.
5.2 County Point of Contact: Director of Caddo County Animal
    Services.
5.3 Annual Review. The Points of Contact will meet at least
    annually, no later than July 1, to review prior-year
    performance, set the renewal-year amount, and address any
    service issues.
5.4 Escalation. Unresolved issues escalate to the City Manager and
    the County Judge in writing.

ARTICLE 6 — LIABILITY AND INSURANCE

6.1 Each Party maintains its own coverage for its own officers,
    employees, vehicles, and operations through its participation
    in the Texas Municipal League Intergovernmental Risk Pool or
    equivalent self-insurance.
6.2 Neither Party is required to name the other as additional
    insured.

ARTICLE 7 — INDEMNIFICATION; SOVEREIGN IMMUNITY PRESERVED

7.1 Each Party is responsible for its own acts and omissions and the
    acts and omissions of its officers and employees. Neither Party
    indemnifies the other.
7.2 Nothing in this Agreement waives, modifies, or impairs the
    sovereign or governmental immunity of either Party, and Tex.
    Gov't Code § 791.029 expressly preserves such immunity.

ARTICLE 8 — RECORDS AND AUDIT

8.1 Each Party retains its own books and records relating to this
    Agreement for the retention period required by the Texas State
    Library Local Government Retention Schedule.
8.2 On reasonable written notice, each Party may inspect the other
    Party's records that directly relate to invoiced amounts under
    this Agreement.

ARTICLE 9 — PUBLIC INFORMATION COOPERATION

9.1 Each Party processes its own requests for public information
    under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552. The Parties will cooperate
    reasonably on requests that implicate both.

ARTICLE 10 — OPEN MEETINGS ACT COMPLIANCE

10.1 Each Party complies with Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 551 for its own
     deliberations on this Agreement and on the Services.

ARTICLE 11 — GOVERNING LAW AND VENUE

11.1 Governing Law. This Agreement is governed by the laws of the
     State of Texas.
11.2 Venue. Mandatory venue for any action arising from this
     Agreement lies in the state district courts of Caddo County,
     Texas.

ARTICLE 12 — NOTICES

12.1 All notices required under this Agreement shall be in writing,
     delivered by hand, certified mail, or recognized overnight
     courier to:

     For City:               For County:
     City Manager             County Judge
     City of Cedar Ridge      Caddo County
     100 Civic Plaza          200 Courthouse Square
     Cedar Ridge, TX [ZIP]    Caddo, TX [ZIP]
     With copy to:            With copy to:
     City Attorney            County Attorney

ARTICLE 13 — COUNTERPARTS; ELECTRONIC SIGNATURES

13.1 This Agreement may be executed in counterparts. Electronic
     signatures are valid under Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code Ch. 322.

ARTICLE 14 — ENTIRE AGREEMENT

14.1 This Agreement is the entire agreement of the Parties on the
     subject matter and supersedes prior oral or written
     understandings. Amendments must be in writing and approved by
     both governing bodies.
```
