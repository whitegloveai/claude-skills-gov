# Example — SaaS Permit Tracking Vendor Redline Summary

> Cedar Ridge purchased a hosted permit-tracking SaaS from PermitFlow, Inc. for the Planning and Development Services Department. The city sent its standard IT services contract; PermitFlow returned 14 redlines. The contract administrator prepared this memo for City Attorney Patricia Mendoza's review before the negotiation call.

---

```

PermitFlow accepted the bulk of the city's terms but pushed on four
clusters: data ownership, liability and indemnity, breach notification
timing, and termination. Their redlines track their public master

4. RISK FLAGS — HIGH

4.1 — Data ownership replaced with license. Vendor inserted "City grants
PermitFlow a perpetual, irrevocable, royalty-free license to use, modify,
and create derivative works from City Data." This converts city data from
city-owned to vendor-licensed, with perpetual rights surviving termination.
RECOMMENDED RESPONSE: Reject. Restore city-owned-data language. Vendor's
license should be limited, revocable, and tied to providing the service.

4.4 — "Aggregated data" carve-out. Vendor added a right to use
"aggregated, de-identified data" for product improvement and analytics
with no geographic or purpose limit. Permit data, even aggregated, can
reveal patterns about specific properties and applicants.
RECOMMENDED RESPONSE: Push back. If retained, narrow to internal product
improvement only, with explicit prohibition on resale, on sharing with
third parties, and on use for any model training that is offered to
other customers. Confirm aggregation methodology in writing.

6.1 / 6.2 — Indemnity narrowed and capped. "Gross negligence" floor and
12-month-fees cap. Cyber breach claims commonly exceed the cap.
RECOMMENDED RESPONSE: Restore "negligence" standard. Carve breach,
indemnity, and IP infringement claims out of the liability cap. If a
cap remains, it should be no less than $5M cyber insurance limit.

7.1 — Breach notification stretched 24 hours to 7 days. Cedar Ridge's
incident response plan assumes 24-hour notification to give the city
time to issue HB 4 notice to affected Texas residents (Tex. Bus. &
Comm. Code § 521.053). A 7-day delay puts the city in a difficult
position on state and federal notice obligations.
RECOMMENDED RESPONSE: Reject. 24-hour written notice of confirmed
incident is non-negotiable for IT contracts handling city data.

11.1 / 11.2 — Delaware law and venue. Common SaaS boilerplate but
inappropriate for a Texas city contract. Removes the city from its
home forum and applies a body of law in which the city has no public
counsel familiar with the underlying disputes.
RECOMMENDED RESPONSE: Reject. Texas law, exclusive venue in Caddo
County district court.

5. RISK FLAGS — MEDIUM

5.1 — Cyber insurance reduced from $5M to $2M. Below the city's IT
contract standard for systems holding PII on residents.
RECOMMENDED RESPONSE: Restore $5M.

5.3 — Primary and non-contributory language struck. Creates layering
disputes with city's TML insurance pool.
RECOMMENDED RESPONSE: Restore.

6.3 — IP infringement indemnity removed. Standard SaaS protection.
RECOMMENDED RESPONSE: Restore.

8.1 — Termination for convenience stretched 30 to 90 days.
RECOMMENDED RESPONSE: Counter at 60 days.

9.2 — Auto-renewal at then-current pricing. Creates appropriations
risk and budgetary surprise.
RECOMMENDED RESPONSE: Reject auto-renewal. City elects renewal each
year in writing or contract expires.

6. RISK FLAGS — LOW / CLEANUP

- 3.2 Net 15 — counter at Net 30 (Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 2251 default).
- 14.1 typo — accept.

7. PROVISIONS POTENTIALLY VOID OR UNENFORCEABLE UNDER TEXAS LAW

- Section 11.1 (Delaware law) and 11.2 (Delaware venue) raise concerns
  about removing a Texas governmental entity from its home forum.
  Recommend rejecting on policy and forum-selection grounds rather
  than litigating enforceability.
- No mandatory arbitration was inserted; no Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem.
  Code Ch. 154 issue.

8. NEGOTIATION RECOMMENDATIONS

Open the call with three non-negotiables: Texas law and Caddo County
venue (11.1, 11.2); 24-hour breach notification (7.1); and city-owned
data (4.1). If PermitFlow cannot move on all three, recommend
escalating to the City Manager before proceeding. The remaining 11
items are negotiable in normal back-and-forth.

9. COUNCIL APPROVAL REQUIRED

Yes. First-year value $84,500 exceeds the Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 252.021
threshold and the Cedar Ridge ordinance threshold. Scheduled for the
May 26, 2026 regular meeting consent agenda pending acceptable redlines.

10. ROUTING — OTHER DEPARTMENT REVIEW

- Finance: § 3.2 payment terms, § 9.2 auto-renewal, total cost of
  ownership over the 5-year horizon.
- IT: §§ 4.1, 4.4, 7.1, the security exhibit, and the vendor risk
  questionnaire already on file (see it/vendor-risk-questionnaire
  output from April 18, 2026 — three high-risk items overlap with
  this redline).
- Risk Management: §§ 5.1, 5.3, 6.1-6.3.

11. OPEN QUESTIONS FOR THE CITY ATTORNEY

a. § 4.4 aggregated data — is "internal product improvement" workable
   if we add a no-resale, no-third-party-sharing prohibition, or do
   we hold the line on no carve-out at all?
b. § 7.1 — confirm 24 hours aligns with the city's adopted incident
   response plan (last revised by IT on Jan 2026).
c. § 9.2 — should we offer 3% annual escalator cap as a compromise on
   no auto-renewal?

End of memo.
```
