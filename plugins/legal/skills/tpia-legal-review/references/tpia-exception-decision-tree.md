# TPIA Exception Decision Tree — AG Referral and Common Arguments

The first question on any TPIA withholding is whether the city must request an AG decision under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.301 within 10 business days. Failure to refer when required generally presumes the records public under § 552.302.

This reference walks the major exceptions and notes (a) whether AG referral is required, (b) the standard factual basis the brief must articulate, and (c) common counter-arguments by requesters.

## When AG Referral Is Required

The general rule: a governmental body that wishes to withhold information must seek an AG decision under § 552.301(a) unless a **previous determination** by the AG addressing the same type of information has already issued ("OR letter" or "OR ruling"). If a previous determination covers the records, the city may withhold without referring, citing the OR letter number in the response.

The city must refer when:

1. No previous determination covers the records.
2. The exception is discretionary and the city wishes to invoke it.
3. The exception is "made confidential by other law" under § 552.101 but no previous determination applies to this specific statute or category.
4. A third party with proprietary or privacy interests asserts an exception (§ 552.305 notice triggers AG involvement).

The city does **not** need to refer when:

1. A prior AG ruling squarely addresses the records (cite OR number in response).
2. The records are made confidential by a specific statute or constitutional provision that has been previously construed by the AG to require withholding without case-by-case referral (some "mandatory" categories).
3. The records fall within an enumerated exception that the AG has held is self-executing (rare — verify).

When in doubt, refer. The cost of a referral is small; the cost of waiver is total disclosure.

## Exception-by-Exception Notes

### § 552.101 — Confidential by Other Law

The catch-all. Covers statutes, constitutional provisions, judicial decisions, and common-law privileges that make information confidential.

**Referral:** Generally required for each new statute or category. Once a previous determination addresses a specific statute (e.g., HIPAA-protected information; § 261.201 child abuse investigation records; § 411.083 criminal history record information), subsequent withholding under that authority may proceed without referral.

**Common arguments by requester:** The cited statute does not in fact make the specific record confidential; the statute applies to a different governmental body; the information has been disclosed elsewhere and confidentiality is waived.

### § 552.103 — Pending or Anticipated Litigation

Information related to pending or reasonably anticipated litigation.

**Referral:** Generally required. The AG tests whether litigation is "pending or reasonably anticipated" — concrete, specific evidence is needed; speculation is insufficient.

**Common arguments:** Litigation is not concrete; the records pre-date or post-date the litigation; the information is otherwise publicly available.

### § 552.104 — Competitive Bidding

Information that would give advantage to a competitor.

**Referral:** Required. Time-limited — typically protects only during the pending procurement.

**Common arguments:** The procurement has closed; the information would not give advantage; the city is improperly withholding to protect a favored bidder.

### § 552.107 — Attorney Communications and Work Product

(1) Information the attorney is prohibited from disclosing because of a duty to the client; (2) attorney work product.

**Referral:** Generally required for each new context, but the AG has a substantial body of prior determinations on common attorney-client contexts. Many cities apply § 552.107 to clearly privileged email without referral, citing the controlling case law and prior AG determinations on the specific context.

**Common arguments:** The communication is not actually privileged (e.g., copied to non-client third parties, waiving privilege); the communication is operational rather than for legal advice; the privilege has been waived by subsequent disclosure.

**Sampling note:** When asserting § 552.107 over a long email thread, separate clearly legal-advice content from operational content. Over-broad redactions are commonly challenged successfully.

### § 552.108 — Law Enforcement

Multiple sub-tests for active investigations, internal records, and prosecutorial files.

**Referral:** Sub-test dependent. Active-investigation withholdings are often handled with prior AG determinations on the specific category.

**Common arguments:** Investigation is no longer active; the information has been released to others (charging documents, witness statements); the public-interest balancing test favors release.

### § 552.110 — Trade Secrets and Commercial Information

Third-party trade secret and commercial information that would cause substantial competitive harm.

**Referral:** Required. Must give the affected third party notice under § 552.305 and an opportunity to brief the AG.

**Common arguments:** The information is not a trade secret under the *In re Bass* factors; competitive harm is not substantial or particularized.

### § 552.111 — Agency Memoranda (Deliberative Process)

Internal advice, opinion, or recommendation before a decision.

**Referral:** Generally required. Limited to genuinely pre-decisional, deliberative material; does not protect factual underlying records or final agency decisions.

**Common arguments:** The record is factual, not opinion; the underlying decision has been made and the deliberative-process rationale no longer applies; the record is final agency action.

### § 552.117 — Personal Information of Officers and Employees

Home address, home telephone, personal email, social security number, family member information of public employees and officials who elect protection.

**Referral:** Not required for the listed categories when the employee has elected protection. The city redacts as a matter of routine.

### § 552.130 — Driver's License and Motor Vehicle Information

Mandatory redaction. No referral required.

### § 552.136 — Credit Card, Debit Card, Charge Card, and Access Device Numbers

Mandatory redaction. No referral required.

### § 552.137 — Email Addresses of Private Citizens

Mandatory redaction unless the requester has affirmatively consented to disclosure or the email address is otherwise required to be public.

**Common arguments:** The email address is a business or governmental address, not a private one; the citizen has previously made the address public.

### § 552.139 — Computer Network and Cybersecurity Information

Information about computer network security, password records, or vulnerabilities.

**Referral:** Generally required for each new category. Many prior determinations cover common cybersecurity records.

### § 552.151 — Encoded Geolocation, Surveillance, and Body-Worn Camera

Recent additions. Verify the current statute language at the time of the request.

## The Cardinal Rule

If the city is uncertain, refer to the AG within 10 business days. A late referral or a missing referral defaults to public disclosure under § 552.302. The cost of asking is two weeks; the cost of waiver is unbounded.
