# Litigation Summary Helper

> Produces an attorney-client privileged, work-product litigation summary for council executive session. Built for the city attorney's office briefing the governing body on a case under Tex. Gov't Code § 551.071.

## When to Use

- "Draft the executive session memo on the slip-and-fall case for the May 26 council meeting."
- "Council needs a brief on the discovery posture of the wage-and-hour matter."
- "Summarize the settlement offer in *Smith v. City* for council action."
- "Draft a § 551.071 memo on the new TCEQ enforcement action."
- Any case requiring a structured executive-session briefing under § 551.071 attorney consultation.

## Inputs Needed

Required:

- Case caption, court, docket number, and parties
- Case posture: pleading stage / discovery / pretrial / trial / appeal / settlement / pre-suit demand
- Allegations summary (factual and legal claims)
- Key legal issues and city's defenses
- Exposure assessment (rough dollar range or non-monetary exposure)
- Settlement posture: any offer outstanding, mediation scheduled, demand made
- Key upcoming dates (motion deadlines, hearings, depositions, trial)
- Recommended next action requiring council direction

Optional:

- Insurance coverage status (TML, self-insured retention, primary, excess)
- Outside counsel of record (if applicable)
- Prior council direction on the case
- Comparable case outcomes for reference
- Reserves established and any prior settlement authority granted
- Discovery materials summary

If exposure or settlement posture is genuinely unknown at the time of the briefing, say so and offer a path to develop the assessment rather than guess.

## Process

1. Privilege markings first. Stamp the document "ATTORNEY-CLIENT PRIVILEGED AND ATTORNEY WORK PRODUCT — PREPARED FOR EXECUTIVE SESSION UNDER TEX. GOV'T CODE § 551.071 — DO NOT DISCLOSE." Privilege does not attach by talisman, but improper handling can waive it. The marking signals the document's character.
2. Confirm executive session authority. § 551.071 permits closed-session consultation when public discussion would conflict with the Texas Disciplinary Rules of Professional Conduct. The agenda must cite § 551.071 specifically (case caption suffices; "consultation with attorney" alone does not).
3. Draft the caption block: parties, court, docket, claim type, key dates, outside counsel and insurance, prior council direction, attorney author.
4. Case posture in one paragraph: procedural state and the next material event. No editorializing.
5. Allegations, two to four paragraphs: plaintiff's factual narrative and legal theories. Stay neutral — the memo may be subpoenaed in collateral litigation if privilege is waived.
6. Defenses, two to four paragraphs: procedural (immunity, limitations, notice), substantive, damages.
7. For municipal cases, run the immunity analysis under Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code Ch. 101 (Tort Claims Act): does a limited waiver apply (motor-vehicle, premises defect with tangible personal property condition or use), § 101.101 pre-suit notice satisfied, § 101.023 damages caps.
8. Exposure assessment. Range with assumptions; distinguish defense costs from indemnity exposure; note how discovery outcomes would shift the range.
9. Discovery and litigation status. Outstanding written discovery, depositions taken and scheduled, expert disclosures, dispositive motion windows.
10. Settlement posture and offers. Outstanding demand or offer, mediation status, prior negotiations.
11. Strategy options, three to five. Each: description, cost, timeline, risk profile, expected outcome.
12. Recommendation. The attorney's recommended path with rationale.
13. Council action requested. Specific: settle within a stated range; accept mediator's recommendation; defend through trial; appeal.

## Output Format

A privileged memo, four to seven pages, in this structure:

```
ATTORNEY-CLIENT PRIVILEGED AND ATTORNEY WORK PRODUCT
PREPARED FOR EXECUTIVE SESSION UNDER TEX. GOV'T CODE § 551.071
DO NOT DISCLOSE

MEMORANDUM

To:        Honorable Mayor and Members of the City Council
           [Council Member names by district]
From:      [City Attorney]
Date:      [Date]
Re:        [Case caption], [Court], [Docket No.]
           Agenda Item: Executive Session under § 551.071

1. CASE CAPTION AND POSTURE
   Court, docket, parties, claim type, key dates,
   outside counsel, insurance.

2. ALLEGATIONS
   Plaintiff's factual narrative and legal theories.

3. DEFENSES
   Procedural, substantive, damages.

4. IMMUNITY ANALYSIS
   For tort claims: Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code Ch. 101
   waiver analysis, pre-suit notice, damages caps.

5. EXPOSURE ASSESSMENT
   Range with assumptions and contingencies.

6. DISCOVERY AND LITIGATION STATUS
   Where the case is and what's coming up.

7. SETTLEMENT POSTURE
   Offers, demands, mediation.

8. STRATEGY OPTIONS
   A | B | C | (D | E)

9. RECOMMENDATION
   [Attorney's recommended path]

10. COUNCIL ACTION REQUESTED
    Specific authority sought.

Attachments (separately marked privileged):
- Pleadings summary
- Mediator profile, if applicable
- Reserves and insurance status
```

Tone: lawyer-to-client, candid, plain. Council members are intelligent non-lawyers; use legal terms with brief glosses. Avoid hedging beyond what the facts warrant. Do not assert facts in dispute as if settled.

## TRAIGA & Compliance Notes

**AI use disclosure — transparency best practice (not a § 552.051 trigger):** Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.051(b)–(d) requires disclosure when a citizen *interacts directly with an AI system* (e.g., a chatbot, automated voice assistant, or AI-driven service interface). The disclosure trigger is *interaction*, not *output*. This skill produces a static document drafted with AI assistance; it does not put a citizen in direct interaction with AI, so § 552.051 is not engaged.

However, transparency about AI assistance in document drafting is sound practice and should align with the city's adopted AI use policy and the GovAI Coalition's guidance on documenting AI tool use. Consider a brief notation on documents distributed publicly or to the governing body: *"AI-assisted draft, reviewed by [staff title and name]."* This is a policy choice, not a statutory mandate.

**Privilege:** Distribute only to council members and the city manager; never include in publicly posted packets; deliver in closed session or via privileged-document delivery; collect copies at session close. Reproduction in the public packet waives privilege.

**Open meetings (Tex. Gov't Code § 551.071):** Closed-session consultation is permitted under § 551.071. Agenda must cite § 551.071 specifically. Final action on settlement occurs in open session under § 551.102.

**Settlement authority:** Confirm the charter's settlement authority allocation. Most charters reserve settlement above a stated threshold to the council.

**Public information:** Memos prepared in anticipation of litigation are excepted under Tex. Gov't Code §§ 552.103, 552.107. Executed settlement agreements are generally public under § 552.022(a)(18).

**Retention:** Litigation files retain 10 years after final disposition per TSLAC Schedule LC Item 5350-01.
