2. ALLEGATIONS
==============================================================

Plaintiff alleges she slipped and fell on a section of cracked
concrete sidewalk at the entrance to the Cedar Ridge Senior
Center (412 Magnolia Avenue) on April 9, 2025 at approximately
9:15 AM. Plaintiff alleges the cracked and uneven sidewalk
created a tripping hazard known to the city for at least eight
months prior, that prior verbal complaints had been made to
Senior Center staff, and that the condition was not marked,
repaired, or otherwise addressed.

Plaintiff's injuries are pleaded as a left hip fracture
requiring open reduction and internal fixation, followed by
twelve weeks of physical therapy. Medical specials pleaded:
$72,400. Lost wages pleaded: $4,200 (part-time consulting
work). Pain and suffering: unliquidated.

Legal theories pleaded:
1. Premises defect under Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code §
   101.022(a) — limited duty owed to licensee.
2. Negligent maintenance.
3. (Originally pleaded) gross negligence claim — dismissed by
   plaintiff at the March 2026 hearing.

==============================================================
3. DEFENSES
==============================================================

Procedural:
- Sovereign immunity preserved by Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code
  Ch. 101 except as expressly waived.
- Pre-suit notice timely filed; this defense is not available.

Substantive:
- The city's duty to a licensee is to warn of, or make safe,
  conditions of which the city has actual knowledge and the
  licensee does not. State Dep't of Highways v. Payne, 838
  S.W.2d 235 (Tex. 1992).
- Actual knowledge is disputed. The city has no written
  complaint log entry for the Senior Center sidewalk in the
  twelve months preceding the incident. Plaintiff's alleged
  verbal complaints to Senior Center staff are contested; the
  staff member named (J. Ortiz) does not recall such a
  conversation.
- Open and obvious. The defect (a 1.25-inch elevation
  differential) is photographed and visible. Cf. Austin v. Kroger
  Texas, L.P., 465 S.W.3d 193 (Tex. 2015) (open-and-obvious
  doctrine limits duty).

Damages:
- Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 101.023 caps liability at
  $250,000 per person for a municipality.
- Comparative responsibility under Ch. 33 may apply.

==============================================================
4. IMMUNITY ANALYSIS
==============================================================

Premises-defect claims against a city are within the limited
waiver of immunity under Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code §
101.021(2) and § 101.022. The waiver is limited.

Elements:
1. Condition or use of tangible personal or real property —
   the sidewalk satisfies this element.
2. Duty owed to licensee absent payment for entry — Smith was
   a licensee (no admission fee).
3. The city's duty is limited to (a) failure to warn or make
   safe a condition that posed an unreasonable risk of harm
   and (b) actual knowledge of the condition.

Actual knowledge is the contested element. If plaintiff
establishes actual knowledge, immunity is waived to the extent
of $250,000 per person under § 101.023. If actual knowledge
is not established, immunity bars recovery.

The city has a non-frivolous immunity defense based on the
absence of written complaint records. The defense is not
airtight: depositions of Senior Center staff are scheduled in
June and July 2026 and may surface unfavorable testimony.

==============================================================
5. EXPOSURE ASSESSMENT
==============================================================

Range estimate (with assumptions):

- Best case (immunity preserved on summary judgment): $0
  indemnity, $35,000 to $50,000 in remaining defense costs
  through trial.
- Likely range (defendant verdict at trial): $0 indemnity,
  $90,000 to $120,000 total defense costs.
- Unfavorable range (plaintiff verdict, comparative
  responsibility allocates some fault to the city): $80,000
  to $140,000 indemnity plus defense costs.
- Worst case (plaintiff verdict on full liability, capped):
  $150,000 to $250,000 indemnity (subject to § 101.023 cap)
  plus full defense costs.

The wide range reflects the unresolved actual-knowledge issue.
A summary-judgment ruling either way will tighten the range
substantially.
