# Example: $25 Million Street Improvement Bond Election Notice — City of Cedar Ridge

This is a worked example for the election-notice-generator skill. The fictional City of Cedar Ridge, TX is calling a general obligation bond election to fund a citywide street improvement program. The bond proposition appears across other skill examples in this plugin (see the tax-rate-hearing-notice example for the FY2026 rate that incorporates debt service on this issuance).

## Input

- **Entity:** City of Cedar Ridge, Texas (home-rule, council-manager, population approximately 32,000)
- **Election type:** General obligation bond election
- **Election date:** Tuesday, November 3, 2026 (uniform election date)
- **Ordering ordinance:** Ordinance 2026-19, adopted by Cedar Ridge City Council on August 11, 2026
- **Proposition:** Proposition A — $25,000,000 in general obligation bonds for street improvements
- **Polling locations on election day:** Cedar Ridge City Hall, Live Oak Library, Pecan Creek Community Center
- **Early voting:** October 19 through October 30, 2026
- **Early voting locations:** Cedar Ridge City Hall, Live Oak Library
- **Early Voting Clerk:** Marisol Tovar, City Secretary, 200 Live Oak Drive, Cedar Ridge, TX 75000, (555) 555-0100, elections@cedarridgetx.gov
- **Newspaper:** Cedar Ridge Gazette
- **Bilingual coverage:** Spanish required under Tex. Election Code Ch. 272 and federal Voting Rights Act

## Notes for the User

- The ballot proposition language must match Ordinance 2026-19 exactly. Bond counsel should review the English and Spanish text in parallel before publication.
- Publication date target: the notice should appear in the Cedar Ridge Gazette no later than October 24, 2026 (ten days before election) and no earlier than October 4, 2026 (thirty days before election).
- Bulletin board and website posting begins October 13, 2026 (twenty-first day before election).
- The early voting clerk role is held by the City Secretary unless the council appoints another officer; this example reflects Marisol Tovar in that role following Helen Marquez's retirement.
- The Spanish translation reflects standard Texas Secretary of State phrasing for general obligation bond ballot language. Any change to proposition wording in English must produce a corresponding update in Spanish.
- Continuity: the FY2026 tax rate example uses a debt service component sized to support this $25M issuance.
