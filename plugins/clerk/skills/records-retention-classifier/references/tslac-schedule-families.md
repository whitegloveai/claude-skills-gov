# TSLAC Schedule Families — Overview

The Texas State Library and Archives Commission publishes a set of records retention schedules for local governments under the authority of Tex. Gov't Code § 441.158 and Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 203. Each schedule covers a functional area and contains numbered items that prescribe retention periods and disposition authority. This file summarizes the schedules a clerk most often encounters and lists representative item types so that staff can route a document to the right schedule before drilling into the specific item number.

This is a working overview. Always verify item numbers and retention periods against the current TSLAC schedule before relying on the output. TSLAC posts the authoritative schedules at tsl.texas.gov.

## GR — General Records Schedule

Applies to all local governments and covers records common across departments. The clerk's office uses GR most heavily.

Typical records:

- Meeting notices, agendas, and minutes (Item 1000-26, 1000-27): PERM
- Public information requests and correspondence (Item 1025-01): AC + 3 years
- Records control schedule and disposition logs (Item 1000-44): PERM
- General administrative correspondence (Item 1025-26): AC + 2 years (varies by significance)
- Routing slips, transmittal letters: US (when obsolete)
- Annual reports and statistical summaries (Item 1000-32): PERM
- Policies and procedures, current and superseded (Item 1000-46): PERM (current); AC + 5 years (superseded)
- Internal audit working papers (Item 1000-13): AC + 5 years

## LC — Records of Local Counsel and the Governing Body

Covers records of the elected body, the city manager's office, the city attorney, and similar offices. The clerk and the city attorney share custody of many of these.

Typical records:

- Ordinances and resolutions (Item 1100-04): PERM
- Charter and charter amendments (Item 1100-01): PERM
- Contracts and agreements (Item 1100-11): AC + 4 years (life of contract plus four years; longer for capital infrastructure)
- Litigation files (Item 1100-15): AC + 5 years from final disposition
- Bond records (Item 1100-08): LIFE of the bond plus 5 years
- Boards, commissions, and committee files (Item 1100-22): AC + 5 years
- Elected official correspondence: classified by content, not by office

## PS — Public Safety

Covers police, fire, EMS, and emergency management. Long retention periods are common; some items are permanent.

Typical records:

- Offense reports (Item 1175-01): retention by offense severity, ranging from AC + 5 years to PERM for homicide and certain unsolved cases
- Use of force reports (Item 1175-11): PERM
- Internal affairs files (Item 1175-14): retention by outcome
- Fire incident reports (Item 1185-01): AC + 10 years
- Training records, sworn personnel: term of service + 5 years
- Body-worn camera footage: retention by category (evidence vs. administrative); see § 1701.661 Tex. Occ. Code overlay

## HR — Human Resources

Covers personnel records. State and federal overlays (ADEA, Title VII, ADA, FLSA) frequently extend TSLAC minimums.

Typical records:

- Personnel files (Item 1200-04): term of employment + 7 years
- Recruitment and selection records (Item 1200-12): AC + 2 years (3 years for federal contractors under OFCCP)
- Workers' compensation files (Item 1200-22): AC + 5 years; longer if open
- Payroll records (Item 1200-30 and following): retention varies by record type, generally 3 to 5 years after the fiscal year end
- Training records (Item 1200-40): term of employment + 5 years
- Benefits administration: term of employment + 5 years; pension records LIFE

## FN — Financial Records

Covers accounts payable, accounts receivable, general ledger, journals, and the financial trail behind every disbursement.

Typical records:

- Annual financial reports and CAFR (Item 1250-09): PERM
- General ledger (Item 1250-02): FE + 10 years
- Vouchers and supporting documentation (Item 1250-14): FE + 5 years (federal grant overlay may extend)
- Bank statements and reconciliations (Item 1250-20): FE + 5 years
- Bid and procurement records (Item 1250-32): AC + 5 years
- Federal grant files: closeout + 3 years per 2 C.F.R. § 200.334 (often longer than TSLAC)

## TX — Tax Records

Covers property tax, sales tax, and other revenue collection.

Typical records:

- Tax rolls (Item 1275-01): PERM
- Tax statements (Item 1275-08): FE + 5 years
- Delinquent tax records (Item 1275-12): AC + 5 years from final collection or write-off
- Tax abatement agreements (Item 1275-22): LIFE of the agreement + 7 years

## UT — Utility Records

Covers water, wastewater, electric, gas, and stormwater operations.

Typical records:

- Customer utility billing records (Item 1325-04): AC + 5 years from account closure
- Customer service correspondence (Item 1325-12): AC + 2 years
- Meter reading records (Item 1325-08): FE + 3 years
- Service connection and disconnect records: AC + 5 years
- Operator certifications and licensing: LIFE of certification + 5 years

## PW — Public Works

Covers streets, drainage, facilities, fleet, and capital construction.

Typical records:

- Capital improvement project files (Item 1350-04): LIFE of the asset (often PERM as part of the asset record)
- Engineering drawings and as-builts (Item 1350-12): PERM for as-builts of city-owned assets
- Maintenance logs and work orders (Item 1350-20): FE + 3 years
- Pavement condition and traffic studies (Item 1350-30): superseded + 5 years
- Equipment test and inspection reports: LIFE of the equipment + 3 years
- Service contracts: AC + 4 years from completion of the contract

## Reading TSLAC retention formulas

| Code | Meaning |
|---|---|
| AC | After the closing, completion, or termination of the matter |
| FE | After the end of the fiscal year |
| US | After the record becomes useless or obsolete (defined by the city) |
| LIFE | For the life of the underlying asset, contract, license, etc. |
| PERM | Permanent retention |
| MO | Months |
| Y | Years |

Always interpret a retention period as a minimum, not a ceiling. The city may retain longer if doing so serves an administrative, legal, or fiscal purpose.

## When the schedule does not fit

If a record does not map cleanly to any TSLAC item, the records management officer may classify it under the closest analogous item with a brief rationale. The decision should be documented in the disposition log. Persistent gaps should be reported to TSLAC for consideration in the next schedule revision.
