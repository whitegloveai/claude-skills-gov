# TPIA Response Drafter

> Drafts a Texas Public Information Act response letter to a requester. Built for city secretaries, deputy clerks, and public information officers working a request under the 10-business-day clock.

## When to Use

- "I got a TPIA request this morning. Draft the acknowledgment letter."
- "We need to refer this to the AG. Draft the requester response and the § 552.301 referral language."
- "The request is way too broad. Draft a letter offering scope clarification before the clock runs."
- "We're producing records with redactions. Draft the cover letter that lists the exceptions claimed."
- "We don't have any responsive records. Draft a no-records response."
- Any request received under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552 where the 10-business-day response window (§ 552.221) is active.

## Inputs Needed

Required:

- Requester name and contact (email, mailing address, phone)
- Date the request was received (this starts the 10-business-day clock under § 552.221)
- Verbatim text of the request
- Scope of responsive records identified by staff
- Response posture: producing in full, producing with redactions, referring to the Attorney General, no responsive records, or seeking clarification

Optional:

- Specific TPIA exceptions claimed (e.g., § 552.101, § 552.107, § 552.117) with brief factual basis
- Cost estimate under § 552.261 (if charges apply and exceed the $40 threshold under § 552.2615)
- Production format and delivery method preferred by requester
- Whether the request involves a third party with proprietary or privacy interests (§ 552.305 notice may apply)
- Any prior correspondence with the requester

If the response posture is unclear or no exceptions have been identified for a withholding, ask before drafting.

## Process

1. Confirm the date received and compute the 10-business-day deadline under § 552.221. Identify which calendar day this falls on, accounting for Texas-recognized holidays.
2. Identify the response posture (full production, partial production with redactions, AG referral, no records, scope clarification). Each posture has a different letter structure.
3. Draft the heading block: city letterhead placeholder, date, requester name and address, reference line containing the request ID and date received.
4. Acknowledge the request in the first paragraph. Restate the request verbatim or by reasonable paraphrase so the record reflects what was understood.
5. State the response posture clearly. If producing, describe the records and delivery method. If withholding in part, list each exception by section number with a short factual basis. If referring to the AG, state that the city is seeking a decision under § 552.301 and that the AG will issue a ruling.
6. For partial redactions, summarize the redaction approach: what categories are redacted, the controlling exception, and that unredacted portions are being released.
7. For cost estimates over $40, include the itemized estimate required by § 552.2615 and the 10-business-day deposit window before the request is treated as withdrawn.
8. Close with appeal rights: the requester may request an Attorney General ruling on any withholding, and the contact information for the city's public information officer.
9. Verify before sending: the deadline calculation, all exception citations, the AG referral letter date if applicable, and whether any third-party notice under § 552.305 is required.

## Output Format

A business letter on city letterhead, approximately three quarters to one and a half pages, structured as follows:

- Heading block: letterhead, date, requester name and address, reference line with PIR number and date received.
- Salutation and acknowledgment paragraph restating the request verbatim or by close paraphrase.
- Response posture paragraph — one of:
  - **Full production:** the city is producing the records by stated method and date.
  - **Partial production with redactions:** itemized list of exceptions, each with a short factual basis (e.g., "Tex. Gov't Code § 552.107(1) — attorney-client privilege").
  - **AG referral:** statement that the city is seeking a ruling under § 552.301 with the referral letter enclosed.
  - **No responsive records:** statement that a reasonable search returned none.
  - **Clarification:** specific question to the requester, with notation that the § 552.221 clock is paused until clarification is received.
- Cost paragraph, when § 552.2615 applies: itemized estimate and 10-business-day deposit window.
- Closing paragraph: appeal rights to the AG and contact for the city's public information officer.
- Signature block.

Tone: factual, neutral, civically professional. No editorializing about the request, the requester, or the records. Cite statutes by full code prefix. See `examples/email-records-request-response.md` for the complete letter form.

## TRAIGA & Compliance Notes

**AI use disclosure — transparency best practice (not a § 552.051 trigger):** Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.051(b)–(d) requires disclosure when a citizen *interacts directly with an AI system* (e.g., a chatbot, automated voice assistant, or AI-driven service interface). The disclosure trigger is *interaction*, not *output*. This skill produces a static document drafted with AI assistance; it does not put a citizen in direct interaction with AI, so § 552.051 is not engaged.

However, transparency about AI assistance in document drafting is sound practice and should align with the city's adopted AI use policy and the GovAI Coalition's guidance on documenting AI tool use. Consider a brief notation on documents distributed publicly or to the governing body: *"AI-assisted draft, reviewed by [staff title and name]."* This is a policy choice, not a statutory mandate.

**Texas Public Information Act (Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552):**

- § 552.221 — Response within 10 business days; certify reason and state a date certain if production exceeds the window.
- § 552.222 — Clarification requests pause the clock.
- § 552.261 / § 552.2615 — Cost rules; estimates over $40 require itemized estimate and a 10-business-day deposit window.
- § 552.301 / § 552.302 — AG referral within 10 business days; legal brief within 15.
- § 552.305 — Third-party notice when production may implicate third-party proprietary or privacy interests.

**Retention:** TPIA correspondence and the city's working file on a request retain three years after the request is closed per Texas State Library Local Government Retention Schedule GR (General Records), Item 1025-01 (Public Information Act Requests). The released records themselves retain per their underlying classification.

**Verify before sending:** Deadline calculation under § 552.221, all exception citations, any third-party notice obligations under § 552.305, and the city attorney's approval on any AG referral.
