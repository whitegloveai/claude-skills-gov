# TPIA Exception Taxonomy

A working catalog of the principal exceptions to required disclosure under the Texas Public Information Act, Tex. Gov't Code §§ 552.101–552.155. The list below is organized by the category staff most often encounter when classifying records for production. For each exception, the form citation language matches the Attorney General's open records letter ruling style and is suitable for use in a TPIA response letter.

This taxonomy is a drafting aid, not legal advice. Every withholding should be reviewed by the city attorney before release of the response.

## Category 1 — Confidential by other law

- **§ 552.101 — Information confidential by law.** Catches information made confidential by federal statute, state statute, judicial decision, or constitutional provision. Most common downstream uses: HIPAA-protected medical records, CJIS-protected criminal history, FERPA student records, federal grant compliance information.
  - Form: *"The attached records contain information made confidential by law and excepted from disclosure under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.101, specifically [cite the underlying law]."*

- **§ 552.108 — Law enforcement and prosecutorial information.** Active investigations, internal affairs, prosecutor work product, settlement information related to pending matters.
  - Form: *"Records relating to a pending criminal investigation are excepted under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.108(a)."*

## Category 2 — Privacy and personal information

- **§ 552.117 — Personal information of public employees, officials, and certain others.** Home address, home telephone, social security number, family member information, and emergency contact information of current and former employees, officials, peace officers, judges, and certain other categories. Election to withhold must have been made by the individual.
  - Form: *"Personal information of [category] is excepted under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.117(a)(1)."*

- **§ 552.130 — Motor vehicle records.** Driver's license numbers, license plate information, and Texas identification card numbers.
  - Form: *"Motor vehicle record information is confidential under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.130."*

- **§ 552.136 — Credit card, debit card, charge card, and access device numbers.** Categorical confidentiality, no balancing test, no requester challenge available.
  - Form: *"Payment card and access device numbers are confidential under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.136."*

- **§ 552.137 — Email addresses provided by members of the public.** Confidential unless the person consents to release or the address is provided in connection with a license or permit.
  - Form: *"Email addresses provided by members of the public to the City are confidential under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.137."*

- **§ 552.147 — Social security numbers.** Confidential without need for an open records decision.
  - Form: *"Social security numbers are confidential under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.147."*

## Category 3 — Deliberative process and intra-agency communications

- **§ 552.111 — Agency memoranda.** Internal advisory, deliberative, or pre-decisional opinions of staff. The exception protects the decision-making process, not the underlying facts.
  - Form: *"Internal deliberative communications between staff regarding [matter] are excepted under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.111. Factual portions, where reasonably segregable, are being released."*

## Category 4 — Attorney-client and litigation

- **§ 552.103 — Litigation and settlement negotiations.** Records related to pending or reasonably anticipated litigation to which the governmental body is a party.
  - Form: *"Records relating to litigation styled [cause] to which the City is a party are excepted under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.103."*

- **§ 552.107(1) — Attorney-client privilege.** Communications between the city and the city attorney for the purpose of providing or receiving legal advice.
  - Form: *"Communications subject to the attorney-client privilege are excepted under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.107(1)."*

## Category 5 — Competitive and procurement information

- **§ 552.104 — Information related to competition or bidding.** Bid responses before opening, evaluation worksheets during a live procurement.
  - Form: *"Information that would give advantage to a competitor or bidder during a pending procurement is excepted under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.104."*

- **§ 552.110 — Trade secrets and certain commercial or financial information of third parties.** Triggers § 552.305 third-party notice. The third party has 10 business days to assert and substantiate the claim.
  - Form: *"Information claimed by a third party as a trade secret has been referred to the third party for response under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.305."*

## Category 6 — Security and infrastructure

- **§ 552.139 — Government information related to security or infrastructure.** Network architecture, security plans, vulnerability assessments.
  - Form: *"Government information related to security or infrastructure is excepted under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.139."*

## Category 7 — Real property and economic development

- **§ 552.105 — Information relating to location or price of property.** Records concerning real property the city is considering for acquisition or has not yet finalized.
  - Form: *"Information concerning real property under consideration for acquisition is excepted under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.105."*

- **§ 552.131 — Economic development negotiations.** Pre-incentive negotiation records that would harm the city's negotiating position. Often used in conjunction with executive session under TOMA § 551.087.
  - Form: *"Information relating to economic development negotiations is excepted under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.131."*

## Mandatory AG referral framework

Whenever the city wishes to withhold information that is not categorically confidential by statute (e.g., § 552.117, § 552.130, § 552.136, § 552.137, § 552.147), the city must request a ruling from the Office of the Attorney General within 10 business days under § 552.301, with legal arguments filed within 15 business days under § 552.302. Failure to meet either deadline waives the exception under § 552.302(a) and results in a presumption of openness.

## Third-party notice framework (§ 552.305)

When records may implicate trade secrets, proprietary information, or privacy interests of a third party, the city sends written notice to the third party within 10 business days. The third party then has 10 business days to file its own arguments with the AG. The city's burden is to deliver the notice; the substantive privilege is asserted by the third party.

|---|---|---|

Verify all statutory citations against current law before sending. The TPIA is amended frequently and section numbers occasionally shift.
