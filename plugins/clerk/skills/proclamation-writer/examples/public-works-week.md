# Example: Public Works Appreciation Week Proclamation — City of Cedar Ridge

This is a worked example for the proclamation-writer skill. The fictional City of Cedar Ridge, TX recognizes Public Works Appreciation Week annually in May. Cedar Ridge's Public Works Director, Patel, has requested a proclamation for the May 19, 2026 regular council meeting. The proclamation will be read aloud by Mayor Castillo and presented to the public works staff.

## Input

- **Entity:** City of Cedar Ridge, TX (home-rule, council-manager, population approximately 32,000)
- **Occasion:** Public Works Appreciation Week (third full week of May, observed nationally under the American Public Works Association calendar)
- **Signing authority:** Mayor Castillo
- **Presentation date:** Tuesday, May 19, 2026, at the regular council meeting
- **Recipient:** Cedar Ridge Public Works Department, approximately 64 staff led by Director Patel
- **Local connection facts provided by Director Patel:**
  - 1,847 work orders completed in calendar year 2025
  - 22 miles of street overlay completed in calendar year 2025
  - Continuous operation during the February 2024 ice storm, including 96 hours of round-the-clock sanding and traffic-management response
  - Eight active certifications across the department (Class A wastewater, Class B water, traffic-signal technician, and others)
- **City Secretary attesting:** Helen Marquez (still in office at proclamation date in May 2026)

## Output

```
CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE
OFFICE OF THE MAYOR
PROCLAMATION

PUBLIC WORKS APPRECIATION WEEK
MAY 18 THROUGH MAY 24, 2026

WHEREAS, the third full week of May has been recognized since 1960 as
National Public Works Week by the American Public Works Association,
calling on communities across North America to acknowledge the work of
public works professionals who design, build, operate, and maintain the
infrastructure on which daily life depends; and

WHEREAS, the Public Works Department of the City of Cedar Ridge,
Texas, operates and maintains the streets, drainage system, water and
wastewater utilities, fleet, traffic-control infrastructure, and city
facilities that serve approximately 32,000 residents and businesses
every day; and

WHEREAS, during calendar year 2025 the department completed 1,847 work
orders and resurfaced 22 miles of city streets, restoring pavement
condition across neighborhoods on the city's adopted five-year overlay
plan; and

WHEREAS, during the February 2024 winter storm event, public works
crews provided ninety-six consecutive hours of sanding, traffic-signal
management, and emergency response that kept primary routes open and
allowed first responders and medical personnel to reach those who
needed them; and

WHEREAS, the men and women of the Cedar Ridge Public Works Department
hold eight active state and professional certifications, including
Class A wastewater treatment, Class B water distribution, and traffic-
signal technician credentials, reflecting a sustained commitment to
professional standards and to the safety of the residents they serve;
and

WHEREAS, the contributions of the public works workforce are often
performed before sunrise, after sunset, or in conditions that no
resident would choose, and they are rarely visible until something
they have built or maintained is no longer working;

NOW, THEREFORE, I, Elena Castillo, Mayor of the City of Cedar Ridge,
Texas, do hereby proclaim May 18 through May 24, 2026, as PUBLIC WORKS
APPRECIATION WEEK in the City of Cedar Ridge, Texas, and call upon all
residents to recognize the contributions of the public works staff who
serve our community and to extend their thanks to a public works
professional whose work has touched their life.

PROCLAIMED this 19th day of May, 2026.

____________________________________
Elena Castillo
Mayor, City of Cedar Ridge, Texas

ATTEST:

____________________________________
Helen Marquez
City Secretary

[OFFICIAL SEAL OF THE CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE]

This proclamation was drafted with the assistance of artificial
intelligence tools and reviewed by the City Secretary before
presentation.
```

## Notes for the User

- Total length is approximately 320 words and reads aloud in about ninety seconds, well within the meeting flow.
- The proclamation pairs the national context (American Public Works Association, since 1960) with three specific Cedar Ridge facts: the work order count, the overlay mileage, and the ice storm response. The combination keeps the document grounded in local reality without overloading the listener with statistics.
- Director Patel's name does not appear in the operative paragraph. The proclamation recognizes the department as a body, which is the convention for occupational recognitions. A separate framed copy can be presented to Director Patel for departmental display.
- The proclamation date (May 19, 2026) is the date the document is signed and presented; the recognition period (May 18 through May 24, 2026) is the third full calendar week of May 2026, matching the national observance.
- If the council wishes to present a copy to the American Public Works Association Texas Chapter, the City Secretary's office should maintain the signed original in the permanent proclamation book and produce a certified copy for the recipient.
- Continuity: Director Patel and Mayor Castillo also appear in the agenda-builder and open-meetings-compliance-checker examples. Helen Marquez's signature on this May 2026 proclamation is appropriate; by the time of the June 9, 2026 agenda she has retired and Marisol Tovar has been sworn in.
