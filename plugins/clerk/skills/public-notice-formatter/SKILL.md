# Public Notice Formatter

> Formats a public notice for newspaper publication or website posting under the controlling Texas statute. Built for city secretaries handling the rolling calendar of notices that anchor the budget, tax, zoning, and procurement cycles.

## When to Use

- "Format the tax rate hearing notice for the proposed FY2026 rate."
- "We need the budget hearing notice published. Build it."
- "Format the public hearing notice for the rezoning case Z-2026-04."
- "Draft a notice of intent to enter into a debt obligation under § 1431.009."
- "We need a notice for the published bid solicitation under Ch. 252."
- Any required public notice with a controlling statute that prescribes content, format, or publication deadlines.

## Inputs Needed

Required:

- Notice type: budget hearing, tax rate hearing, zoning hearing, bid solicitation, debt issuance, request for proposals, or other statutorily required notice
- Controlling statute (the user provides the cite or the skill identifies it from the notice type)
- Date, time, and location of the hearing or action being noticed
- Required content per statute: financial information for tax rate notice, ballot language for debt notice, scope of work for bid notice, etc.
- Publication channel: newspaper of general circulation, city website, bulletin board, or all three
- Posting deadline relative to the hearing or action date

Optional:

- Newspaper name and submission requirements
- Spanish or other language version
- Contact for accommodations under HAVA or ADA
- Any required taxpayer-rights or protest-rights language (tax rate notices have a specific protest paragraph; zoning hearings have a notice-of-rights paragraph)

If the notice type does not map to a clear controlling statute, ask. Each notice type has a distinct statutory frame and a distinct cadence.

## Process

1. Identify the controlling statute for the notice type. See the statute table in Compliance Notes below for budget, tax rate, zoning, bid, debt, RFP/RFQ, and plat notices.
2. Compute publication and posting deadlines from the hearing or action date back. State the deadline explicitly in the notice so staff can verify the publication schedule.
3. Draft the notice with the statutorily prescribed content. For tax rate and budget notices, content is heavily prescribed — reproduce verbatim. For zoning notices, include the case number, property description, proposed change, hearing date and place, and right of interested persons to appear.
4. Format for the publication channel. Newspaper notices use a tight single-column body for legal classifieds; bulletin and website postings use a more readable layout with section headers.
5. Add the city secretary verification block: posting date and time, name, and the statutory citation for the posting.
6. Flag required ancillary actions: mailed notice, certified-mail postings, courthouse postings, or internet posting if the city maintains a website.

## Output Format

A single-document notice with the controlling statute named at the top, statutorily prescribed content in the required order, and a verification block at the bottom. Structure:

- "PUBLIC NOTICE / [NOTICE TITLE]" header
- Controlling statute line and posting deadline computation
- "NOTICE IS HEREBY GIVEN" opening identifying the governing body, action, date, time, and location
- Statutorily prescribed content (rates and homestead impact for tax rate; budget figures for budget; case number and property description for zoning; bid scope for procurement)
- Required rights paragraph (taxpayer protest, appearance rights, etc.)
- Contact for questions and accessibility accommodations
- City Secretary signature line and posting verification block citing the controlling statute
- Optional Spanish version below

See `examples/tax-rate-hearing-notice.md` for the complete tax-rate form.

Tone: formal, statutory, exact. For prescribed notices (tax rate, budget, debt), reproduce statutory language verbatim. For non-prescribed notices, use plain civic language without editorializing.

## TRAIGA & Compliance Notes

**AI use disclosure — transparency best practice (not a § 552.051 trigger):** Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.051(b)–(d) requires disclosure when a citizen *interacts directly with an AI system* (e.g., a chatbot, automated voice assistant, or AI-driven service interface). The disclosure trigger is *interaction*, not *output*. This skill produces a static document drafted with AI assistance; it does not put a citizen in direct interaction with AI, so § 552.051 is not engaged.

However, transparency about AI assistance in document drafting is sound practice and should align with the city's adopted AI use policy and the GovAI Coalition's guidance on documenting AI tool use. Consider a brief notation on documents distributed publicly or to the governing body: *"AI-assisted draft, reviewed by [staff title and name]."* This is a policy choice, not a statutory mandate.

**Controlling statutes by notice type:**

- **Budget hearing:** Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 102.0065 — newspaper publication at least 10 days before; notice states date, time, place, and the taxpayer's right to attend and participate.
- **Tax rate hearing:** Tex. Tax Code § 26.05 (adoption), § 26.06 (notice on increase), § 26.061 (notice when proposed rate does not exceed the lower of the no-new-revenue or voter-approval rate). Notice includes no-new-revenue rate, voter-approval rate, proposed rate, dollars-and-cents homestead impact, and hearing details. Specific protest language is required when the rate exceeds the voter-approval rate.
- **Zoning hearing:** Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 211.006 (P&Z commission); § 211.007 (mailed notice to property owners within 200 feet at least 10 days before the governing body's hearing; newspaper publication at least 15 days before).
- **Bid solicitations:** Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 252.041 — newspaper publication once a week for two consecutive weeks before the bid opening, first publication at least 14 days before.
- **Debt obligations:** Tex. Gov't Code § 1431.009 — notice of intent to issue certificates of obligation published at least 45 days before issuance.

**Retention:** Public notices and the publisher's affidavit of publication retain permanently under TSLAC Schedule GR, Item 1000-26 (Meeting and hearing notices) or the analogous local item. The publisher's affidavit is the load-bearing proof of publication if compliance is later challenged.

**Bilingual notices:** Notices to be read by a Spanish-speaking audience should be prepared in Spanish alongside the English version even when not statutorily required, as a matter of inclusive practice in covered jurisdictions.

**Verify before posting:** The statutory deadline computation, the prescribed content for tax and budget notices (verbatim from statute), the newspaper publication schedule, and the publisher's affidavit return after publication.
