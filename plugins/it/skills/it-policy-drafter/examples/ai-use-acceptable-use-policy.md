# CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE, TEXAS
## GENERATIVE ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY
**Policy No. IT-014**
**Effective: January 1, 2026**
**Adopted by: City Council, Resolution No. R-2025-58**
**Review cycle: Annual**

### 1. PURPOSE

This policy governs the appropriate use of generative artificial intelligence ("generative AI") tools by City of Cedar Ridge personnel. It establishes the boundary between permitted productivity uses and uses that present unacceptable risk to public records integrity, citizen privacy, or the City's compliance posture under the Texas Responsible Artificial Intelligence Governance Act (TRAIGA), Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code Ch. 552.

### 2. SCOPE

This policy applies to all City employees, elected officials, appointed board and commission members, contractors, interns, and volunteers ("Users") who access generative AI tools — whether public (e.g., ChatGPT, Claude.ai, Gemini, Copilot consumer tier), enterprise-licensed (e.g., Microsoft 365 Copilot, ChatGPT Enterprise, Claude for Enterprise), or embedded in third-party SaaS — in connection with City business. It covers all City data and any communication or work product created with generative AI assistance.

### 3. DEFINITIONS

- **Generative AI** — An AI system that produces synthetic content (text, code, images, audio, video) in response to a prompt.
- **Prompt** — Any input submitted to a generative AI tool.
- **Output** — Any content returned by a generative AI tool.
- **Public Records** — Information collected, assembled, or maintained by or for the City in connection with the transaction of official business under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552.
- **Sensitive Information** — Information classified as Confidential or Restricted under the City's Data Classification Policy (IT-006), including PII, PHI, CJIS data, financial account data, and information protected from public disclosure under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552.
- **Approved Tool** — A generative AI tool that has been reviewed under the City's AI Governance Intake (IT-015) and authorized by the CIO.

### 4. POLICY STATEMENT

4.1 **Approved tools only.** Users shall use only Approved Tools listed on the IT Department's intranet for City business involving non-public data. — *NIST 800-53 CM-7, AC-22*

4.2 **Sensitive information prohibition.** Users shall not enter Sensitive Information into any consumer or non-Approved generative AI tool. Enterprise-licensed tools that meet the City's data-handling requirements may accept Confidential information; Restricted information (CJIS, PHI, criminal investigation records) requires written CIO authorization. — *NIST 800-53 AC-3, SC-28*

4.3 **AI literacy training.** Users with access to Approved Tools shall complete the City's AI literacy training within 30 days of access provisioning and annually thereafter, consistent with Tex. HB 3512. — *NIST 800-53 AT-2, AT-3*

4.4 **Human review required.** Outputs used in any City decision, communication to the public, or document filed in a public record shall be reviewed and verified by a qualified human reviewer before use. Users are responsible for the accuracy of outputs they adopt. — *NIST AI RMF MS-1.3, MG-1.1*

4.5 **Citizen-facing AI interaction disclosure.** Any deployment of generative AI that places a citizen in direct interaction with an AI system — including chatbots, voice assistants, automated triage interfaces, and AI-driven self-service portals — shall include a clear, conspicuous disclosure that the citizen is interacting with AI, consistent with Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.051(b)–(d). Deployment requires prior AI Governance Intake review. — *TRAIGA § 552.051*

4.6 **Public records compliance.** Prompts and outputs created in the course of City business are public records subject to Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552. Users shall not enter prompts they would be unwilling to disclose in response to a TPIA request and shall preserve outputs in the appropriate record system. — *Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552*

4.7 **Retention.** Prompts and outputs incorporated into a City decision or work product shall be retained in accordance with the Local Government Retention Schedule applicable to the resulting record. Standalone chat logs in Approved Tools retain per IT-017 (Logging and Monitoring Retention). — *TSLAC retention schedules*

4.8 **Prohibited uses.** Users shall not use generative AI to: (a) make a final decision that adversely affects a citizen's rights, benefits, or legal status; (b) generate content that violates TRAIGA's prohibited uses (Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.101–§ 552.108); (c) impersonate another person; (d) produce deepfake content; (e) circumvent the City's records or open meetings obligations. — *TRAIGA Subch. C; NIST AI RMF GV-1.1*

4.9 **Intellectual property and confidentiality.** Users shall not submit proprietary code, vendor confidential information, or content subject to a non-disclosure agreement to any tool that has not been confirmed as compliant with the underlying agreement. — *NIST 800-53 PM-30*

4.10 **Attribution.** Documents drafted with substantial generative AI assistance and distributed externally or to the governing body should carry a footer notation: *"AI-assisted draft, reviewed by [staff title and name]."* This is a transparency best practice consistent with GovAI Coalition guidance; it is not the § 552.051 disclosure trigger.

### 5. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Chief Information Officer | Owns this policy. Approves tools. Approves exceptions. |
| AI Governance Lead (CIO designee) | Operates the AI Governance Intake. Maintains the Approved Tools list. |
| Department Directors | Ensure department staff complete required training and comply with this policy. |
| Privacy Officer | Reviews use cases involving PII or PHI. |
| Records Management Officer | Ensures prompts and outputs are retained per TSLAC schedules. |
| End Users | Comply with this policy. Complete training. Report misuse and incidents. |

### 6. COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT

Violations of this policy may result in discipline up to and including termination, consistent with the City's Personnel Manual and progressive discipline policy. Contractor violations may result in contract action up to termination. Suspected criminal conduct shall be referred to the City Attorney.

### 7. EXCEPTIONS

Written exception requests shall be submitted to the CIO. Approved exceptions expire 12 months from issuance and require renewal.

**Drafting notes (not part of the adopted policy):**

This policy operates against the substantial-compliance safe harbor under Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.057 by aligning with NIST AI RMF 1.0 and the NIST Generative AI Profile. The City of Cedar Ridge's Skill Library — including the council-ops `agenda-builder` skill, the finance `budget-narrative-writer` skill, and the planning `staff-report-writer` skill — are Approved Tools because they use Approved enterprise AI under controlled workflows and produce static documents that pass through human review before reaching the public or the governing body. None of those skills place a citizen in direct AI interaction, so § 552.051 disclosure is not engaged at the document level.
