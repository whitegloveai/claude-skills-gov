# Example — Cedar Ridge Boil-Water Advisory Package, Pecan Pressure Zone

> On May 9, 2026 at 4:47 AM, a 12-inch main on Pecan Avenue between Magnolia and Live Oak failed during a high-flow event, dropping pressure across the Pecan Pressure Zone (approximately 2,300 service connections) below 20 psi. Under 30 TAC § 290.46(q), a boil-water notice is required. The Public Works Director, Renata Hsu, authorized issuance of this package at 6:15 AM. The notice was distributed by CodeRED, the city website, social media, and door hangers in the affected zone. This is the same incident referenced in `council-ops/agenda-builder/examples/regular-meeting-cedar-ridge.md` (the emergency item on the May 12 special meeting agenda) and in `communications/crisis-comms-template`.

PWS ID: TX0250012. Operator of record: Renata Hsu, P.E., Class B Surface Water Operator.

EMERGENCY NOTICE — BOIL-WATER ADVISORY
CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE PUBLIC WATER SYSTEM (PWS ID TX0250012)
Issued: May 9, 2026, 6:15 AM CDT
Status: Active
Authorized by: Renata Hsu, Public Works Director / Class B Operator

==============================================================
1. LONG-FORM NOTICE (English)
==============================================================

BOIL-WATER NOTICE
Pecan Pressure Zone — City of Cedar Ridge

Due to a 12-inch water main failure on Pecan Avenue near Live Oak,
the Cedar Ridge Public Water System (PWS ID TX0250012) is advising
all customers in the Pecan Pressure Zone to boil all tap water used
for drinking, cooking, making ice, brushing teeth, or washing dishes.
Bring water to a vigorous rolling boil and continue boiling for at
least two (2) minutes. In lieu of boiling, customers may purchase
bottled water or obtain water from another suitable source.

WHO IS AFFECTED
Residents and businesses bounded by Cypress Avenue (north),
Highway 90 (south), Industrial Drive (east), and Pecan Creek
(west). Approximately 2,300 service connections. If you are
unsure whether your address is included, call 311 or visit
cedarridgetx.gov/water-advisory.

WHO IS MOST AT RISK
Infants, young children, the elderly, and persons with weakened
immune systems should take particular care. Use bottled or boiled
water for preparing infant formula.

CAUSE
A 12-inch water main on Pecan Avenue near Live Oak failed at
approximately 4:47 AM, causing pressure to drop below 20 psi
across the Pecan Pressure Zone. Loss of pressure can allow
contaminants to enter the distribution system.

WHAT THE CITY IS DOING
Public Works crews are on site. Repairs are expected to be
complete within 24 hours. The city will flush the affected mains
and collect two consecutive bacteriological samples at least 24
hours apart. Once samples confirm the water is safe and TCEQ
clears the system, the city will issue a lift notice through
the same channels as this notice.

DURATION AND NEXT UPDATE
This notice is expected to remain in effect for 48 to 72 hours.
The next update will be released by 6:00 PM today, Friday May 9,
2026.

QUESTIONS
311 (in city) or 940-555-0311. Website:
cedarridgetx.gov/water-advisory. Email: water@cedarridgetx.gov.

Renata Hsu, P.E.
Public Works Director and Class B Surface Water Operator
