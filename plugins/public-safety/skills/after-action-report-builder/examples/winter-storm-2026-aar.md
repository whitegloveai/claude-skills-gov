# Worked Example: Winter Storm 2026 After-Action Report

This is a fictional AAR for the Cedar Ridge response to a regional winter storm that struck January 14–17, 2026. The incident involved freezing rain transitioning to ice, multi-day sub-freezing temperatures, regional power instability, and water-system stress. Mutual aid was requested from Caddo County and coordinated through TDEM. The AAR was prepared by Cedar Ridge OEM and distributed to council in February 2026.

---

```
AFTER-ACTION REPORT
Cedar Ridge Winter Storm Response
January 14–17, 2026
Issued by: Cedar Ridge Office of Emergency Management
Date of report: February 24, 2026

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
On January 14, 2026, a winter storm brought freezing rain followed
by three days of sub-freezing temperatures to Cedar Ridge. The
city activated the EOC at 1400 on January 13, opened two warming
centers, coordinated with Oncor on rolling power outages, and
worked with the Cedar Ridge Public Utility on a localized boil-
water advisory for the south service area following a pressure
loss event on January 16. Three weather-related injuries were
reported; no fatalities. The response demonstrated strong inter-
agency coordination at the command level and substantial community
engagement through faith-based and nonprofit partners. Four
specific areas warrant improvement: warming center activation
timing relative to forecast, public communications cadence
during overnight hours, mutual aid request workflow, and post-
storm debris removal coordination with Public Works.

INCIDENT OVERVIEW
- Name: Winter Storm Response, January 2026
- Type: Real-world incident
- Date(s): January 13–18, 2026 (EOC activation through deactivation)
- Location: City of Cedar Ridge and unincorporated Caddo County
  service area
- Hazard: Winter weather — freezing rain, hard freeze, derived
  consequences (power, water, transportation)

TIMELINE OF EVENTS (abridged; full timeline in Appendix A)
Jan 13, 0900 — National Weather Service issues Winter Storm Warning
Jan 13, 1400 — EOC activated to Level 2 by OEM Coordinator
Jan 13, 1830 — City Manager briefs Council via email; warming
              centers staffed and stocked
Jan 14, 0220 — First freezing rain reported at FD Station 2
Jan 14, 0530 — PD reports first multi-vehicle slide on US-380
Jan 14, 0900 — Warming Center A (Community Center) opens
Jan 14, 1100 — Warming Center B (Library branch) opens
Jan 14, 1630 — Mayor approves local disaster declaration; signed
              and transmitted to Caddo County and TDEM
Jan 15, 0445 — Oncor begins rotating outages; OEM coordinates
              priority restoration for warming centers
Jan 16, 0800 — Pressure-loss event in south service area; CRPU
              issues boil-water advisory in coordination with
              CRPD PIO, CRFD, and OEM
Jan 16, 1400 — Caddo County emergency declaration approved;
              joint EOC stand-up with Caddo OEM
Jan 17, 1130 — Roads passable on primary routes; Public Works
              shifts to ice-melt and debris assessment
Jan 17, 1900 — Warming centers reduced from 24-hour to daytime
Jan 18, 1500 — Boil-water advisory lifted after sample clearance
Jan 18, 1730 — EOC deactivated

PARTICIPATING ORGANIZATIONS
- City of Cedar Ridge: OEM, CRPD, CRFD, Public Works, CRPU,
  Communications, Parks (warming centers), Library
- Caddo County: OEM, Sheriff's Office, Road and Bridge
- State: TDEM District Coordinator, TxDOT District, TCEQ Region
- Federal: NWS forecast support
- Mutual aid and NGO: American Red Cross North Texas, two
  faith-based partners (First United Methodist Cedar Ridge and
  Iglesia Cristiana Nueva Vida), Caddo County Volunteer Fire
- Utilities: Oncor, Atmos Energy

COMMAND STRUCTURE
- Incident Commander: Wendell Briggs, Fire Chief, CRFD
- Operations Section Chief: Anika Patel, Lt., CRPD (transitioned
  to Asst. Chief Reyes, CRFD, on Jan 16)
- Planning Section Chief: OEM Deputy
- Logistics Section Chief: Public Works Operations Manager
- Finance/Administration Section Chief: Asst. Finance Director
- Public Information Officer: City Communications Director,
  supported by CRPD PIO and CRFD PIO; JIC stood up Jan 16 with
  Caddo County
- Safety Officer: CRFD Battalion Chief
- Liaison Officer: OEM Coordinator
- Unified Command from Jan 16 onward with Caddo County OEM

CORE CAPABILITIES EXERCISED
- Public Information and Warning
- Operational Coordination
- Mass Care Services
- Critical Transportation
- Operational Communications
- Public Health, Healthcare, and EMS
- Situational Assessment
- Infrastructure Systems

OBSERVATIONS

Core Capability: Public Information and Warning
Strengths:
- The bilingual outreach through Iglesia Cristiana Nueva Vida
  reached an estimated 600 Spanish-speaking residents who would
  otherwise have missed the boil-water advisory (Jan 16, 1000).
- The Joint Information Center stood up cleanly with Caddo
  County on Jan 16.
Areas for Improvement:
- Overnight communications cadence was thin between 2200 and
  0500 on Jan 14 and Jan 15. Residents reported confusion about
  warming center hours.
Recommendations:
- Establish a written overnight comms cadence (one update per
  4-hour block at minimum) in the Crisis Comms Annex.
  Owner: Communications Director. Target: April 30, 2026.

Core Capability: Mass Care Services
Strengths:
- Two warming centers opened within the forecast window;
  combined nightly capacity peaked at 218 residents (Jan 15).
- Red Cross cot deployment was on site within 90 minutes of
  request.
Areas for Improvement:
- Warming Center A opened at 0900 on Jan 14 although freezing
  rain began at 0220. Eight residents were transported by
  ambulance for cold exposure between 0300 and 0900.
Recommendations:
- Revise the warming-center activation trigger to "open four
  hours before forecast onset of freezing precipitation," and
  build a 24/7 staffing roster.
  Owner: Parks Director (facilities) and OEM Coordinator
  (trigger). Target: May 15, 2026.

Core Capability: Operational Coordination
Strengths:
- The mayoral disaster declaration was signed and transmitted on
  Day 2, well within the 7-day requirement under Tex. Health &
  Safety Code Ch. 418.
Areas for Improvement:
- The mutual aid request to Caddo County went through three
  different points of contact before reaching the correct
  liaison. Approximately 90 minutes were lost.
Recommendations:
- Update the Mutual Aid Annex with a single named point of
  contact at Caddo County OEM and a backup, and rehearse the
  request workflow at the next joint tabletop.
  Owner: OEM Coordinator. Target: June 30, 2026.

Core Capability: Infrastructure Systems
Strengths:
- The pressure-loss event was identified within 12 minutes of
  the SCADA alarm and the boil-water advisory was issued within
  the TCEQ Public Notification Rule window under 30 TAC §
  290.46(q).
Areas for Improvement:
- Post-storm debris removal coordination with Public Works
  lagged the operational tempo of the response. Three council
  members received resident complaints about downed limbs
  during the response.
Recommendations:
- Integrate Public Works debris assessment into the EOC
  Planning P starting at Day 2 of any multi-day weather event,
  with a daily debris-status briefing.
  Owner: Public Works Director. Target: April 15, 2026.

LESSONS LEARNED
- Activation timing for mass care must lead the forecast, not
  follow it. Cost of an empty warming center for four hours is
  a fraction of the cost of an avoidable ambulance transport.
- Communications cadence is a 24-hour discipline, not an
  8-hour shift.
- Mutual aid contact lists must be verified quarterly, not
  exercised once a year.
- Debris is part of the response, not a post-incident
  afterthought.

ACKNOWLEDGMENTS
Cedar Ridge OEM recognizes the personnel of CRPD, CRFD, Public
Works, CRPU, the Library, Parks, Communications, and Finance;
the volunteers of the Red Cross North Texas chapter and our two
faith-based partners; and the leadership of Caddo County OEM,
TDEM District, and Oncor. Specific recognition is due to
Lt. Patel and Asst. Chief Reyes for their Operations leadership.

PREPARED BY: OEM Coordinator
REVIEWED BY: Chief Wendell Briggs, Incident Commander
APPROVED BY: City Manager and Chief Eduardo Vega (CRPD)

AI-assisted draft, reviewed by the OEM Coordinator and the
incident command staff.
```
