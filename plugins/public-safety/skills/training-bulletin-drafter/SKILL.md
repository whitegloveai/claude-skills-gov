# Training Bulletin Drafter

> Drafts a training bulletin formatted for roll-call distribution in a Texas police, fire, or EMS agency. Built for training officers and supervisors who need to move quickly on a new ruling, policy, or lesson learned.

## When to Use

- "We need a training bulletin on the new vehicle search ruling by Friday roll call."
- "Draft a memorandum on the revised use-of-force policy. Sworn distribution."
- "The state changed the Tier II reporting threshold. Write me a bulletin for the captains."
- "Cap pulled a body-worn camera review and we need a training note on activation timing."
- A court ruling, legislative change, or AG opinion has changed the standard of care.
- An after-action review identified a procedural gap that warrants training, not discipline.

## Inputs Needed

Required:

- Training topic and a one-sentence problem statement.
- Controlling authority: statute, case, Texas AG opinion, agency policy, or NFPA standard.
- Learning objective in the form "By the end of roll call, the [audience] will be able to [observable behavior]."
- Target audience: sworn, civilian, specific rank, specific unit, or full agency.
- Distribution timing: next roll call, within seven days, before a specific operation.

Optional:

- Agency bulletin number format (Cedar Ridge PD uses TB-YYYY-### for training bulletins).
- TCOLE / TCFP / DSHS training credit category if credit will be claimed.
- Roll-call discussion questions the training officer wants included.
- Required documentation of receipt (signature sheet, RMS acknowledgment, LMS module).

If controlling authority or the learning objective is missing, ask. A bulletin without an authority citation is unenforceable on review.

## Process

1. Confirm the controlling authority. For peace officers, the authority is usually Tex. Penal Code, Tex. Code Crim. Proc., a binding case, or agency General Order. For firefighters, NFPA standards and Tex. Health & Safety Code Ch. 419. For EMTs, DSHS rules at 25 TAC and the agency medical director's protocols.
2. Confirm the learning objective is observable. "Understand consent" is not observable; "articulate the three elements of valid consent and identify two scenarios where consent is invalid" is.
3. Draft the header block: bulletin number, distribution, effective date, supersedes (if it replaces a prior bulletin), and the controlling authority citation.
4. Draft the Background section: a plain-language summary of the change and why it matters. Two to four paragraphs. Cite the controlling authority inline.
5. Draft the Procedure section: numbered steps describing the required behavior. Each step is observable and verifiable on review.
6. Draft Documentation Requirements: how the officer documents compliance (BWC narrative tag, RMS field, supplemental report).
7. Draft Common Mistakes and Watch-Outs: three to five items pulled from agency experience or the case being responded to.
8. Draft Roll-Call Discussion Points: open-ended questions a supervisor can use to lead a fifteen-minute conversation.
9. Draft the acknowledgment block: signature line, date, badge number, and supervisor's verification.

## Output Format

Plain markdown. One- to three-page document. No emoji. Sober and direct. The audience will read this at 0530 on a roll-call floor with coffee in one hand.

```
[AGENCY NAME]
TRAINING BULLETIN [TB-YYYY-###]
DISTRIBUTION: [Audience]
EFFECTIVE: [Date]
SUPERSEDES: [Prior bulletin, if any]
CONTROLLING AUTHORITY: [Citation]

TITLE: [Short, specific title]

LEARNING OBJECTIVE
By the end of this roll-call training, [audience] will be able to:
1. [Observable behavior]
2. [Observable behavior]

BACKGROUND
[Two to four paragraphs of plain-language context, with the controlling
authority cited inline. State what changed, when it changed, and why it
matters operationally.]

PROCEDURE
1. [Step, observable]
2. [Step, observable]
3. [Step, observable]
...

DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
- [BWC, RMS, supplemental, etc.]

COMMON MISTAKES AND WATCH-OUTS
- [Common error 1]
- [Common error 2]
- [Common error 3]

ROLL-CALL DISCUSSION POINTS
1. [Open-ended question]
2. [Open-ended question]
3. [Scenario, with no scripted answer]

ACKNOWLEDGMENT
I acknowledge I have received and understood this training bulletin.

Name (printed): ______________________
Badge / ID:    ______________________
Date:          ______________________
Signature:     ______________________

Supervisor verification:
Name (printed): ______________________
Date:          ______________________
Signature:     ______________________

Issued by: [Rank, Name, Title]
Approved by: [Chief / Deputy Chief / Training Director]
```

Length: 800 to 2,000 words.

Tone: declarative. Avoid hedges. Officers and firefighters will follow the procedure as written.

## TRAIGA & Compliance Notes

**AI use disclosure — transparency best practice (not a § 552.051 trigger):** Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.051(b)–(d) requires disclosure when a citizen *interacts directly with an AI system* (e.g., a chatbot, automated voice assistant, or AI-driven service interface). The disclosure trigger is *interaction*, not *output*. This skill produces a static document drafted with AI assistance; it does not put a citizen in direct interaction with AI, so § 552.051 is not engaged.

However, transparency about AI assistance in document drafting is sound practice and should align with the city's adopted AI use policy and the GovAI Coalition's guidance on documenting AI tool use. Consider a brief notation on documents distributed publicly or to the governing body: *"AI-assisted draft, reviewed by [staff title and name]."* This is a policy choice, not a statutory mandate.

**TCOLE (peace officers):** Tex. Occ. Code Ch. 1701 and TCOLE Rules at 37 TAC Ch. 215, 217 govern peace officer training. If credit will be claimed, the bulletin must map to a TCOLE course number. Mandated topics include cultural diversity, de-escalation, and use of force.

**TCFP (firefighters):** Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 419 and TCFP rules at 37 TAC Ch. 421–451. Continuing education hours require curriculum approval.

**DSHS / EMS:** Tex. Health & Safety Code Ch. 773 and 25 TAC Ch. 157. Medical director sign-off required for clinical protocol changes.

**CJIS Security Policy:** If the bulletin addresses any handling of CJIS data, the FBI CJIS Security Policy training requirements apply. Acknowledgments must be retained for audit.

**Civil service:** Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 143 jurisdictions require that any procedure with disciplinary consequences be issued under proper authority and documented. Failure to document training receipt undermines a later disciplinary action.

**Public records (Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552):** Training bulletins are public records. Drafts may be exempt under § 552.111 until issued. Acknowledgment signatures are personnel records subject to the Ch. 143 personnel-file rules.

**Retention:** Training bulletins and signed acknowledgments retain per the Texas State Library Local Government Retention Schedule PS, generally three to five years and longer for use-of-force-related training.
