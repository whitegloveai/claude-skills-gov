# Elementary Summer Reading Partnership — Cedar Ridge Public Library and Cedar Ridge ISD

## Input

- Partner organization: Cedar Ridge Independent School District (elementary division)
- Primary contact: Dr. Renata Vasquez, Assistant Superintendent for Elementary Programs
- Initiative: Read Together Cedar Ridge — a coordinated summer reading program across the four Cedar Ridge ISD elementary campuses
- Audience: students entering grades K through 5 at Cedar Ridge ISD elementary campuses (Maple Creek, Oak Grove, Pine Hollow, and Willow Bend)
- Library scope: program design, books and digital access through the library's e-reading platform, library cards for every enrolled student, weekly thematic programming at the main branch
- Partner scope: teacher facilitators for an end-of-summer book club hosted on each campus, school-side promotion and enrollment, transportation to the main branch for two library field days
- Term: two years, beginning May 1, 2026, with a one-year renewal option
- Funds: no funds flow in either direction; all contributions are in-kind
- Signing authority: Library — Imani Brooks, Library Director (with City Manager acknowledgment); District — Dr. Renata Vasquez, by delegation from the Superintendent

## Output

```
PARTNERSHIP PROPOSAL
Cedar Ridge Public Library and Cedar Ridge Independent School District
Read Together Cedar Ridge — Elementary Summer Reading Program
Date: May 9, 2026

INITIATIVE OVERVIEW
Read Together Cedar Ridge is a coordinated summer reading program
serving students entering grades K through 5 at the four Cedar
Ridge ISD elementary campuses. The program is designed to keep
students reading through the summer, to put a library card in the
hand of every elementary student, and to bring the public library
and the district's elementary teachers into a shared summer
practice. The library provides books, digital access, and weekly
programming. The district provides teacher facilitators for an
end-of-summer book club hosted on each campus. The program is
free for all participating families and is offered in English and
Spanish.

BOTH PARTIES' CONTRIBUTIONS AND COMMITMENTS

Library scope (Cedar Ridge Public Library):
- Program design and the Read Together Cedar Ridge reading list
- Library cards for every enrolled student, issued through a
  simplified school-enrollment data exchange
- Print books and digital access through the library's e-reading
  platform
- Weekly thematic programming at the main branch through June and
  July
- Two library field days hosted at the main branch
- Bilingual program materials (English and Spanish)
- Promotion through library channels and the city newsletter

Partner scope (Cedar Ridge ISD):
- Teacher facilitators for an end-of-summer book club hosted on
  each of the four elementary campuses in early August
- School-side promotion and enrollment through teacher
  communications, family folders, and the district's family app
- Transportation by district school bus for the two library field
  days
- Photo and media releases collected through the district's
  standard family enrollment process
- Background-checked teacher staffing for all on-campus sessions

AUDIENCE SERVED
Students entering grades K through 5 at Maple Creek, Oak Grove,
Pine Hollow, and Willow Bend Elementary campuses. The program
serves an estimated 1,650 students. The library and the district
will publish the program in English and Spanish and will provide
accommodations on request.

TERM AND RENEWAL
Effective: May 1, 2026
Through: April 30, 2028
Renewal: Automatic one-year renewal unless either party provides
written notice of non-renewal at least 60 days before the
expiration date.

BRANDING AND RECOGNITION
The program is co-branded as Read Together Cedar Ridge. Both
parties' logos appear on all program materials of equal weight.
The library is credited as the program lead for the reading list,
the e-reading platform, and the field day programming. The
district is credited for the on-campus book club and transportation.
Press releases are jointly approved before issue. Social media
posts tag both parties. Photo and media releases for minors are
collected by the district through its standard family enrollment
process and are honored by the library.

EVALUATION AND REPORTING
Metrics:
- Number of library cards issued under the program
- Number of books and digital titles checked out by enrolled
  students
- Attendance at weekly programming and at the two field days
- Attendance at the end-of-summer book club on each campus
- A short family survey at the end of the program

The library collects checkout and program attendance data. The
district collects book club attendance and family survey responses
through its standard channels. Both parties share aggregate data;
neither party shares student-level data outside the program
without parental consent. A joint year-end report is prepared by
the library and the district's elementary leadership and presented
to the Cedar Ridge Public Library Board and the Cedar Ridge ISD
Board of Trustees each fall.

MOU vs CONTRACT PATHWAY
Recommended instrument: Memorandum of Understanding.
Rationale: no funds flow between the parties; all contributions
are in-kind. Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 252 procurement rules are
not engaged. As a partnership between two governmental units, the
parties may also rely on the Interlocal Cooperation Act, Tex.
Gov't Code Ch. 791, if either party's policy prefers that
instrument. The city attorney and the district's counsel will
confirm.

APPROVAL PATHWAY
Library side: Library Director, with acknowledgment from the City
Manager and report to the Library Board.
District side: Assistant Superintendent for Elementary Programs,
by delegation from the Superintendent, with report to the Board
of Trustees per district policy on outside partnerships.

Signing authority:
Library: Imani Brooks, MLS, Library Director, Cedar Ridge Public
Library
District: Dr. Renata Vasquez, Assistant Superintendent for
Elementary Programs, Cedar Ridge ISD

ATTACHMENTS
- Photo and media release form (district standard form)
- Brand standards summary (each party)
- Read Together Cedar Ridge reading list (library, attached
  separately each program year)

AI-assisted draft, reviewed by Imani Brooks, Library Director.
```
