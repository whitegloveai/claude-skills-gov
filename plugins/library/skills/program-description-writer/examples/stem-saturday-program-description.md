This June, Cedar Ridge Public Library is launching a four-week
introduction to robotics for kids ages 8 to 12. STEM Saturday meets
on the four Saturdays of June — the 7th, 14th, 21st, and 28th — from
10:00 to 11:30 a.m. in the Children's Activity Room.

Over four sessions, children will work in small teams to design,
build, and program their own robots. Week one is design and
construction. Week two introduces sensors and inputs. Week three
adds programming. Week four, in partnership with a teaching artist
from the Cedar Ridge Makerspace, brings the robots together for a
final challenge course. No prior experience is required. All
materials, including the classroom robotics kits loaned by the
Makerspace, are provided by the library.

"STEM Saturday is one of the programs we wait all year for," said
Imani Brooks, Library Director. "Watching a kid figure out for the
first time that a sensor can talk to a motor is exactly what a
public library should be doing in a summer."

Registration opens May 17 at cedarridgelibrary.gov/programs.
Capacity is 16 children per session and registration is required
for each Saturday separately. Drop-offs are welcome; a caregiver
must remain in the library building. A simple parental release is
required for any child who appears in promotional photographs taken
during the program.

This program is offered in partnership with the Cedar Ridge
Makerspace. For questions, contact the Children's Services Desk at
the library or programs@cedarridgelibrary.gov.

ACCESSIBILITY STATEMENT
The Cedar Ridge Public Library is committed to providing access

program.
