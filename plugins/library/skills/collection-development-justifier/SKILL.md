# Collection Development Justifier

> Drafts the written justification a library uses to document an acquisition, a weeding decision, or a response to a reconsideration request. Built for collection development librarians and library directors who must show their work to the public, the library board, and the city.

## When to Use

- "Document why we are acquiring this title for the YA collection."
- "Write the weeding rationale for the 200 cookbooks I pulled from the open stacks last week."
- "We received a reconsideration request on [title]. Draft the response."
- "The library board wants the rationale memo for the audio collection refresh."
- "A patron submitted a formal challenge — I need the response in the format the policy requires."

## Inputs Needed

Required:

- Decision type: acquisition, weeding (deselection), or reconsideration response
- Title or collection segment under review
- The library's controlling collection development policy and reconsideration policy (cite by section)
- Justification factors that apply: relevance to community, circulation history, currency, physical condition, community need, professional reviews, awards, gaps in the collection, intellectual freedom considerations

If the decision is a reconsideration response, also required:

- The challenger's stated concern (verbatim or summarized)
- The format the reconsideration policy requires for the response (memo, letter, board report)
- Whether the request goes to the director, the library board, or both
- The age-range placement of the material and any access controls (e.g., shelving in the young adult section with parental-controlled cards available)

If decision type, controlling policy section, or — for reconsiderations — the challenger's stated concern is missing, ask before drafting.

## Process

1. Confirm the decision type. The structure, tone, and procedural posture differ between acquisitions, weeding decisions, and reconsideration responses. Do not blur them.
2. Pull the specific criteria from the library's collection development policy. The justification must map each factor to a policy criterion rather than to staff preference.
3. For acquisitions, identify the gap the title fills, the professional review record (Booklist, Kirkus, Library Journal, School Library Journal, etc. as applicable), awards, and community demand signals (hold queue, request slips, programming alignment).
4. For weeding, apply the CREW method or the library's adopted equivalent (Continuous Review, Evaluation, and Weeding) — typically MUSTIE factors (Misleading, Ugly, Superseded, Trivial, Irrelevant, Elsewhere). Note circulation history and condition. Weeding is a routine, professional collection management decision and is not a reconsideration.
5. For reconsiderations, follow the library's adopted reconsideration policy step by step. Acknowledge the patron, restate the concern faithfully, describe the review and award record of the material, map the title against each collection development criterion, address the intellectual freedom question directly, describe any access controls already in place (age placement, card permissions), and state the procedural next step (appeal to library board, etc.).
6. Anchor the intellectual freedom analysis in the American Library Association Library Bill of Rights and Freedom to Read Statement. See `references/ala-intellectual-freedom-framework.md` for the controlling principles.
7. Close with a signature block for the librarian or director and route the document per the controlling policy.

## Output Format

Plain markdown memo. No emoji. No emotive language. Professional and procedural.

```
COLLECTION DEVELOPMENT DECISION MEMO
[Library Name]
Date: [Date]
From: [Librarian or Director name and title]
To: [Audience — Director, Library Board, Patron, File]
Re: [Title or Collection Segment]

DECISION STATEMENT
[One sentence: the library will / will not acquire | retain | remove the
material.]

DECISION TYPE
[Acquisition | Weeding (Deselection) | Reconsideration Response]

COLLECTION DEVELOPMENT POLICY REFERENCE
[Cite the controlling policy section.]

FACTORS SUPPORTING THE DECISION
[Bulleted list. Each bullet maps to a specific policy criterion.]

INTELLECTUAL FREEDOM ANALYSIS
[Required where the decision type is a reconsideration response.
Brief, sincere, grounded in the ALA Library Bill of Rights and the
Freedom to Read Statement. State the library's commitment to access
and the role of parents and caregivers in guiding their own children's
reading.]

PROCEDURAL COMPLIANCE
[For reconsiderations: confirm the policy steps followed and state
the next available step (appeal to library board).
For weeding: confirm the CREW / MUSTIE factors applied.
For acquisitions: confirm the request and approval workflow.]

SIGNATURE
___________________________________
[Name, Title]
```

Length: one to three pages depending on decision complexity. Reconsideration responses tend toward the longer end.

## TRAIGA & Compliance Notes

**AI use disclosure — transparency best practice (not a § 552.051 trigger):** Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.051(b)–(d) requires disclosure when a citizen *interacts directly with an AI system* (e.g., a chatbot, automated voice assistant, or AI-driven service interface). The disclosure trigger is *interaction*, not *output*. This skill produces a static document drafted with AI assistance; it does not put a citizen in direct interaction with AI, so § 552.051 is not engaged.

However, transparency about AI assistance in document drafting is sound practice and should align with the city's adopted AI use policy and the GovAI Coalition's guidance on documenting AI tool use. Consider a brief notation on documents distributed publicly or to the governing body: *"AI-assisted draft, reviewed by [staff title and name]."* This is a policy choice, not a statutory mandate.

**Texas public library standards:** TSLAC (Texas State Library and Archives Commission) administers accreditation standards for Texas public libraries. Accredited libraries must maintain a written collection development policy and a written reconsideration (challenge) policy. The skill output is built to fit those policies, not replace them.

**Municipal and county library authority:** Municipal libraries operate under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 326 and county libraries under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 323. The library director typically reports to the city manager or county judge; the library board, where established, is advisory unless local charter or ordinance provides otherwise.

**First Amendment posture:** A municipal library is a limited public forum for expressive content. Removal based on viewpoint disagreement raises constitutional issues. Removal decisions should rest on policy-based factors (currency, condition, relevance), not content disagreement.

**Public records:** Reconsideration requests, decision memos, and board correspondence are public records under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552. Patron personally identifying information should be redacted where Tex. Gov't Code § 552.124 (library patron records) applies.

**Retention:** Decision memos and reconsideration records are retained per the TSLAC Local Government Retention Schedule. Many libraries retain reconsideration files permanently for institutional memory.
