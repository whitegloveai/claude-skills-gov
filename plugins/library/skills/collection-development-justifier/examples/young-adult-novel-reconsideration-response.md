# Young Adult Novel Reconsideration Response — Cedar Ridge Public Library

## Input

- Decision type: reconsideration response
- Title: *The Quiet Hours* by L. M. Patel (fictional young adult novel)
- Controlling policy: Cedar Ridge Public Library Collection Development Policy, Section 4 (Selection Criteria) and Section 7 (Reconsideration of Library Materials)
- Challenger's stated concern: contains scenes of underage drinking and a discussion of a same-sex relationship; requests removal from the young adult section
- Age placement: shelved in the Young Adult section (ages 12 and up)
- Review record: starred reviews in Booklist and School Library Journal; long-listed for the Walter Award
- Access controls: parents may request a "youth card" that restricts checkout to the children's collection
- Procedural posture: response from Library Director to challenger and copied to library board chair; appeal pathway is to the library board

## Output

```

DECISION STATEMENT
The Cedar Ridge Public Library will retain The Quiet Hours by L. M.
Patel in the Young Adult collection without restriction. The
library will continue to make available the youth-card access
control for families who prefer to limit their own children's
checkout to the children's collection.

DECISION TYPE
Reconsideration Response.

COLLECTION DEVELOPMENT POLICY REFERENCE
Cedar Ridge Public Library Collection Development Policy, Section 4
(Selection Criteria) and Section 7 (Reconsideration of Library
Materials), adopted by the Library Board on March 11, 2024.

FACTORS SUPPORTING THE DECISION
- Professional review record (Policy § 4.2): The Quiet Hours
  received starred reviews in Booklist and School Library Journal,
  the two standard professional review sources for young adult
  fiction.
- Recognition (Policy § 4.3): the title was long-listed for the
  Walter Dean Myers Award for Outstanding Children's Literature,
  recognizing books that authentically reflect the diverse
  experiences of young readers.
- Relevance and community need (Policy § 4.4 and § 4.5): the title
  addresses themes — friendship, family conflict, identity — that
  are central to the developmental experience of the audience for
  which it is intended.
- Age placement (Policy § 4.7): the title is shelved in the Young
  Adult section, which the policy defines as ages 12 and up, the
  age range for which the publisher and reviewers have written.

INTELLECTUAL FREEDOM ANALYSIS
The library acknowledges the patron's concern and has reviewed the
title against the library's policy and the American Library
Association's Library Bill of Rights. Article II of the Library
Bill of Rights provides that materials should not be removed
because of partisan or doctrinal disapproval. Article V provides
that a person's right to use a library should not be denied or
abridged because of age. The library's role is to provide a
collection that reflects the breadth of experience and viewpoint
of the community it serves. The role of selecting what an
individual child reads belongs to that child's parents and
caregivers, not the library. The youth-card access control is
provided precisely so that families who wish to limit their own
children's reading to the children's collection can do so without
restricting access for other families.

PROCEDURAL COMPLIANCE
This response follows Section 7 of the Cedar Ridge Public Library
Collection Development Policy. The challenger submitted the
written Request for Reconsideration of Library Materials on April
24, 2026. The Library Director reviewed the request, the title,
the professional review record, and the policy criteria during the
two-week response window required by policy. If the challenger
wishes to appeal this decision, the policy provides for appeal to
the Cedar Ridge Public Library Board. A written appeal may be
submitted to the Board Chair within thirty (30) days of this
response and will be heard at the next regular meeting of the
board for which the appeal can be timely posted under the Texas
Open Meetings Act, Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 551.
```
