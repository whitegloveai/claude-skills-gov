# Municipal Newsletter Section Templates

Reusable 50–100 word boilerplate blocks for recurring newsletter sections. Replace bracketed fields with current numbers, dates, and contact information before publishing. Each template is jurisdiction-neutral; the example placeholders use Cedar Ridge for consistency with the example folder.

## 1. Budget Update

**Use after budget adoption, mid-year amendments, or quarterly status reports.**

The City Council adopted the FY[YEAR] budget on [DATE] with a total operating budget of $[AMOUNT]. The adopted property tax rate is $[RATE] per $100 of valuation, [unchanged from / a decrease from / an increase from] the prior year. Major investments this year include [PROJECT 1], [PROJECT 2], and [PROJECT 3]. The full adopted budget, the City Manager's transmittal letter, and the property tax rate calculation worksheets required under Tex. Tax Code Ch. 26 are available at [URL]. Residents with questions about the budget may contact the Finance Department at [PHONE] or [EMAIL].

## 2. Capital Project Update

**Use for capital improvement project status, road closures, or facility construction.**

The [PROJECT NAME] is now [PHASE — design / bid / construction / final acceptance]. The total budget is $[AMOUNT], funded by [SOURCE — bond, grant, general fund, utility revenue]. Work is being performed by [CONTRACTOR], with substantial completion expected by [DATE]. During construction, residents may experience [SPECIFIC IMPACT — lane closures, detours, water shut-offs, parking restrictions]. Real-time updates and a project map are posted at [URL]. To report a construction-related concern, call [PHONE]. For accessibility accommodations during construction, contact the ADA Coordinator at [PHONE] or [EMAIL].

## 3. Code Enforcement Reminder

**Use seasonally for high-violation categories: lawns, junked vehicles, sign ordinances, short-term rentals.**

A reminder from Code Enforcement: under City Code Chapter [NUMBER], property owners are responsible for keeping [WEEDS / GRASS / JUNKED VEHICLES / OUTDOOR STORAGE] in compliance. Grass and weeds taller than [HEIGHT] inches must be cut. Inoperable or unlicensed vehicles parked in public view violate Section [NUMBER]. First-time violations typically receive a courtesy notice with [NUMBER] days to comply. Continued violations may result in administrative citations starting at $[AMOUNT]. To report a concern, use the city's online portal at [URL] or call [PHONE]. Reports may be made anonymously.

## 4. Public Safety Tip

**Use for seasonal safety, scam awareness, traffic enforcement focus, or community engagement.**

The [POLICE / FIRE] Department reminds residents to [SEASONAL TIP — e.g., check smoke detector batteries, lock vehicles, secure pool gates]. Last year, the department responded to [NUMBER] incidents related to [CATEGORY], most of which were preventable. Officers will be conducting enhanced [PATROLS / INSPECTIONS] in [AREA / TIME PERIOD]. To request a community safety presentation for a neighborhood association, church, or HOA, contact Community Engagement at [PHONE]. In an emergency, call 911. For non-emergency police service, call [PHONE]. The department's annual report and use-of-force data are posted at [URL].

## 5. Water Conservation Tip

**Use during summer, drought stages, or after a TCEQ drought-contingency-plan activation.**

The City of [CITY] is currently in [STAGE NUMBER] of its Drought Contingency Plan, approved by the Texas Commission on Environmental Quality. Outdoor watering is permitted on [DAY/TIME SCHEDULE]. Residents are asked to [SPECIFIC ASK — limit irrigation, fix leaks, use a broom not a hose]. A typical household can save [NUMBER] gallons per month by reducing irrigation to twice per week. To check your water use, log in to the customer portal at [URL]. Rebates for high-efficiency fixtures and rain barrels are available at [URL]. To report a water main break, call the after-hours line at [PHONE].

## 6. Severe Weather Preparedness

**Use before storm season, hurricane season, or after a regional event.**

Severe weather season is here. The City of [CITY] participates in the regional emergency notification system. To receive alerts by text or email, sign up at [URL] or text [KEYWORD] to [NUMBER]. The city's emergency operations plan, adopted by Council under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 418, identifies [LOCATIONS] as designated shelter or warming centers when activated. Build a household emergency kit with three days of water, food, medications, and important documents. Keep a battery-powered radio. Know your evacuation route. For more information, visit [URL] or call the Emergency Management Office at [PHONE]. In an emergency, always call 911.

## Notes on Use

- Verify every dollar amount, date, and statutory citation before publishing. Templates are scaffolding, not facts.
- Spanish-language versions of each template should be maintained in parallel; do not rely on machine translation alone for safety-critical sections (Drought Contingency Plan stages, evacuation routes, emergency contact numbers).
- Templates should be reviewed annually by the relevant department head and the city attorney for any statutory updates (Tex. Tax Code Ch. 26 truth-in-taxation thresholds, TCEQ drought rules, and ADA Title II coordinator requirements change periodically).
- A template that has not been used in 18 months may need refreshing; statutory references and dollar thresholds drift.
- The "Code Enforcement Reminder" template should be coordinated with the city attorney and the municipal court clerk to ensure cited ordinances and fine amounts are current.
