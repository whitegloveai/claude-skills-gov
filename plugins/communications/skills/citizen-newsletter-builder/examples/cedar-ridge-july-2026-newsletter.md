# Example: City of Cedar Ridge July 2026 Citizen Newsletter

This is a worked example for the citizen-newsletter-builder skill. It uses the fictional City of Cedar Ridge, TX. The newsletter is the post-budget-adoption issue and recaps decisions from the June 9, 2026 regular council meeting (see council-ops agenda-builder example). The street overlay project referenced was procured through the finance procurement-rfp-builder workflow.

## Input

- **Period:** July 2026 (monthly newsletter)
- **Distribution channel:** Utility bill insert + email + website + bilingual edition
- **Council decisions from June 9, 2026 meeting:**
  - Adopted FY2026 budget amendment ($312,000 to street maintenance)
  - Approved Z-2026-04 rezoning at 1100 Pecan Street
  - Awarded street overlay contract
  - Adopted Resolution 2026-14 honoring retiring City Secretary Helen Marquez
- **Upcoming events:**
  - July 4 — Independence Day, City Hall closed
  - July 14 — Regular Council Meeting, 6:30 p.m.
  - July 18 — Fire Station #3 groundbreaking
  - July 22 — Planning & Zoning Commission, 6 p.m.
  - July 28 — Regular Council Meeting, 6:30 p.m.
- **Manager column topic:** Daniel Ahn on the FY2026 budget and the street overlay project
- **Featured project:** Street overlay program
- **Departments reporting:** Public works, parks (summer programming), library (summer reading), finance (utility billing), public safety (heat safety)
- **Bilingual:** Yes — Spanish summary box

## Output

```
CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE CITIZEN NEWSLETTER
July 2026 Edition

FROM THE CITY MANAGER

Cedar Ridge neighbors,

On June 9, your City Council adopted an amendment to the FY2026 budget
that moves $312,000 from the General Fund contingency reserve into the
street maintenance program. With that funding in place, the Council
awarded the FY2026 street overlay contract the same evening. Crews
begin work the week of July 21.

You will see lane closures and short detours over the coming three
months. Public Works has posted the full street list and a project map
at cedarridgetx.gov/streets. I want to acknowledge in advance that
construction is disruptive. We chose this work because Cedar Ridge has
been deferring street maintenance for longer than is fiscally
responsible, and we are catching up.

The Council also honored Helen Marquez, who retires this month after
twenty-five years as our City Secretary. Helen has posted every council
agenda, certified every election, and protected every public record
this city has produced since 2001. She is irreplaceable. I am grateful
for her work and her friendship.

A few thanks: to the Pecan Grove Land Trust for donating the site for
Fire Station #3, which we break ground on July 18. To every resident
who showed up to the budget hearings in April and May. To the public
works crew that worked the holiday weekend cleanup after the June 28
storms.

I am at danielahn@cedarridgetx.gov if you have questions. My door is
open.

Daniel Ahn
City Manager

DEPARTMENTAL UPDATES

PUBLIC WORKS
The FY2026 street overlay program begins July 21. Crews will work
weekdays, 7 a.m. to 5 p.m., on a rotating block schedule. Residents on
overlay streets will receive a door hanger 48 hours before work begins
on their block. To report a pothole, missed trash pickup, or signal
malfunction, use the SeeClickFix portal at cedarridgetx.gov/report.
Public Works is hiring two equipment operators; applications at
cedarridgetx.gov/jobs.

PARKS AND RECREATION
Summer programming is in full swing. The Cedar Ridge Aquatic Center is
open daily 10 a.m. to 8 p.m. through Labor Day. Youth swim lessons
register on the 15th of each month at cedarridgetx.gov/parks. The
splash pad at Live Oak Park runs 9 a.m. to 9 p.m. daily and is free.
July movies in the park: July 11 ("Encanto"), July 25 ("Coco"). Bring
a blanket; concessions benefit the Friends of Cedar Ridge Parks.

LIBRARY
Summer Reading runs through August 9. Readers of all ages can register
at the front desk or online. This year's theme is "Read Beyond the
Beaten Path." Prizes for kids, teens, and adults. The library is also
hosting free Spanish-English conversation circles on Saturdays, 10
a.m., open to all ability levels. New e-book and audiobook titles are
added every Tuesday.

PUBLIC SAFETY
Cedar Ridge Police remind residents to lock vehicles overnight. Forty
percent of last month's reported thefts from vehicles involved unlocked
cars. Fire Department urges heat safety: never leave children or pets
in parked vehicles. Average July afternoon temperatures inside a parked
car can reach 130 degrees within 30 minutes. To request a community
safety presentation, call (555) 555-0150. In an emergency, call 911.

FINANCE AND ADMINISTRATION
Utility billing customers may now sign up for paperless billing and
auto-pay at cedarridgetx.gov/utilities. Customers who switch to
paperless before August 1 are entered to win a one-month credit. The
FY2026 adopted budget and the property tax rate calculation worksheets
required under Tex. Tax Code Ch. 26 are posted at
cedarridgetx.gov/budget.

PLANNING AND DEVELOPMENT
Three public hearings are scheduled in July and August. The July 22
Planning and Zoning Commission will consider a preliminary plat for
the Live Oak Trails subdivision and a variance request at 808 Magnolia
Lane. Notices have been mailed to property owners within 200 feet
under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 211.007. Anyone may attend and comment.

FEATURED PROJECT: FY2026 STREET OVERLAY PROGRAM

This summer, Cedar Ridge will overlay 14.3 miles of city streets, the
largest single year of street maintenance in the city's history. The
$1.847 million program funds asphalt overlay, crack sealing, and ADA
ramp upgrades on streets selected by the Public Works Department based
on the 2024 pavement condition index. Streets included this year are
in the Pecan Grove, Live Oak Heights, and Mockingbird Hill
neighborhoods. The full list, a project map, and a frequently asked
questions document are at cedarridgetx.gov/streets. The contractor,
Bluebonnet Construction, was selected as the lowest responsible bidder
through a competitive procurement under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 252.
Substantial completion is expected by October 31, 2026.
```

## Notes for the User

- The "What Council Decided" section is the highest-risk part. Cross-check vote counts and dollar amounts against the official minutes from the council-ops minutes-drafter workflow before publication.
- The street overlay procurement reference assumes the finance procurement-rfp-builder workflow produced the Bluebonnet Construction selection; verify the actual contractor name and award amount against the bid tabulation.
- Spanish summary box uses ASCII-only characters for compatibility with the utility billing system insert; replace with properly accented text for the web version.
- For the utility-bill insert version, trim to 4 pages by combining the manager column and "What Council Decided" sections; full version goes to email and web.
- Schedule the August edition draft for July 28, after the second July council meeting, so it can recap both July meetings.
- The "AI-assisted draft" notation in the colophon reflects the city's adopted AI use policy; remove or modify if the city has different policy.
