# Example: Street Overlay Road Closures Social Posts

This is a worked example for the social-media-municipal-style skill. It uses the fictional City of Cedar Ridge, TX. The City Council awarded the FY2026 street overlay contract on June 9 (see council-ops agenda-builder example), and PIO Marcia Delgado needs platform-tailored social posts to announce the road closures beginning July 21.

## Input

- **Topic:** FY2026 street overlay program road closures begin July 21
- **Platforms:** Facebook, X/Twitter, Nextdoor
- **Urgency:** Time-sensitive (advance notice to residents)
- **Audience:** Residents in affected neighborhoods (Pecan Grove, Live Oak Heights, Mockingbird Hill) plus general public
- **Tone target:** Informational, slightly cautionary (acknowledges disruption)
- **Aligned with:** July 2026 citizen newsletter feature; press release pending
- **Image asset on hand:** Map of overlay streets, generic public-works crew photo
- **Cadence:** Weekly during construction (12 weeks, July 21 through October 31)
- **Bilingual:** Yes — include Spanish summary in each post

POSTING CADENCE (12 weeks, July 21 through October 31)

- Week before kickoff (week of July 13): Variations above on Facebook,
  X, and Nextdoor. Friday: press release goes out.
- Week 1 (July 21): Monday morning kickoff post on all platforms with
  current week's street list. Mid-week safety reminder on Twitter.
- Weeks 2-11: Monday weekly update post with current week's street
  list and any schedule changes. Thursday photo or progress post
  (Instagram-friendly).
- Final week (week of October 27): Wrap-up post with total miles
  completed and thanks. Include a one-paragraph note that the FY2027
  pavement assessment will be conducted in winter and the next year's
  street list will be developed from it.

---

WHAT NOT TO POST

- Do not post the contractor's name in a way that implies endorsement.
  Mention the contractor only as a factual reference and only in the
  press release or the linked FAQ.
- Do not respond in-thread to complaints about the bid award. The bid
  process is documented and posted; link to the documentation. Route
  any allegation of impropriety to the City Manager and City Attorney.
- Do not delete critical comments unless they violate the moderation
  policy (slurs, threats, doxxing, spam). Document every moderation
  action.
- Do not engage with comments that compare this project favorably or
  unfavorably to neighboring cities' street work. Stay neutral and on
  topic.
- Do not post photos of identifiable residents' homes or vehicles
  without permission. Crew photos and street-view photos are fine.
- Do not use the city accounts to share resident complaints or praise
  about specific neighbors. Refer to the appropriate department.
- For traffic safety incidents in or near a work zone: do not post
  about the incident from the public works account. Refer to the
  police department's standard process.

Drafted with AI assistance, reviewed by Marcia Delgado, Public

## Notes for the User

- Confirm the affected street list and the contractor name (Bluebonnet Construction, per the June 9 council action) before posting.
- Coordinate with Public Works Director Patel on the weekly Monday post: she should provide the current week's street list each Friday afternoon.
- For the Nextdoor neighborhood-tailored versions, post in each of the three named Nextdoor neighborhoods, not the citywide Nextdoor feed; the post performs better localized.
- The Friday afternoon press release should be drafted with the press-release-drafter skill.
- Archive every post through the city's social media archiving tool. Save moderation logs.
- The "AI-assisted draft" notation is internal documentation and is not posted publicly; remove or modify per city AI use policy.
- If a TPIA request comes in for social media records related to the overlay project, the archiving tool's export should be sufficient; coordinate with the City Secretary.
