# Example: Fire Station #3 Groundbreaking Press Release

This is a worked example for the press-release-drafter skill. It uses the fictional City of Cedar Ridge, TX. The city's Public Information Officer, Marcia Delgado, is preparing a release for a public groundbreaking ceremony.

## Input

- **Topic:** Groundbreaking ceremony for new Cedar Ridge Fire Station #3
- **Five Ws:**
  - Who: Mayor Castillo, City Manager Daniel Ahn, Fire Chief Reyna, City Council
  - What: Official groundbreaking ceremony for Fire Station #3
  - Where: 4400 West Loop Road, Cedar Ridge, TX
  - When: Saturday, July 18, 2026, 10:00 a.m.
  - Why: Station #3 will reduce average response time on the west side of the city from 9 minutes to under 5 minutes; funded by the 2024 voter-approved public safety bond
- **Release timing:** FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
- **Primary contact:** Marcia Delgado, Public Information Officer, (555) 555-0125, mdelgado@cedarridgetx.gov
- **Quote:** From Mayor Castillo
- **Background:** Voters approved a $14.5M public safety bond in November 2024 (Proposition A, 62% in favor). Station #3 is the bond's largest single project at $9.2M. Land was donated by the Pecan Grove Land Trust.
- **Bilingual:** Yes — Spanish summary paragraph requested for the city's ~30% Spanish-speaking population.

## Output

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

July 10, 2026

Media Contact:
Marcia Delgado, Public Information Officer
City of Cedar Ridge
(555) 555-0125 | mdelgado@cedarridgetx.gov

Cedar Ridge Breaks Ground on Fire Station #3 on July 18

CEDAR RIDGE, Texas — The City of Cedar Ridge will break ground on Fire
Station #3 at 10 a.m. Saturday, July 18, at 4400 West Loop Road, marking
the start of construction on the largest project funded by the 2024
public safety bond.

The 12,400-square-foot station will house two engine companies and a
brush truck, with quarters for nine firefighters on shift. Once
operational in late 2027, Station #3 is projected to reduce average
emergency response times on the city's west side from nine minutes to
under five minutes.

"For the families living west of Loop 12, a five-minute response time is
not a statistic — it is the difference between a kitchen fire and a lost
home," said Mayor Castillo. "Cedar Ridge voters told us in 2024 that
public safety could not wait. This groundbreaking is us keeping that
promise."

The $9.2 million project is funded by the public safety bond approved by
Cedar Ridge voters in November 2024 (Proposition A, 62 percent in
favor). The 1.8-acre site was donated by the Pecan Grove Land Trust in
March 2025. Construction is being managed under the Tex. Loc. Gov't Code
Ch. 252 competitive sealed proposal procurement awarded to Bluebonnet
Construction at the June 9, 2026 council meeting.

Fire Chief Reyna, City Manager Daniel Ahn, and members of the Cedar
Ridge City Council will join Mayor Castillo for the ceremonial first
shovel. Members of the Cedar Ridge Volunteer Fire Department, who served
the city before the establishment of paid fire service in 1978, will be
recognized.

Event details:
- Date: Saturday, July 18, 2026
- Time: 10 a.m.
- Location: 4400 West Loop Road, Cedar Ridge, TX 75000
- Parking: Public lot at 4350 West Loop Road
- ADA accessibility: Paved walkway from parking to the ceremony area;
  reserved seating available
- The ceremony is open to the public. Light refreshments will be
  provided.

En espanol: La Ciudad de Cedar Ridge invita a los residentes a la
ceremonia inaugural de la Estacion de Bomberos numero 3 el sabado 18 de
julio a las 10 de la manana en 4400 West Loop Road. La nueva estacion,
financiada por el bono de seguridad publica aprobado por los votantes en
2024, reducira el tiempo promedio de respuesta de emergencia en el oeste
de la ciudad. Para mas informacion, comuniquese con la Oficial de
Informacion Publica Marcia Delgado al (555) 555-0125.

About the City of Cedar Ridge
The City of Cedar Ridge is a home-rule municipality of approximately
32,000 residents located in Caddo County, Texas. Incorporated in 1894,
Cedar Ridge operates under a council-manager form of government with a
mayor and six council members. The city provides full-service municipal
operations including police, fire, water utilities, parks, and library
services.

For more information, visit www.cedarridgetx.gov or contact Marcia
Delgado at (555) 555-0125.
```

## Notes for the User

- Confirm Mayor Castillo's exact quote wording before distribution. The draft quote should be approved by the mayor's office.
- Confirm the bond election margin (62%) and bond amount ($14.5M) against the certified canvass.
- Confirm the procurement reference; the example assumes the contract was awarded at the June 9, 2026 regular meeting (see council-ops agenda-builder example).
- The Spanish summary paragraph uses ASCII-only characters to ensure compatibility with email systems and CMS posting; replace with properly accented text if the city's distribution channels support UTF-8 cleanly.
- Distribute to: Cedar Ridge Gazette, KCRG radio, regional ABC/NBC affiliates, county wire, council members, all-staff email, city website news page, and city social media accounts (use the social-media-municipal-style skill for the post variations).
- Schedule a follow-up release one week prior to the ribbon-cutting in 2027; archive this release in the city's press release folder under the FY2026 public safety bond project file.
- Suggested AI-use notation on the draft routed to the City Manager for review: *"AI-assisted draft, reviewed by Marcia Delgado, Public Information Officer."*
