# Press Release Drafter

> Drafts a clean, AP Style press release in inverted-pyramid form. Built for public information officers, communications directors, and department heads who need a publishable draft fast.

## When to Use

- "Draft a press release for the groundbreaking on Fire Station #3."
- "We just got a $1.2M TxDOT grant. I need a release by end of day."
- "Mayor wants a release announcing the new community garden ribbon-cutting."
- "Police chief wants a release on the citizen academy graduation."
- "Library is launching summer reading. Draft the release."
- "Council adopted the FY2026 budget last night. Send out a release."
- Any time staff needs an announcement that will go to local media, be posted on the city website, and be archived as a public record.

## Inputs Needed

Required:

- Topic or announcement (one sentence)
- Key facts: who, what, where, when, why
- Release timing: FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE or EMBARGOED UNTIL [date/time]
- Primary media contact (name, title, phone, email)
- City name, state, and dateline city

Optional:

- Quote and attribution (name, title). If omitted, the skill leaves a clearly marked placeholder for the quote and identifies whom to ask.
- Secondary contact for after-hours
- Background paragraph (history, prior project phases, related council actions)
- Photo or b-roll availability note
- Spanish-translation flag for jurisdictions with significant Spanish-speaking populations

If timing, contact, or the five Ws are missing, ask before drafting. Do not fabricate quotes; if no quote is supplied, mark a placeholder and identify the appropriate spokesperson.

## Process

1. Confirm release timing. FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE is the default. An embargo is only used when the city has coordinated the embargo with reporters in advance; do not invent embargoes.
2. Confirm the dateline city and state. For most municipal releases, this is the city name itself.
3. Draft a headline under 80 characters. Active voice. No editorializing. Sentence-case or title-case per the city's style guide; default to title case.
4. Draft a one-sentence lead that covers as many of the five Ws as readable. Aim for under 35 words.
5. Build supporting paragraphs in inverted-pyramid order — most important to least. Each paragraph stands alone in case an editor truncates from the bottom.
6. Place one attributed quote in the upper third. Quote should advance the story (express significance, gratitude, public benefit) rather than restate facts. If no quote provided, insert a clearly marked placeholder and name the appropriate spokesperson.
7. Add a background paragraph if context is needed (prior council action, related project, grant program description).
8. Close with the city boilerplate ("About [City]") and the media contact block.
9. End with the ### three-pound-sign mark indicating end of release.

## Output Format

Plain text, AP Style. No emoji. No bold or italic emphasis in the body (the release will be reformatted by media outlets).

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
[OR: EMBARGOED UNTIL [DATE], [TIME] CT]

[DATE]

Media Contact:
[Name], [Title]
[City Department or PIO Office]
[Phone] | [Email]

[HEADLINE IN TITLE CASE — UNDER 80 CHARACTERS]

[DATELINE CITY], [STATE] — [Lead sentence covering who, what, where, when,
and why in under 35 words.]

[Supporting paragraph with the second-most-important facts. Keep
paragraphs to 2–3 sentences. Use active voice and concrete numbers.]

"[Attributed quote that advances the story — significance, gratitude, or
public benefit. Not a restatement of facts]," said [Name], [Title].

[Additional supporting paragraph. Add context, project phasing, funding
sources, or related actions.]

[Background paragraph if needed. Prior council action, grant program,
historical context.]

[Logistics paragraph for events: date, time, location, parking,
accessibility, RSVP if required.]

About the City of [City Name]
[2–3 sentence boilerplate. Population, form of government, year
incorporated, signature programs or assets. Same boilerplate on every
release.]

For more information, visit [URL] or contact [Name] at [phone/email].

###
```

Length: typically 350–600 words. Longer releases bury the lead.

Tone: neutral, factual, civically appropriate. No marketing superlatives ("amazing," "groundbreaking," "world-class"). Concrete numbers over adjectives.

## TRAIGA & Compliance Notes

**AI use disclosure — transparency best practice (not a § 552.051 trigger):** Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.051(b)–(d) requires disclosure when a citizen *interacts directly with an AI system* (e.g., a chatbot, automated voice assistant, or AI-driven service interface). The disclosure trigger is *interaction*, not *output*. This skill produces a static document drafted with AI assistance; it does not put a citizen in direct interaction with AI, so § 552.051 is not engaged.

However, transparency about AI assistance in document drafting is sound practice and should align with the city's adopted AI use policy and the GovAI Coalition's guidance on documenting AI tool use. Consider a brief notation on documents distributed publicly or to the governing body: *"AI-assisted draft, reviewed by [staff title and name]."* This is a policy choice, not a statutory mandate.

**AP Style baseline:** Use AP Style for dates (Jan. 9, not January 9th), titles before names (Mayor Smith, not Smith, Mayor), numbers (spell out one through nine; numerals for 10 and above; always numerals for ages, money, and percentages), and Oxford comma omission. Cities should adopt and document any deviations.

**ADA and plain language:** Target an 8th-grade reading level. Spell out acronyms on first use. Avoid jargon and bureaucratese. The Americans with Disabilities Act requires effective communication; releases posted online should meet WCAG 2.1 AA, including alt text on any accompanying images and accessible PDF if posted as PDF.

**Spanish translation:** For jurisdictions with significant Spanish-speaking populations (typically 5% or greater limited-English-proficient under DOJ LEP guidance), produce a parallel Spanish version or include a Spanish summary paragraph. Cities with very high Spanish-speaking populations (roughly 25%+ LEP) should treat bilingual release as routine, not exceptional.

**Public records:** The final release is a public record under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552. Internal drafts, edit history, and email threads about the release may be exempt under § 552.111 (intra-agency memoranda) until released. Do not include in drafts anything that would be embarrassing if released in a TPIA response.

**Embargoes:** Do not invent embargo language. An embargo is a coordinated agreement with reporters and has no legal force; it is honored on professional norms. Use FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE unless the PIO has confirmed an embargo with the receiving outlets.

**Attribution and verification:** Quotes must be verified with the named spokesperson before release. Numbers (grant amounts, populations, costs) must be sourced. Errors in a press release become errors in news coverage and create public records that may surface in later disputes.
