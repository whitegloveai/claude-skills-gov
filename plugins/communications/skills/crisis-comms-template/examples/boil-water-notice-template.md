# Example: Cedar Ridge Boil-Water Notice Crisis Comms Package

This is a worked example for the crisis-comms-template skill. It uses the fictional City of Cedar Ridge, TX. A boil-water emergency is the same incident referenced in the council-ops agenda-builder skill's "When to Use" examples ("We have an emergency meeting tomorrow on the boil-water notice — draft the agenda."). The PIO, Marcia Delgado, is preparing the comms package at 3:15 a.m. as crews respond to a major main break that has dropped pressure across the north half of the city.

## Input

- **Incident type:** Boil-water notice (loss of pressure below 20 psi due to 12-inch main break at Mockingbird Hill Booster Station)
- **Date/time activated:** Tuesday, August 18, 2026, 3:00 a.m.
- **Scope:** Approximately 14,500 residents north of FM 1942 (Pecan Grove, Live Oak Heights, Mockingbird Hill neighborhoods, plus the school district's three north-side campuses)
- **Verified status:** Main break confirmed at 2:18 a.m.; system pressure dropped below 20 psi; TCEQ regional office notified at 2:45 a.m.; isolation valve closed at 3:05 a.m.; sampling begins at 6 a.m.; system rebuild and refill underway
- **Known unknowns:** Time to complete two consecutive clean TCEQ samples (typically 24–48 hours); whether any private wells in the city limits were affected
- **Lead agency:** City of Cedar Ridge (TCEQ in coordinating role for sampling and clearance)
- **Incident commander:** Public Works Director Patel
- **Spokesperson:** Marcia Delgado, PIO (for press); Daniel Ahn, City Manager (for council and media-ready)
- **Channels:** Website, Facebook, X/Twitter, Nextdoor, CodeRED mass notification, KCRG radio, Cedar Ridge ISD parent notification system, regional ABC/NBC affiliates
- **Bilingual:** Yes (mandatory)

For release at: 4:00 a.m. Tuesday, August 18
Approved by: ______________________ (City Manager)

BOIL-WATER NOTICE — CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE

The City of Cedar Ridge is issuing a boil-water notice for all
addresses north of FM 1942, including the Pecan Grove, Live Oak
Heights, and Mockingbird Hill neighborhoods, effective immediately.

This notice is being issued because a 12-inch water main failed at the
Mockingbird Hill Booster Station at approximately 2:18 a.m., causing
system pressure in the affected area to drop below 20 psi. Under Texas
Commission on Environmental Quality rules (30 TAC § 290.46), the
City is required to issue a boil-water notice and complete two
consecutive negative samples before the water is confirmed safe to
drink.

UNTIL FURTHER NOTICE, RESIDENTS IN THE AFFECTED AREA SHOULD:

- Bring tap water to a vigorous rolling boil for at least two minutes
  before using it for drinking, cooking, ice, brushing teeth, washing
  dishes, or preparing baby food and formula.
- Use bottled water as an alternative.
- Avoid swallowing water while bathing.
- Throw away ice made with tap water during the notice period.

The City's Public Works crews have closed the isolation valve and
begun system rebuild. TCEQ-required sampling begins at 6 a.m.
Typical clearance time is 24 to 48 hours.

Cedar Ridge Independent School District has been notified. Cedar
Ridge ISD will release information about its three north-side campuses
through its own channels.

The next update will be issued at 9 a.m.

For updates: cedarridgetx.gov/boilwater | @cedarridgetx on Facebook,
Twitter, and Nextdoor. To sign up for CodeRED emergency alerts:
cedarridgetx.gov/alerts.

For accessibility accommodations, contact the City Secretary's office
at (555) 555-0100.

EN ESPANOL:

Ambiental de Texas (TCEQ), la ciudad debe emitir un aviso de hervir el

## Notes for the User

- The boil-water emergency in this example connects to the council-ops agenda-builder example, which mentions a boil-water emergency in its "When to Use" examples. After the all-clear, the council may convene a special meeting to receive the after-action briefing; use the council-ops agenda-builder skill to draft that agenda under Tex. Gov't Code § 551.043 or, if circumstances warrant, § 551.045 for an emergency posting.
- Spanish summaries in the templates use ASCII-only characters for compatibility with mass notification systems; full versions for web and social should use properly accented text.
- TCEQ's prescribed boil-water notice language must be used verbatim in the consumer-facing notice. The version here incorporates the required instructions; confirm with the TCEQ Region 4 office that the language matches the current required template before release.
- For the school district communication, Cedar Ridge ISD has its own parent notification system and PIO; do not communicate to parents directly. The city's role is to alert the school district and let it communicate with families.
- The bottled water distribution detail should be confirmed against the actual logistics (whether the Recreation Center is staffed, supply on hand, mutual aid arrivals).
- Document every draft, edit, and approval in the city's records system. The after-action review (AAR) and all related communications are public records under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552; the AAR should be drafted with disclosure in mind.
- If the incident escalates (additional infrastructure failure, public health concern, federal involvement), pause and reconfirm the spokesperson chain and the lead agency.
