# Crisis Communications Holding Statement Patterns

Proven holding-statement structures for the most common municipal crises. Each pattern includes the three-part structure (what we know, what we are doing, next update), the most common do's and don'ts, and the pivot from holding to fuller communication. Use these as scaffolding; verify every fact with incident command before release.

## 1. Boil-Water Notice

**Trigger:** Loss of pressure below 20 psi, detected contamination, or another condition requiring public notice under TCEQ rules (30 TAC § 290.46(q) and § 290.46(r)).

**Three-part holding statement:**

- *What we know:* "The City of [City] is issuing a boil-water notice for [specific area or system-wide] effective [timestamp]. [Cause if verified: main break, loss of pressure, contamination detection.]"
- *What we are doing:* "Crews are working to restore pressure and complete the testing required by the Texas Commission on Environmental Quality. The notice will remain in effect until two consecutive samples confirm the water is safe to drink, which typically takes 24 to 48 hours."
- *Next update:* "We will issue the next update at [timestamp]."

**Required TCEQ language:** TCEQ prescribes specific notice text. Use it verbatim. Failure to use prescribed language can trigger regulatory action. The mandated text instructs residents to bring tap water to a vigorous rolling boil for two minutes, or use bottled water, for drinking, cooking, ice, brushing teeth, and washing dishes.

**Do:**
- Use TCEQ's prescribed text verbatim.
- Identify the affected area clearly (specific addresses, neighborhoods, or system-wide).
- Provide a clear "when safe" trigger (two consecutive negative samples).
- Run bilingual from the first statement.
- Coordinate with the water utility director and TCEQ regional office.
- Notify schools, dialysis centers, hospitals, restaurants, and food service operations directly.

**Don't:**
- Speculate on cause if not verified.
- Promise a timeline TCEQ has not confirmed.
- Imply the water is "probably fine" before testing clears.
- Soften the language to avoid alarm — clarity protects health.

**Pivot:** As testing progresses, updates report sampling status and any narrowing of the affected area. The all-clear statement requires TCEQ confirmation of two consecutive clean samples and includes flush-and-rinse instructions.

## 2. Gas Leak / Utility Emergency

**Trigger:** Confirmed gas leak, hazardous spill, or major utility failure requiring evacuation or shelter-in-place.

**Three-part holding statement:**

- *What we know:* "A [gas leak / hazardous condition] has been confirmed at [location] as of [timestamp]. [Affected area]. [Lead agency on scene.]"
- *What we are doing:* "[Lead utility / fire / emergency responders] are on scene. [Shelter-in-place / evacuation] is in effect for [specific area]. Residents in the affected area should [specific instruction]."
- *Next update:* "We will issue the next update at [timestamp]."

**Do:**
- Identify the lead agency clearly (utility company, fire department, HazMat).
- Provide one specific action for residents (shelter, evacuate, avoid the area).
- Identify the evacuation/shelter perimeter precisely.
- Coordinate with the gas utility's public information officer.
- Activate mass notification for the affected area.

**Don't:**
- Speculate on cause.
- Identify potential criminal or civil responsibility before investigation.
- Promise a return-to-normal time before confirmed.

**Pivot:** Updates report perimeter changes, evacuation lifts, and status of repairs. All-clear requires confirmation from the lead utility and fire department.

## 3. Cyber Incident

**Trigger:** Confirmed ransomware, data breach, system compromise affecting city services or constituent data.

**Three-part holding statement:**

- *What we know:* "The City of [City] is responding to a cybersecurity incident affecting [specific systems, if confirmed and disclosable]. [What is offline or limited.]"
- *What we are doing:* "The city is working with [law enforcement / forensic vendor / DIR] to investigate and restore service. As a precaution, [specific service] is temporarily [offline / limited]. Residents who need [service] can [alternative]."
- *Next update:* "We will issue the next update at [timestamp]."

**Do:**
- Limit initial disclosure to operational impact, not technical details.
- Coordinate with law enforcement (FBI, Texas DIR) and legal counsel before any disclosure of attacker, ransom demand, or data exposure.
- Distinguish operational outage from data exposure clearly.
- Identify alternative ways residents can access services.
- Check whether Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code Ch. 521 (Texas Identity Theft Enforcement and Protection Act) breach notification obligations apply, and Tex. Gov't Code § 2054.1125 reporting to DIR.
- Coordinate with the IT department's adopted incident response plan.

**Don't:**
- Confirm or deny ransom demand details publicly.
- Name the threat actor before law enforcement clears it.
- Disclose technical indicators that could aid the attacker or copycats.
- Promise specific recovery timelines.
- Imply data was or was not exposed before forensic confirmation.

**Pivot:** Updates report restored services and, when forensic investigation supports it, what data was or was not affected. Statutory notification to affected individuals follows separately under Ch. 521.

## 4. Severe Weather

**Trigger:** National Weather Service warning, regional emergency declaration, or local incident requiring public action.

**Three-part holding statement:**

- *What we know:* "The National Weather Service has issued a [type] warning for [area] effective [timestamp] through [timestamp]. [Conditions expected.]"
- *What we are doing:* "The City of [City] has activated [response level]. [Specific city actions — shelters open, sandbags available, road closures.]"
- *Next update:* "We will issue the next update at [timestamp]."

**Do:**
- Lead with NWS-issued language and timing.
- Identify open shelters, warming/cooling centers, evacuation routes.
- Provide specific actions for residents.
- Coordinate with county emergency management and state EOC.
- Use mass notification and Wireless Emergency Alerts where authority exists.
- Run bilingual from the first statement.

**Don't:**
- Contradict NWS guidance.
- Promise shelter capacity that cannot be confirmed.
- Identify deceased or missing persons without next-of-kin notification and ME confirmation.
- Speculate on storm path beyond NWS forecast.

**Pivot:** Updates track conditions, shelter availability, damage assessment, and recovery resources. The all-clear includes damage reporting hotline, debris collection schedule, and federal/state disaster declaration status if applicable.

## 5. Officer-Involved Incident

**Trigger:** Officer-involved shooting, in-custody death, or use-of-force incident under active investigation.

**Three-part holding statement:**

- *What we know:* "An officer-involved incident occurred at [location] at approximately [time]. [Confirmed, disclosable facts only — e.g., one person was transported to a hospital.]"
- *What we are doing:* "[Texas Rangers / outside agency / internal affairs] is conducting the investigation in accordance with the department's adopted critical incident protocol. [Involved officer / officers] [is/are] on administrative leave as standard procedure. The scene remains closed; please avoid the area."
- *Next update:* "We will issue the next update at [timestamp]."

**Do:**
- Coordinate every word with the chief, the City Attorney, and the investigating agency.
- Confirm next-of-kin notification before any identifying information is released.
- Identify the investigating agency clearly (Texas Rangers, county sheriff, or internal affairs).
- Acknowledge that an independent investigation is occurring.
- State the involved officers' administrative leave status as procedural.

**Don't:**
- Name involved officers in the initial statement.
- Characterize the incident as justified or unjustified.
- Release video before the investigating agency's release schedule.
- Release the decedent's name before next-of-kin notification.
- Speculate on cause of death, motive, or sequence of events.
- Engage with social media speculation in-thread.

**Pivot:** Subsequent releases follow the investigating agency's schedule. Body camera footage release is governed by Tex. Occ. Code Ch. 1701 and department policy. The case may move to a grand jury or prosecutor's office; further public statements are typically limited until then.

## General Patterns

**Three-part discipline.** Every holding statement: what we know, what we are doing, next update. Anything outside those three parts is risk.

**Cadence.** Even if there is nothing new, issue an update on the scheduled cadence with that fact. "We have no new information to report. The next update will be at [time]." Silence creates speculation.

**Single source of truth.** One spokesperson, one URL, one social account designated as primary. Cross-link other accounts to the primary.

**Document everything.** Every draft, every approval, every social post, every comment moderation action is a public record. Treat the after-action review as a public document.

**Bilingual is operational, not optional.** In a jurisdiction with significant Spanish-speaking residents, English-only crisis comms means residents who could not protect themselves. Maintain a 24/7 bilingual review roster.

**Practice.** The first time staff uses a crisis comms package should not be during a crisis. Annual tabletop exercises improve performance more than any template ever will.
