# Example: Planning Staff Report — Case Z-2026-04

This is a worked example for the staff-report-writer skill. It uses the fictional City of Cedar Ridge, TX. This report pairs with the zoning-narrative-writer example (the substantive narrative) and the public-hearing-notice example (the three-part notice package). Together the three documents form the case file for Item 5b on the June 9, 2026 City Council regular meeting agenda.

## Input

Same case as the zoning-narrative-writer example: rezoning at 1100 Pecan Street from R-1 to NC-1. The Planning and Zoning Commission heard the case on May 12, 2026 and recommended approval with conditions by a 5-1 vote. The case is now before City Council.

## Output

```
PLANNING STAFF REPORT
City of Cedar Ridge, Texas
Department of Planning and Development Services

COVER PAGE
==========
Case Number:           Z-2026-04
Hearing Body:          Cedar Ridge City Council
Hearing Date:          Tuesday, June 9, 2026, 6:30 p.m.
Applicant:             Magnolia Holdings, LLC
Property:              1100 Pecan Street; Lot 7, Block 4,
                       Pecan Grove Addition; 0.58 acres
Current Zoning:        R-1 Single-Family Residential
Proposed Zoning:       NC-1 Neighborhood Commercial
Request:               Change in zoning to allow conversion
                       of the existing 1948 residence to a
                       1,800 sf coffee shop and bakery with
                       12 parking spaces.
P&Z Recommendation:    Approval with conditions, 5-1, May 12,
                       2026.
Staff Recommendation:  Approval with conditions.

BODY
====

1. BACKGROUND

The subject property is a 0.58-acre lot at 1100 Pecan Street
on the west side of Pecan Street between Magnolia Avenue and
11th Street. The property is improved with a 1,820-square-foot
single-family residence built in 1948, identified as a
contributing resource in the city's 2019 historic resources
inventory. The structure has been vacant since 2023. The
applicant, Magnolia Holdings, LLC, acquired the property in
February 2026 and proposes adaptive reuse of the structure as
a neighborhood-serving coffee shop and bakery with twelve
off-street parking spaces in a paved lot to the rear.

The property has been zoned R-1 since the city's original 1969
comprehensive zoning ordinance. A 2009 rezoning approved the
NC-1 strip on the east side of Pecan Street (Case Z-2009-22).
No prior rezoning has been requested for the subject property.

The applicant filed Case Z-2026-04 on March 6, 2026. Staff
held neighborhood meetings on April 15 and April 29. The
Planning and Zoning Commission heard the case on May 12, 2026
and voted 5-1 to recommend approval with the conditions
identified in Section 7 below.

2. APPLICABLE REGULATIONS

The case proceeds under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 211 and the
Cedar Ridge Code of Ordinances Chapter 24 (Zoning), Article V
(Zoning Map Amendments).

Substantive criteria for a map amendment under Cedar Ridge
Code § 24-503:
   (1) Conformance with the adopted comprehensive plan.
   (2) Compatibility with surrounding land uses.
   (3) Adequacy of public facilities.
   (4) Effect on traffic circulation and access.
   (5) Effect on property values.
   (6) Any other factor the body determines is relevant.

Comprehensive plan reference: Cedar Ridge 2035 Comprehensive
Plan, adopted April 2023, designates the property as
Neighborhood Mixed Use on the Future Land Use Map.

Notice given under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 211.007: newspaper
publication in the Cedar Ridge Gazette on May 22, 2026 (18
days before the hearing); mailed notice to 27 owners of
property within 200 feet on May 19, 2026 (21 days before the
hearing); sign posted on the property on May 19, 2026 under
Cedar Ridge Code § 24-303(c).

Protest petition under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 211.006(d): as
of the date of this report, no written protest meeting the
20% threshold has been filed with the City Secretary.

3. SURROUNDING CONTEXT

   North:  Single-family residence on R-1 (1098 Pecan Street).
   South:  Single-family residence on R-1 (1102 Pecan Street).
   East:   NC-1 strip across Pecan Street with three small
           service uses (dry cleaner, insurance agency, barber
           shop), constructed 2009.
   West:   MF-2 multi-family apartments fronting Magnolia
           Avenue with a 6-foot masonry screening wall at the
           shared rear property line.

4. STAFF ANALYSIS

The proposed NC-1 zoning is the closest implementing zone to
the Neighborhood Mixed Use designation on the adopted
comprehensive plan's Future Land Use Map. The Neighborhood
Mixed Use designation contemplates small-scale retail and
food and beverage uses serving the surrounding neighborhood,
located on collector streets and sized to function as
walkable destinations.

The proposed use is low intensity. ITE Trip Generation,
Edition 11, indicates that a small specialty retail use of
this size generates roughly 180 daily trips. The 2024
average daily traffic count on Pecan Street is 3,400 vehicles
per day; the planning-level capacity of the section is
approximately 12,000. Sight distance at the proposed
driveway meets the city design standard. No traffic impact
analysis is required under the city's TIA thresholds.

Water and sewer capacity are adequate per the 2022 system
update. A grease interceptor will be required at building
permit.

The 25-foot landscaped buffer required under NC-1 where it
abuts R-1 will be required on the north and south property
lines. The buffer, combined with the proposed hours of
operation (6 a.m. to 7 p.m.), addresses compatibility with
the abutting R-1 residences. The applicant has offered a
condition prohibiting a drive-through; staff supports the
condition because a drive-through would change the use
intensity and traffic pattern in a way inconsistent with
the walkable destination contemplated by the comp plan.

Adaptive reuse of the 1948 contributing structure is a
positive factor under Section 7.4 of the Cedar Ridge 2035
plan, which directs the city to preserve historic
neighborhood character where possible.

Public input has been mixed but not adverse. The department
received 14 written comments: 8 in support and 6 in
opposition. The opposition comments centered on parking
spillover and early-morning delivery activity, both of
which are addressable by condition. No protest petition has
been filed.

5. FINDINGS

   5a. Conformance with the Comprehensive Plan. The request
       conforms to the Neighborhood Mixed Use designation on
       the Cedar Ridge 2035 Future Land Use Map. NC-1 is the
       closest implementing zone.

   5b. Compatibility with Surrounding Land Uses. The
       proposed use is compatible with the existing NC-1
       strip across Pecan Street, is buffered from the
       abutting R-1 residences by the 25-foot landscape
       buffer required under NC-1, and operates within
       hours that limit nighttime impact on adjoining
       residences. The masonry wall on the MF-2 boundary
       provides separation to the west.

   5c. Adequacy of Public Facilities. Water, sewer, and
       roadway capacity are adequate for the proposed use.
       Stormwater compliance will be addressed at site
       plan.

   5d. Effect on Traffic Circulation and Access. The
       expected trip generation is below the city's
       traffic impact analysis threshold. Driveway sight
       distance meets the design standard.

   5e. Effect on Property Values. No evidence of likely
       diminution in property values has been presented.
       The NC-1 pattern across Pecan Street has coexisted
       with R-1 since 2009 without an observable adverse
       effect.

   5f. Other Factors. The 1948 contributing structure is
       preserved through adaptive reuse, which advances
       Section 7.4 of the Cedar Ridge 2035 plan.

6. RECOMMENDATION

Staff recommends approval of Case Z-2026-04 with the
conditions in Section 7 below. The Planning and Zoning
Commission has recommended the same.

7. CONDITIONS

   (a) No drive-through service is permitted.
   (b) Hours of operation are limited to 6:00 a.m. through
       7:00 p.m. daily.
   (c) The 25-foot landscaped buffer required under NC-1
       along the north and south property lines must be
       installed before issuance of a certificate of
       occupancy.
   (d) Refuse collection must occur from the rear alley, in
       a screened enclosure meeting NC-1 standards.

8. ATTACHMENTS

   Exhibit A: Vicinity map
   Exhibit B: Application and applicant narrative
   Exhibit C: Staff narrative (full)
   Exhibit D: Cedar Ridge 2035 Future Land Use Map excerpt
   Exhibit E: Three-part public hearing notice package
              (Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 211.007)
   Exhibit F: Mailing list of 27 owners within 200 feet
   Exhibit G: P&Z Commission minutes of May 12, 2026
   Exhibit H: Public comment log (14 comments received)
   Exhibit I: Concept site plan showing 12-space parking lot
              and 25-foot buffers

PREPARED BY:     Adaeze Okafor, AICP, Planning Director
                 June 2, 2026

REVIEWED BY:     Daniel Ahn, City Manager
                 June 3, 2026

AI-assisted draft, reviewed by Planning Director Adaeze Okafor.
```

## Notes for the User

- Verify the P&Z minutes have been approved before they are attached as Exhibit G; if not yet approved, attach the unapproved minutes and so identify them.
- Confirm the publisher's affidavit from the Cedar Ridge Gazette and the USPS Form 3877 for the mailed notices are in the case file before the hearing begins.
- If a protest petition is filed between the date of this report and the close of the hearing, the staff supplement should be filed on the dais identifying the threshold count and the resulting supermajority requirement.
- If Council adopts conditions different from those in Section 7, the conditions stated in the rezoning ordinance must match what Council adopted on the record.
