# Example: Site Plan Review Memo — Live Oak Crossing Mixed-Use

This is a worked example for the site-plan-summary skill. It uses the fictional City of Cedar Ridge, TX.

## Input

- **Case:** SP-2026-11
- **Project name:** Live Oak Crossing
- **Property:** 800 Live Oak Drive, Cedar Ridge, TX 75000; Lots 1 and 2, Block A, Live Oak Commercial Addition; 2.40 acres
- **Zoning:** MU-2 Mixed Use, Medium Intensity
- **Proposed use:** Mixed-use building with 12,000 sf of ground-floor retail and 32 residential units on two upper floors; underground parking with surface overflow
- **Applicant:** Live Oak Crossing Development, LLC, c/o Stoneridge Capital Partners
- **Engineer of record:** Foster Engineering Group; Texas P.E. Hannah Foster, License #98221
- **Sheets received:** C-1 (cover), C-2 (existing conditions), C-3 (site plan), C-4 (grading and drainage), C-5 (utility plan), C-6 (landscape plan), C-7 (photometric plan), L-1 (tree preservation), A-2 (elevations)
- **Building footprint:** 18,400 sf; 3 stories; 42 feet to top of parapet
- **Parking proposed:** 64 underground spaces, 24 surface spaces; 88 total
- **MU-2 dimensional standards:** Front 10 ft min, side 0 ft (5 ft if abutting residential), rear 10 ft, height 45 ft max, lot coverage 70% max, no FAR cap, density up to 30 du/acre by right
- **Parking requirement:** Retail 1 per 300 sf gross; residential 1.25 per dwelling unit; on-site shared parking reduction up to 15% permitted by ordinance
- **Public input:** Pre-application neighborhood meeting March 18, 2026; eight attendees; no formal opposition

## Output

```
SITE PLAN REVIEW MEMO

CASE NUMBER:       SP-2026-11
PROJECT NAME:      Live Oak Crossing
PROPERTY:          800 Live Oak Drive, Cedar Ridge, TX 75000
                   Lots 1 and 2, Block A, Live Oak Commercial
                   Addition; 2.40 acres
ZONING:            MU-2 Mixed Use, Medium Intensity
PROPOSED USE:      12,000 sf ground-floor retail and 32
                   residential units on two upper floors
APPLICANT:         Live Oak Crossing Development, LLC

1. PROJECT SUMMARY

The applicant proposes a three-story, 18,400-square-foot
building with 12,000 square feet of ground-floor retail and
32 residential units on the second and third floors at 800
Live Oak Drive. The 2.40-acre site sits two blocks west of
Cedar Ridge City Hall on the south side of Live Oak Drive
between 8th Street and 9th Street. The project is the second
mixed-use submittal under the MU-2 zoning adopted in 2024 and
is consistent with the comprehensive plan's Activity Center
designation for this corridor.

2. ZONING COMPLIANCE TABLE

   | Standard            | Required        | Proposed         | Status     |
   |---------------------|-----------------|------------------|------------|
   | Front setback       | 10 ft minimum   | 12 ft            | Compliant  |
   | Side setback (E)    | 0 ft            | 8 ft             | Compliant  |
   | Side setback (W)    | 5 ft (abuts R)  | 14 ft            | Compliant  |
   | Rear setback        | 10 ft           | 22 ft            | Compliant  |
   | Maximum height      | 45 ft           | 42 ft            | Compliant  |
   | Maximum lot cover   | 70%             | 17.6%            | Compliant  |
   | Density (residential) | 30 du/acre    | 13.3 du/acre     | Compliant  |

3. PARKING ANALYSIS

   3a. Required by use
       Retail:      12,000 sf / 300 sf per space  = 40 spaces
       Residential: 32 units x 1.25               = 40 spaces
       Subtotal                                    = 80 spaces

   3b. Shared parking reduction
       Up to 15% permitted by ordinance based on
       mixed-use peak overlap analysis:
       Reduction (15% x 80)                        = 12 spaces
       Adjusted required                           = 68 spaces

   3c. Provided
       Underground: 64 spaces
       Surface: 24 spaces
       Total provided                              = 88 spaces

   3d. Compliance status
       Provided (88) exceeds adjusted required (68). Compliant.
       Eight surface spaces are designated short-term retail
       loading; four are van-accessible per ADA standards.

4. LANDSCAPING COMPLIANCE

   4a. Street trees
       Required: 1 tree per 30 linear feet of street frontage.
       Frontage on Live Oak Drive: 320 ft; required 11 trees.
       Provided 12 cedar elms, 3-inch caliper, at 28 ft on
       center. Compliant.

   4b. Parking lot interior
       Required: 1 tree per 10 surface spaces; 24 spaces
       require 3 trees. Provided 4 cedar elms in two landscape
       islands. Compliant.

   4c. Perimeter buffers
       Required: 10-ft landscaped buffer where the property
       abuts the R-2 single-family district on the west side.
       Provided 14-ft buffer with a 6-ft masonry wall and a
       continuous row of wax myrtle shrubs at 4 ft on center.
       Compliant.

   4d. Tree preservation
       Tree survey identifies two protected post oaks (24 in.
       and 28 in. caliper) on site. The 24-inch tree is
       preserved in the front landscape island. The 28-inch
       tree is shown to be removed for the underground parking
       ramp. Mitigation required under the city's tree
       ordinance: 28 in. caliper inches removed x 1:1
       replacement; provided as 14 two-inch caliper additions
       in the parking lot and rear yard. Compliant.

5. DRAINAGE AND STORMWATER

The site drains to the Pecan Creek watershed. Pre-development
runoff is 6.4 cubic feet per second for the 100-year design
storm. Post-development unmitigated runoff is 11.2 cfs.
On-site underground detention is proposed, sized for the
100-year storm to release at the pre-development rate. The
detention design is sealed by the engineer of record.
Compliant with the city drainage manual.

6. ACCESS AND CIRCULATION

Two driveways are proposed on Live Oak Drive. The east
driveway is 220 ft from the 9th Street intersection; the west
driveway is 180 ft from the 8th Street intersection. Both
exceed the 150-ft minimum spacing standard. Internal
circulation provides a clear fire lane loop with 26-ft minimum
width and turning radii meeting the Cedar Ridge Fire
Department aerial apparatus standard. The Fire Marshal signed
off on May 22, 2026.

7. PHOTOMETRIC / LIGHTING

Maximum footcandle at the west property line abutting R-2 is
0.3 fc; ordinance limit is 0.5 fc. All fixtures are
full-cutoff per the dark-sky overlay applicable east of
Cedar Hill Road. Compliant.

8. VARIANCES OR WAIVERS REQUESTED

None.

9. RECOMMENDED CONDITIONS OF APPROVAL

   (a) The 28-inch post oak shown for removal must be field-
       verified by the City Arborist before tree removal
       permit issuance.
   (b) The eight short-term retail loading spaces must be
       signed and striped consistently with the loading zone
       standard.
   (c) An on-site mixed-use parking management plan must be
       filed with Planning before certificate of occupancy
       for the residential units.

10. STAFF RECOMMENDATION

Staff recommends approval of Site Plan SP-2026-11 with the
three conditions stated in Section 9. The site plan complies
with MU-2 dimensional, parking, landscape, drainage, access,
and lighting standards. No variances are required.

PREPARED BY:       Adaeze Okafor, AICP, Planning Director
                   May 28, 2026

DRC REVIEW DATE:   June 4, 2026

AI-assisted draft, reviewed by Planning Director Adaeze Okafor.
```

## Notes for the User

- Confirm with the engineer of record that the underground detention design has been peer-reviewed by the city engineer.
- The mixed-use parking management plan condition is critical to keeping the shared parking reduction defensible.
- This case may be administratively approved under MU-2 since no variances are requested; confirm the local code section that governs the approval pathway.
