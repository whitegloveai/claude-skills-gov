# Example: Zoning Narrative — Case Z-2026-04, 1100 Pecan Street

This is a worked example for the zoning-narrative-writer skill. It uses the fictional City of Cedar Ridge, TX, and pairs with the council-ops agenda-builder example (where this case appears as Item 5b on the June 9, 2026 regular agenda), the public-hearing-notice example, and the staff-report-writer example.

## Input

- **Case:** Z-2026-04
- **Applicant:** Magnolia Holdings, LLC (owner-applicant)
- **Property:** 1100 Pecan Street, Cedar Ridge, TX 75000; Lot 7, Block 4, Pecan Grove Addition; 0.58 acres
- **Current zoning:** R-1 Single-Family Residential
- **Proposed zoning:** NC-1 Neighborhood Commercial
- **Applicant's reason:** Convert a former single-family residence built in 1948 to a neighborhood coffee shop and bakery (roughly 1,800 sf interior, 12-space parking lot)
- **Comp plan designation:** Neighborhood Mixed Use (Cedar Ridge 2035 Future Land Use Map)
- **Surrounding context:** R-1 single-family north and south; NC-1 small retail strip across Pecan Street to the east (built 2009); MF-2 apartments west on Magnolia Avenue
- **Infrastructure:** 8-inch water main and 8-inch gravity sewer on Pecan Street with adequate capacity; Pecan Street classified as a minor collector; 2024 average daily traffic 3,400 vehicles per day
- **Public input:** Two neighborhood meetings (April 15 and April 29); 14 emailed comments (8 in support, 6 in opposition); no written protest petition filed as of the date of this narrative

## Output

```
CASE NUMBER:    Z-2026-04
APPLICANT:      Magnolia Holdings, LLC
PROPERTY:       1100 Pecan Street, Cedar Ridge, TX 75000
                Lot 7, Block 4, Pecan Grove Addition
                0.58 acres
REQUEST:        Rezone from R-1 Single-Family Residential to NC-1
                Neighborhood Commercial

1. PROPERTY AND REQUEST SUMMARY

The subject property is a 0.58-acre lot on the west side of Pecan
Street between Magnolia Avenue and 11th Street. The property is
improved with a 1,820-square-foot single-family residence built in
1948 and currently vacant. The applicant proposes to rezone the
property from R-1 Single-Family Residential to NC-1 Neighborhood
Commercial to allow conversion of the structure to a coffee shop
and bakery with twelve off-street parking spaces.

2. COMPREHENSIVE PLAN CONFORMANCE

The Cedar Ridge 2035 Comprehensive Plan, adopted April 2023,
designates the property as Neighborhood Mixed Use on the Future
Land Use Map. The Neighborhood Mixed Use designation contemplates
small-scale retail, service, and food and beverage uses that serve
the surrounding neighborhood, located on collector streets and
sized to function as walkable destinations from adjoining
residential blocks. The proposed NC-1 zoning is the closest
implementing zone to the Neighborhood Mixed Use designation, with
use limitations and a 25-foot landscaped buffer requirement where
NC-1 abuts a residential district. The request conforms to the
adopted plan.

3. SURROUNDING LAND USES

   North:  Single-family residence on R-1 zoning (1098 Pecan
           Street); detached 1950s home, owner-occupied.
   South:  Single-family residence on R-1 zoning (1102 Pecan
           Street); detached 1960s home, owner-occupied.
   East:   Neighborhood Commercial strip (NC-1) across Pecan
           Street, including a dry cleaner, an insurance agency,
           and a barber shop; constructed 2009.
   West:   MF-2 multi-family apartments fronting Magnolia Avenue;
           the rear of the apartment property abuts the rear lot
           line of the subject property with a six-foot masonry
           screening wall in place.

4. COMPATIBILITY ANALYSIS

The proposed use is low intensity. A coffee shop and bakery of
approximately 1,800 square feet generates traffic comparable to a
small specialty retail use under ITE Trip Generation, Edition 11.
Hours of operation in the application are 6:00 a.m. to 7:00 p.m.,
which limits late-evening activity adjacent to the abutting R-1
properties. The 25-foot landscaped buffer required under NC-1
where it abuts an R-1 district will be required on the north and
south lot lines and is sufficient to provide visual separation. No
drive-through is proposed; staff recommends a condition prohibiting
a drive-through to preserve the walkable character contemplated by
the comp plan. Lighting will be subject to the city's full-cutoff
fixture standard. Refuse collection is proposed in a screened
enclosure off the rear alley, which keeps service activity off
Pecan Street.

5. PUBLIC INFRASTRUCTURE IMPACT

   5a. Water and sanitary sewer
       The 8-inch water main and 8-inch gravity sewer on Pecan
       Street have adequate capacity for the proposed use.
       Domestic and fire flow modeling at the most recent system
       update (2022) indicates available pressure and flow
       exceeding the demand projected for the conversion. A
       grease interceptor will be required at building permit
       under the city's plumbing code.

   5b. Transportation
       Pecan Street is a minor collector with a 2024 average
       daily traffic count of 3,400 vehicles per day, well below
       the planning-level capacity of approximately 12,000 for
       the section. The proposed use is expected to add
       approximately 180 daily trips, with a small a.m. peak.
       No traffic impact analysis is required under the city's
       TIA thresholds. Sight distance at the proposed driveway
       meets the city's design standards.

   5c. Stormwater
       The site is within the Pecan Creek drainage area. The
       proposed parking lot adds approximately 4,200 square feet
       of impervious cover. The applicant will be required to
       provide on-site detention or pay a fee in lieu under the
       city's drainage ordinance at the site plan stage.

   5d. Schools
       Not applicable; the proposed use is non-residential.

6. PUBLIC INPUT SUMMARY

Staff held two neighborhood meetings on April 15 and April 29 at
the Cedar Ridge Senior Center. Approximately 24 residents attended
across both meetings. The department received 14 written comments
between the application filing date and the date of this
narrative: 8 in support and 6 in opposition. Comments in support
emphasized walkability and the preservation of the existing
historic structure. Comments in opposition emphasized parking
spillover onto Pecan Street and concerns about delivery truck
activity in the early morning. No written protest petition meeting
the 20% threshold under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 211.006(d) has been
filed as of the date of this narrative.

7. FINDINGS

   7a. Conformance with the Comprehensive Plan
       The request conforms to the Neighborhood Mixed Use
       designation on the Cedar Ridge 2035 Future Land Use Map.
       NC-1 is the closest implementing zone.

   7b. Compatibility with Surrounding Land Uses
       The proposed use is compatible with the existing NC-1
       strip across Pecan Street, is buffered from the abutting
       R-1 properties by the NC-1 25-foot landscape requirement,
       and operates within hours that limit nighttime impact on
       adjoining residences.

   7c. Adequacy of Public Facilities
       Water, sewer, and roadway capacity are adequate for the
       proposed use. Stormwater compliance will be addressed at
       site plan.

   7d. Effect on Traffic Circulation and Access
       The expected trip generation is below the threshold that
       would trigger a traffic impact analysis. Driveway sight
       distance meets the design standard.

   7e. Effect on Property Values
       No evidence of likely diminution in property values has
       been presented. The pattern of NC-1 across Pecan Street
       has coexisted with R-1 on the west side of the street
       since 2009 without an observable adverse effect.

   7f. Other Factors
       The 1948 structure is identified as a contributing
       resource in the city's 2019 historic resources inventory.
       The proposed adaptive reuse preserves the structure,
       which staff treats as a positive factor.

8. RECOMMENDATION

Staff recommends approval of Case Z-2026-04 with the following
conditions:

   (a) No drive-through service is permitted.
   (b) Hours of operation are limited to 6:00 a.m. through
       7:00 p.m. daily.
   (c) The 25-foot landscaped buffer required under NC-1 along
       the north and south property lines must be installed
       before issuance of a certificate of occupancy.
   (d) Refuse collection must occur from the rear alley, in a
       screened enclosure meeting NC-1 standards.

PREPARED BY:    Adaeze Okafor, AICP, Planning Director
                May 30, 2026

REVIEWED BY:    Daniel Ahn, City Manager
                June 2, 2026

AI-assisted draft, reviewed by Planning Director Adaeze Okafor.
```

## Notes for the User

- Confirm mailed notice was sent to property owners within 200 feet at least 10 days before the June 9 hearing.
- Confirm newspaper publication ran at least 15 days before the hearing per § 211.007.
- Track any protest petition through the close of the hearing and report the count on the record.
- If the conditions are accepted by the applicant, attach them to the rezoning ordinance; if not, restate the conditions in the ordinance and document the basis for each.
