# Texas Zoning Procedure Outline (Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 211)

This is a working outline for planners assembling a zoning case file. It is a quick reference, not legal advice. Route close questions to the city attorney.

## Authority to Zone

Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 211.001 authorizes home-rule and general-law municipalities to regulate land use through zoning to promote public health, safety, morals, and general welfare. The city's adopted zoning ordinance is the substantive instrument; Chapter 211 supplies the procedural floor.

## Planning and Zoning Commission Recommendation

Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 211.006 requires the governing body to appoint a zoning commission (commonly called the Planning and Zoning Commission, or P&Z) to recommend boundaries for the original zoning districts and any subsequent amendments. The city may not adopt a zoning regulation or amend a district map until the commission has made its recommendation, after a public hearing.

The commission's recommendation is not binding on council, but it is procedurally required. A council vote without the commission recommendation is voidable.

## Notice of Public Hearing

Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 211.007 governs notice for the public hearing before the governing body:

- Published in an official newspaper of general circulation in the municipality at least 15 days before the hearing.
- Written notice mailed to each owner of real property within 200 feet of the property on which the change is proposed, as the ownership appears on the most recently approved municipal tax roll. Mailed notice must be sent at least 10 days before the hearing.
- For an initial comprehensive zoning ordinance, mailed notice is not required.

Local ordinances frequently extend the notice radius beyond 200 feet or add a posted-sign requirement on the property itself. Comply with the more stringent local rule.

## Protest Petition — Supermajority Trigger

Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 211.006(d) requires a three-fourths affirmative vote of all members of the governing body to approve a rezoning when a written protest is filed by:

- The owners of at least 20% of the area of lots or land covered by the proposed change; or
- The owners of at least 20% of the area of lots or land immediately adjoining the area covered by the proposed change and extending 200 feet from that area.

The protest must be in writing and signed. The city clerk or planning department typically receives, time-stamps, and verifies signatures against the tax roll. Staff should count the threshold carefully before the hearing and report the count on the record.

## Conduct of the Hearing

The hearing should be transcribed or recorded. A typical sequence:

1. Staff presentation of the case, the narrative, and the recommendation.
2. Applicant presentation.
3. Public testimony in favor.
4. Public testimony in opposition.
5. Applicant rebuttal.
6. Close of the public hearing.
7. Commission or council deliberation and motion.

Members may ask questions of staff and the applicant. The hearing should not be closed until all who wish to speak have spoken or the time allotted has expired.

## Findings of Fact and Conclusions

The substantive criteria are supplied by the local zoning ordinance. Many Texas cities adopt criteria similar to:

- Conformance with the comprehensive plan.
- Compatibility with surrounding land uses.
- Adequacy of public facilities (water, sewer, transportation, schools).
- Effect on traffic circulation and access.
- Effect on property values.
- Any other factor the body determines is relevant.

Findings should be specific. A finding that says only "the request is compatible" is not a finding. A finding that says "the request is compatible because the proposed NC-1 zoning permits limited retail uses with a 25-foot landscaped buffer required where the property abuts R-1, and the existing R-1 properties to the south retain access from a separate street" is a finding.

## Judicial Review

Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 211.011 provides for judicial review of zoning decisions by writ of certiorari to a district court of competent jurisdiction. The writ must be presented to the court within 10 days after the decision. The court does not reweigh the evidence; it reviews the record for legality. The record will include the staff narrative, the notice, the minutes, and any exhibits introduced at the hearing.

## Vested Rights

Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 245 protects an applicant's rights under the regulations in effect when the first complete application in a project is filed. Be careful what staff treats as a "complete" application for vested rights purposes — the determination affects which version of the zoning regulations governs the case.

## Fair Housing Considerations

Zoning decisions that affect group homes, supportive housing, transitional housing, or other uses serving a protected class under 42 U.S.C. § 3601 et seq. carry Fair Housing Act exposure. Even facially neutral zoning regulations may be challenged on disparate impact grounds. Coordinate with the city attorney early on any case touching residential uses for a protected population.

## Special-Use Permits and Planned Developments

Many cities use special-use permits (SUPs) and planned developments (PDs) to grant conditional approvals or negotiate site-specific standards. Procedurally these are zoning amendments and follow Chapter 211. Substantively the local ordinance supplies the criteria. SUP and PD conditions must be reasonably related to the impacts of the use; arbitrary or unrelated conditions invite challenge.

## Practical Checklist for Staff

- Confirm the case type and the controlling ordinance section.
- Confirm the comp plan future land use designation.
- Pull the tax roll list of owners within 200 feet (or the local extended radius).
- Confirm mailing date is at least 10 days before the hearing; newspaper publication at least 15 days before the hearing.
- Track any protest petition and verify the 20% threshold against the tax roll.
- Prepare the staff narrative, findings, and recommendation.
- Confirm the P&Z recommendation is on the record before the council hearing.
- Preserve the record for any § 211.011 writ of certiorari that may follow.
