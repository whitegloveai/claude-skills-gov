# Example: Variance Analysis — Side-Yard Setback on a Corner Lot

This is a worked example for the variance-request-analyzer skill. It uses the fictional City of Cedar Ridge, TX.

## Input

- **Case:** V-2026-07
- **Property:** 503 Hickory Lane, Cedar Ridge, TX 75000; Lot 1, Block 12, Hickory Hollow Addition (corner of Hickory Lane and 5th Street); 7,200 square feet; rectangular
- **Zoning:** R-1 Single-Family Residential
- **Applicant:** Sandra and Marcus Lin (owner-applicants)
- **Code section being varied:** Cedar Ridge Code of Ordinances § 24-205(b), R-1 dimensional standards — front-yard setback. Corner lots are required to maintain a front-yard setback on both street frontages.
- **Requested deviation:** Reduce the front-yard setback on the 5th Street frontage from 25 feet to 20 feet, a 5-foot reduction, to allow construction of a 280-square-foot covered porch on the existing residence
- **Hardship claim:** Corner lot with two front yards under the code; the buildable footprint is approximately 18% smaller than a comparable interior lot in the same block, all of which have only one front yard
- **Surrounding context:** Block 12 contains 11 lots; the subject lot is the only corner lot and the only lot subject to two front-yard setbacks; the other 10 lots front Hickory Lane only and are not subject to the 5th Street setback
- **Public input:** Three mailed comments received; all three in support; no opposition
- **Hardship not self-created:** Lot configuration is original to the 1962 subdivision plat; not the result of any action by the current owners

## Output

```
VARIANCE ANALYSIS MEMO

CASE NUMBER:       V-2026-07
PROPERTY:          503 Hickory Lane, Cedar Ridge, TX 75000
                   Lot 1, Block 12, Hickory Hollow Addition
                   7,200 sf; corner of Hickory Lane and 5th
                   Street
ZONING:            R-1 Single-Family Residential
APPLICANT:         Sandra and Marcus Lin
HEARING BODY:      Zoning Board of Adjustment
HEARING DATE:      June 18, 2026

1. REQUEST SUMMARY

The applicants request a variance from Cedar Ridge Code of
Ordinances § 24-205(b) to reduce the front-yard setback on
the 5th Street frontage of a corner lot from 25 feet to 20
feet, a 5-foot reduction, to allow construction of a
280-square-foot covered porch on the existing residence.
The front-yard setback on Hickory Lane (the primary street
frontage) is not affected.

2. PROPERTY CHARACTERISTICS

The subject property is a rectangular 7,200-square-foot lot
at the southeast corner of Hickory Lane and 5th Street,
platted in 1962 as Lot 1, Block 12, Hickory Hollow Addition.
The lot has 80 feet of frontage on Hickory Lane and 90 feet
of frontage on 5th Street. The existing 1,650-square-foot
one-story residence was constructed in 1964 and sits at the
required 25-foot setback from both street frontages, leaving
a buildable rear and interior side yard area but no
buildable area in either front yard. The lot is the only
corner lot in Block 12.

3. APPLICABLE STANDARDS

Cedar Ridge Code of Ordinances § 24-205(b) requires a
25-foot front-yard setback in R-1, applied to both street
frontages of a corner lot. The standard is dimensional, not
use-related; the proposed porch is a permitted accessory
structure in R-1.

4. FINDINGS ON EACH STATUTORY CRITERION

   4a. Special conditions unique to the property and not
       self-created (Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 211.009(a)(3)
       and Cedar Ridge Code § 24-321(c)(1))

       Finding: The criterion is met. The property is a
       corner lot subject to a 25-foot front-yard setback on
       each of two street frontages. The remaining 10 lots
       in Block 12 are interior lots fronting Hickory Lane
       only and are subject to a 25-foot setback on only one
       frontage. The corner-lot configuration is original to
       the 1962 plat and was not created by any action of
       the current owners. The buildable footprint of the
       subject property is approximately 18% smaller than
       comparable interior lots in the same block as a
       direct result of this configuration.

   4b. Literal interpretation would deprive the applicant of
       rights commonly enjoyed by others in the same
       district

       Finding: The criterion is met. Other R-1 lots in the
       district can construct a 280-square-foot covered
       porch within their buildable area. The subject
       property cannot, because two front-yard setbacks
       constrain the buildable area. Literal application of
       the 25-foot setback on the 5th Street frontage
       denies the applicants the ability to build a porch
       that interior lots in the same district can build.

   4c. Variance does not confer a special privilege

       Finding: The criterion is met. The variance allows
       the applicants to achieve a buildable footprint
       comparable to interior lots, not greater than. The
       porch dimension and position (20-foot setback on the
       5th Street frontage; standard 25-foot setback on
       Hickory Lane) place the structure no closer to the
       street than the standard setback on interior lots.

   4d. Variance is the minimum necessary

       Finding: The criterion is met. The applicants
       requested a 5-foot reduction. A 280-square-foot porch
       at 7 feet deep requires either the 5-foot reduction
       on the 5th Street frontage or a redesign to a porch
       smaller than 200 square feet. The 5-foot reduction is
       the minimum necessary to construct the porch as
       designed.

   4e. Variance is consistent with the spirit of the
       ordinance and substantial justice will be done

       Finding: The criterion is met. The spirit of the
       R-1 setback standard is to preserve light, air, and
       front-yard character along the street. The proposed
       20-foot setback is consistent with the front-yard
       depth common in the block (the houses across 5th
       Street are set back 20 to 22 feet) and preserves the
       streetscape pattern.

5. ALTERNATIVES ANALYSIS

The applicants considered three alternatives:
   (i) A smaller porch under 200 sf that fits within the
       existing buildable area. Rejected because it does
       not accommodate two side-by-side rocking chairs and
       the front entry, which is the use the applicants
       seek.
   (ii) A rear porch in lieu of a front porch. Rejected
       because the rear yard contains an existing detached
       garage and a mature pecan tree the applicants wish
       to preserve.
   (iii) Demolition and reconstruction of the residence
       further into the lot. Rejected as
       disproportionately expensive relative to the use
       sought.

Staff concurs that the alternatives are not reasonable
substitutes given the lot's unique two-front-yard condition.

6. PUBLIC INPUT

Three written comments were received in response to the
mailed notice to property owners within 200 feet. All three
expressed support. No written opposition or protest
petition has been filed.

7. STAFF RECOMMENDATION

   [X] Approval with conditions
   [ ] Denial

Conditions:
   (a) The variance applies only to the 5th Street
       front-yard setback and only for the 280-square-foot
       covered porch as shown on the site plan dated
       April 22, 2026. Any expansion of the porch or
       relocation requires a new variance.
   (b) The 25-foot setback on the Hickory Lane frontage is
       not affected and must be maintained.
   (c) The covered porch may not be enclosed (screened or
       walled) without a separate building permit and code
       review.

Rationale tied to findings: The hardship is unique to the
corner-lot configuration, is not self-created, and would
deprive the applicants of a buildable area commonly enjoyed
by interior lots in the same district. The 5-foot reduction
is the minimum necessary and is consistent with the
front-yard pattern across 5th Street. All five statutory
criteria are met.

PREPARED BY:       Adaeze Okafor, AICP, Planning Director
DATE:              June 5, 2026

AI-assisted draft, reviewed by Planning Director Adaeze Okafor.
```

## Notes for the User

- A 75% supermajority of the ZBA is required to grant the variance under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 211.008(c). Confirm the meeting will have a quorum and the supermajority is available.
- The ZBA decision is appealable by writ of certiorari to district court within 10 days under § 211.011. Preserve the record.
- The conditions in Section 7 should be incorporated into the variance order issued by the ZBA.
