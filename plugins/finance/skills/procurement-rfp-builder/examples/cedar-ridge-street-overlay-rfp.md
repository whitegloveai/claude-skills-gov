# Example: FY2026 Street Overlay RFP — City of Cedar Ridge

This is a worked example for the procurement-rfp-builder skill. It uses the fictional City of Cedar Ridge, TX. This RFP produced the bidders referenced in the council-ops agenda example Item 6a (June 9, 2026 council meeting — award of the FY2026 street overlay contract).

For this procurement Cedar Ridge is electing Competitive Sealed Proposals under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 2269, which permits selection on a combination of price and qualifications for public works projects, rather than strict lowest-bidder under LGC Ch. 252. The two paths are both lawful; Cedar Ridge selected CSP because pavement quality and contractor responsiveness historically matter as much as unit price.

## Input

- **Entity:** City of Cedar Ridge, TX
- **Procurement:** FY2026 Street Overlay Program (~12 lane-miles, multiple locations)
- **Estimated budget:** $1,750,000 to $1,850,000 (Capital Projects Fund; bond-funded, no federal match)
- **Method:** Competitive Sealed Proposals (Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 2269)
- **Schedule:** advertise March 27 and April 3, 2026; proposals due April 24, 2026; award June 9, 2026 council meeting; start June 23, 2026; substantial completion September 30, 2026
- **Project:** mill-and-overlay on listed segments; full-depth repairs at deteriorated locations; pavement markings restoration

1. PROJECT OVERVIEW

repair where designated, hot-mix asphaltic concrete overlay,
miscellaneous concrete work, and pavement marking restoration on the
streets listed in Attachment A.

1.2 Background. The Program addresses approximately twelve (12) lane-
miles of street identified in the City's 2025 Pavement Condition
Index update as Pavement Condition Index 50 to 65 ("fair, declining")
and prioritized in the FY2026 Capital Improvement Plan adopted by
City Council Resolution 2025-37.

1.3 Funding Source. The Program is funded from Capital Projects Fund
proceeds of the 2024 Certificates of Obligation. No federal funds are
involved in this procurement.

2. SCOPE OF WORK

2.1 Deliverables. Successful completion of paving work on the
segments described in Attachment A, in conformance with TxDOT 2014
Standard Specifications for Construction (with Cedar Ridge
modifications in Attachment B), including:

(a) Approximately 12 lane-miles of mill (1.5 inch typical) and
overlay (1.5 inch HMAC Type D);
(b) Up to 6,000 square yards of full-depth pavement repair at
locations marked in the field by the Engineer prior to overlay;
(c) Replacement of damaged concrete curb, gutter, and sidewalk
panels within the project limits, approximately 1,200 linear feet
and 800 square feet respectively;
(d) Restoration of pavement markings (longitudinal, transverse,
legends) using thermoplastic;
(e) Adjustment of manhole and valve covers to final grade;
(f) Traffic control under TMUTCD and the City's approved temporary
traffic control standards;
(g) Construction signage and project information signs.

2.2 Performance Standards. Final lift density 92.5 percent of
theoretical maximum density (Rice). Smoothness measured by ride
quality index per TxDOT Item 585 with a 95 percent corrective work
threshold. Ride quality reports submitted within 14 days of
completion of each segment.

2.3 Period of Performance. Notice to Proceed anticipated June 23,
2026. Substantial completion: September 30, 2026. Final completion:
October 31, 2026. Liquidated damages of $750 per calendar day past
substantial completion.

Attachment A. A map of the project limits is included in
Attachment C.

3.4 References. Up to 4 pages. Five comparable Texas municipal or

3.5 Price Proposal. Up to 8 pages. Total proposed price with the

3.6 Required Forms (see Section 7). Included separately at the end

Proposals will be evaluated by a five-member committee. Scoring is

| Criterion                                          | Weight |

proposer's proposed price). The City reserves the right under Tex.
Gov't Code § 2269.155 to negotiate with the highest-ranked proposer

## Notes for the User

- This RFP uses Competitive Sealed Proposals under Tex. Gov't Code
  Ch. 2269. If the city elects Competitive Sealed Bids under LGC Ch.
  252, price becomes the sole award factor, evaluation criteria
  drops out, and the bid opening publicly reads dollar amounts.
- Bonding requirements are statutory under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 2253
  for public works over $100,000. Confirm the bond forms attached to
  the General Conditions are current.
- The advertisement schedule satisfies the once-a-week-for-two-
  consecutive-weeks rule (March 27 and April 3 — seven days apart).
- If federal funds were involved, the file would need a 2 C.F.R.
  § 200.318(i) procurement record, BABA certification, and Davis-
  Bacon wage decision insertion. Cedar Ridge confirmed no federal
  funds for this project.
- This draft was prepared with AI assistance and should be reviewed
  by the City Attorney, the Public Works Director, and the
  Purchasing Manager before advertisement.
