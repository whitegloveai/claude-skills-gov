# Uniform Guidance Summary — 2 C.F.R. Part 200

A concise reference for municipal grant managers, finance directors, and finance staff. This is not a substitute for the regulation itself, which lives at ecfr.gov. The Uniform Guidance was substantially revised effective October 1, 2024 (the "2024 OMB revisions"); cite the current version, not legacy text.

## Scope

2 C.F.R. Part 200 — the Office of Management and Budget's Uniform Administrative Requirements, Cost Principles, and Audit Requirements for Federal Awards (commonly "Uniform Guidance" or "UG") — governs almost every federal grant a Texas city will receive directly or as a subrecipient. State agencies that pass through federal funds (TxDOT, TDEM, TDA, TWDB, etc.) flow these requirements down through their grant agreements.

## Structure of Part 200

- **Subpart A.** Acronyms and definitions.
- **Subpart B.** General provisions.
- **Subpart C.** Pre-award (notice of funding opportunity, applications).
- **Subpart D.** Post-award requirements (financial and program management, property, procurement, performance, monitoring, closeout).
- **Subpart E.** Cost Principles (what costs are allowable, allocable, and reasonable).
- **Subpart F.** Audit Requirements (the Single Audit Act).
- **Appendices.** Indirect cost rates, special considerations, deviations.

## Cost principles (Subpart E)

A cost is allowable if it meets the standard at 2 C.F.R. § 200.403:

- *Necessary and reasonable* for the performance of the award.
- *Allocable* to the award.
- Conforms to *limitations or exclusions* in the principles or the award.
- Consistent with the city's policies and procedures.
- *Adequately documented*.

Selected items of cost are at §§ 200.420–200.476. Common ones that trip up municipal applicants:

- *Compensation.* § 200.430 — must be reasonable; time and effort certification required for federal awards.
- *Travel.* § 200.475 — must follow the city's written travel policy and the federal per diem framework where applicable.
- *Pre-award costs.* § 200.458 — generally unallowable without prior approval.
- *Bid and proposal costs.* § 200.460 — generally unallowable.
- *Bad debts.* § 200.426 — unallowable.
- *Entertainment.* § 200.438 — unallowable.

## Audit requirements (Subpart F — the Single Audit)

A non-federal entity that expends $1,000,000 or more in federal awards in a fiscal year must have a Single Audit under 2 C.F.R. § 200.501. (The threshold was $750,000 prior to the 2024 OMB revisions; the new threshold is $1,000,000 for fiscal years beginning on or after October 1, 2024.)

The Single Audit produces:

- Auditor's reports on financial statements and on internal controls over compliance.
- A Schedule of Expenditures of Federal Awards (SEFA).
- A Schedule of Findings and Questioned Costs.
- The Data Collection Form (SF-SAC) and the audit reporting package submitted to the Federal Audit Clearinghouse (FAC).

Findings are categorized as material weakness, significant deficiency, or other matters. Each finding requires a management response and corrective action plan documented under § 200.512(b). See the audit-response-helper skill for the format.

## Procurement standards (§§ 200.317–200.327)

Federal procurement standards apply to any procurement using federal funds. The five methods are:

- *Micro-purchase* (§ 200.320(a)) — current threshold $10,000 (subject to adjustment). No competition required if price is reasonable.
- *Small purchase* (§ 200.320(a)) — current threshold up to $250,000 (the Simplified Acquisition Threshold). Rate quotations from at least two qualified sources.
- *Sealed bids* (§ 200.320(b)(1)) — formal IFB process for construction, typically.
- *Competitive proposals* (§ 200.320(b)(2)) — RFP process when sealed bids are not appropriate.
- *Noncompetitive procurement (sole source)* (§ 200.320(c)) — only under listed circumstances (single source, public exigency, prior written approval, inadequate competition).

The non-federal entity's own procurement standards apply if they are more restrictive than the federal floor. For Texas cities, that means LGC Ch. 252 thresholds and procedures generally control where they are stricter than the federal rules. Federal-funded procurements still must layer on Build America Buy America (BABA), Section 3 (HUD), Davis-Bacon (most construction with federal funds), and other program-specific requirements.

## Subrecipients vs. contractors (§ 200.331)

The Uniform Guidance distinguishes between subrecipients (who carry out a portion of the federal program) and contractors (who provide goods or services as a vendor). The characteristics test at § 200.331(a)–(b) controls; the label in the agreement does not. Subrecipients are subject to flow-down of UG requirements and to subrecipient monitoring. Contractors are not.

## Indirect cost rates

Three options under 2 C.F.R. § 200.414:

- *Negotiated rate* with the cognizant federal agency. Required documentation, periodic renegotiation.
- *De minimis rate of 10 percent* of modified total direct costs (MTDC). The 2024 OMB revisions increased this to 15 percent for entities that have never had a negotiated rate. Available to non-federal entities that have never had a negotiated indirect cost rate, with some exceptions.
- *Negotiated rate with the pass-through entity* if the city is a subrecipient of a state agency.

## Common Texas applications

- *TxDOT TASA (Transportation Alternatives Set-Aside).* Federal funds passed through TxDOT. UG and Texas Administrative Code Title 43 apply.
- *CDBG (Community Development Block Grant).* HUD funds via Texas Department of Agriculture (TxCDBG for rural cities) or direct entitlement for larger cities.
- *FEMA Public Assistance / Hazard Mitigation Grant Program.* TDEM administered, FEMA flow-down rules.
- *EPA Brownfields / Lead Service Line.* Direct EPA grants subject to Part 200.
- *USDA Rural Development.* Direct grants and loans, Subpart E cost principles apply.

## Common pitfalls

- Treating the de minimis indirect rate as a flat-fee revenue item rather than a recovery against actual indirect costs.
- Missing subrecipient monitoring (§ 200.332) when the city subgrants federal funds to a partner.
- Failing to flow UG requirements down into subawards (§ 200.332(a)).
- Procuring federal-funded goods or services under state procurement procedures only, without confirming the federal procurement standards are met.
- Drawing down federal cash before incurring the expenditure (cash management rules at § 200.305).
