# Example: TxDOT TASA Application — Cedar Ridge Safe Routes to School Sidewalks

This is a worked example for the grant-application-drafter skill. It uses the fictional City of Cedar Ridge, TX. The example assumes a published TxDOT Transportation Alternatives Set-Aside (TASA) Notice of Funding Opportunity (NOFO).

## Input

- **Funder:** Texas Department of Transportation, Transportation Alternatives Set-Aside (TASA)
- **NOFO #:** TASA-2026-01 (Statewide; example reference only)
- **Assistance Listing:** 20.205 (Highway Planning and Construction; federal pass-through)
- **Deadline:** September 15, 2026
- **Page limit:** 12 pages narrative
- **Project:** Sidewalk and crossing improvements connecting Pecan Elementary (1804 Pecan Street) to Veterans Park (corner of Live Oak and Walnut), approximately 0.9 miles of new ADA-compliant sidewalk, three rectangular rapid-flashing beacon (RRFB) crossings, two ADA curb ramp upgrades.
- **Total project cost:** $1,062,500
- **Federal request (80 percent):** $850,000
- **Local match (20 percent):** $212,500 (Capital Projects Fund, cash, council-approved March 24, 2026)
- **Applicant:** City of Cedar Ridge, TX
- **Beneficiaries:** ~480 students at Pecan Elementary; surrounding neighborhood of ~1,200 residents
- **Partners:** Cedar Ridge ISD; Caddo County Public Health Coalition; Veterans Park Neighborhood Association

## Output (abridged — full application would be ~12 pages)

```
CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE, TEXAS
TxDOT Transportation Alternatives Set-Aside Application
NOFO TASA-2026-01 | Assistance Listing 20.205
Submitted: August 22, 2026

PROJECT TITLE: Pecan Elementary Safe Routes to School Sidewalk
              Connectivity Project
TOTAL PROJECT COST: $1,062,500
FEDERAL REQUEST: $850,000 (80%)
LOCAL MATCH: $212,500 (20%, cash, Capital Projects Fund)

NEED / PROBLEM STATEMENT

Pecan Elementary School serves 478 students in pre-kindergarten through
fifth grade in the older residential core of Cedar Ridge. The 0.9-mile
route between the school and Veterans Park (the nearest neighborhood
park) crosses two collector roads and traverses a corridor where 64
percent of sidewalk segments are either absent, sub-standard, or out of
ADA compliance, based on the city's 2025 Sidewalk Condition Assessment.

The corridor served as the site of three pedestrian-involved crashes
between 2020 and 2025, including one involving a student walking home
from school in October 2023 (TxDOT CRIS records). Twenty-eight percent
of children in the Pecan Elementary attendance zone live in households
below 200 percent of the federal poverty level (2023 American Community
Survey, five-year estimates, Census Tract 0202.01), and walking to
school is a primary mode of travel. The lack of continuous sidewalk
and the absence of high-visibility crossings on Pecan Street near the
school entrance create a daily safety risk for these students.

In May 2025 the Caddo County Public Health Coalition included the
Pecan Street corridor on its Top Five Pedestrian Risk Locations
inventory. Cedar Ridge ISD's 2026 Wellness Plan identifies the
corridor as the top sidewalk priority for the district's elementary
schools.

PROJECT APPROACH

The project will construct 0.9 miles of new five-foot ADA-compliant
concrete sidewalk on the south side of Pecan Street between Sycamore
Lane and Live Oak Drive, fill two existing sidewalk gaps on Walnut
Street, install three rectangular rapid-flashing beacons (RRFBs) at
mid-block and intersection crossings near the school, and upgrade two
ADA curb ramps at the Live Oak/Walnut intersection to current PROWAG
standards.

The project approach follows the FHWA Safe System framework: separate
people walking from vehicles, slow vehicles where conflicts occur, and
make pedestrians visible at conflict points. RRFB devices are
identified by FHWA as a proven safety countermeasure with documented
crash reductions at uncontrolled crossings.

The project has completed conceptual design through the city's
on-call engineering contract; final engineering is included in the
TASA budget request. Right-of-way is fully within existing public
right-of-way on Pecan and Walnut Streets; no acquisitions are
anticipated.

GOALS AND OBJECTIVES

Goal 1: Improve walking safety for Pecan Elementary students by
        eliminating sidewalk gaps and improving crossing visibility
        on the school-to-park corridor.

  Objective 1.1: Construct 0.9 miles of new ADA-compliant sidewalk
  connecting Pecan Elementary to Veterans Park by August 1, 2028.

  Objective 1.2: Install three RRFB crossings at high-conflict points
  identified in the corridor safety analysis by August 1, 2028.

  Objective 1.3: Increase the percentage of Pecan Elementary students
  walking or biking to school from the 22 percent baseline reported in
  the 2025 Cedar Ridge ISD Travel Survey to 32 percent within one year
  of project completion.

WORK PLAN AND TIMELINE

| Task                                | Deliverable          | Responsible | Start    | End      |
|-------------------------------------|----------------------|-------------|----------|----------|
| 1.1 Environmental review (CE)       | NEPA CE document     | City/TxDOT  | Q1 2027  | Q2 2027  |
| 1.2 Final engineering               | 100% PS&E            | City Engr   | Q2 2027  | Q4 2027  |
| 1.3 Competitive procurement (LGC    | Construction         | City        | Q1 2028  | Q2 2028  |
|     Ch. 252; UG § 200.320)          | contract award       |             |          |          |
| 1.4 Construction                    | Substantial          | Contractor  | Q2 2028  | Q3 2028  |
|                                     | completion           |             |          |          |
| 1.5 Closeout and as-builts          | Final inspection     | City        | Q3 2028  | Q4 2028  |
| 1.6 Post-construction evaluation    | Travel survey        | CRISD/City  | Q4 2028  | Q3 2029  |

EVALUATION PLAN

Outcome measures and data sources:

- Mode-share change: Cedar Ridge ISD Travel Survey, baseline May 2025
  (22 percent walk/bike), follow-up surveys in spring of each post-
  construction year through 2031.
- Crash reduction: TxDOT CRIS pedestrian-involved crash data for the
  corridor, baseline 2020–2025, evaluated annually post-construction.
- Sidewalk condition: city Sidewalk Condition Assessment refresh in
  2030 will confirm the corridor moves from "deficient" to
  "compliant."

Reporting cadence: quarterly progress reports to TxDOT through
project closeout; annual post-construction outcome reports for three
years.

BUDGET NARRATIVE

| Line                                  | Federal     | Local      | Total       |
|---------------------------------------|-------------|------------|-------------|
| Final engineering (8.5%)              |  $72,000    |  $18,000   |   $90,250   |
| Construction — sidewalk (0.9 mi)      | $480,000    | $120,000   |  $600,000   |
| Construction — RRFBs (3 ea.)          | $128,000    |  $32,000   |  $160,000   |
| ADA curb ramps (2 ea.)                |  $48,000    |  $12,000   |   $60,000   |
| Construction inspection (6%)          |  $48,000    |  $12,000   |   $60,000   |
| Contingency (10%)                     |  $72,000    |  $18,250   |   $90,250   |
| Indirect (de minimis 10% on direct;   |  $2,000     |     $0     |    $2,000   |
|  limited to allowable lines)          |             |            |             |
| TOTAL                                 | $850,000    | $212,500   |$1,062,500   |

Federal share is 80 percent (TASA standard). Local match of $212,500
is cash from the Capital Projects Fund, council-approved March 24,
2026 (Resolution 2026-09). De minimis indirect cost rate is applied
per 2 C.F.R. § 200.414 against direct administrative cost only; the
city does not have a negotiated rate.

ORGANIZATIONAL CAPACITY

Cedar Ridge is a home-rule, council-manager city of 32,000 with a
GFOA Certificate of Achievement for Excellence in Financial Reporting
in each of the last seven years. The most recent independent audit
(FY2025) was unmodified with no material weaknesses. The city
performed a Single Audit in FY2024 with no compliance findings. The
city has successfully closed three TxDOT-administered grants in the
past decade (a 2019 SRTS infrastructure project, a 2017 HSIP
intersection project, and a 2014 STBG resurfacing project).

The project will be managed by the Public Works Engineering Division
under Director K. Patel, PE. Grant compliance will be administered by
Finance Director M. Rivera.

SUSTAINABILITY

Sidewalks, curb ramps, and signal infrastructure will be maintained
under the city's existing General Fund sidewalk and signal
maintenance program (FY2026 budget: $186,000 sidewalk maintenance;
$94,000 signal maintenance). RRFB replacement lamps and batteries are
within the existing operations and maintenance budget. The Caddo
County Public Health Coalition has committed to ongoing Safe Routes
education programming at Pecan Elementary (Letter of Support
attached, Attachment 7). Cedar Ridge ISD has committed to including
the corridor in its annual student travel survey through 2031
(Letter of Support, Attachment 8).
```

## Notes for the User

- Confirm the actual TASA NOFO point allocation and required sections
  before finalizing. TxDOT updates the TASA process periodically; the
  page limit and budget format here are illustrative.
- The CE-level environmental determination assumes the project stays
  within existing right-of-way; if any acquisition is needed, an EA
  is more likely.
- Attachments to the application typically include: TxDOT eligibility
  form, council resolution committing local match, letters of support,
  preliminary plans and corridor map, photos, audit reports.
- This draft was prepared with AI assistance and should be reviewed by
  the Public Works Director and Finance Director Rivera before
  submission. Some federal NOFOs require explicit AI use disclosure
  in the application; check the TASA NOFO for this requirement.
```
