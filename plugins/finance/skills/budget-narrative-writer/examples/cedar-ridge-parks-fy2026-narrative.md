# Example: FY2026 Parks and Recreation Budget Narrative — City of Cedar Ridge

This is a worked example for the budget-narrative-writer skill. It uses the fictional City of Cedar Ridge, TX, which also appears in council-ops, audit, and procurement examples across the library.

## Input

- **Entity:** City of Cedar Ridge, TX (home-rule, council-manager, population 32,000)
- **Department:** Parks and Recreation
- **Fiscal year:** FY2026 (Oct 1, 2025 – Sept 30, 2026)
- **Fund:** General Fund
- **FY2025 actuals:** $2,189,400
- **FY2026 adopted (current year):** $2,275,800
- **FY2026 YTD through Q2 (Mar 31):** $1,118,600 (49 percent of budget)
- **FY2027 proposed ask:** $2,418,200
- **FTEs:** 18.5 (no change from FY2026)
- **Three program areas:** Park Operations ($1,420,000), Recreation Programs ($612,000), Special Events ($386,200)
- **Capital request:** $190,000 for replacement of two aging tractor-mowers (above the city's $25,000 capitalization threshold)
- **Goals:** improve park maintenance cycle time, expand youth recreation participation, complete Veterans Park playground replacement (council priority)
- **KPIs:** mowing cycle days, registered program participants, customer satisfaction score, acres maintained per FTE

(The fiscal year named in the example output below is the FY+1 ask, FY2027. The skill convention treats "FY+1" as the year being budgeted.)

## Output

```
PARKS AND RECREATION DEPARTMENT
FY2027 Proposed Budget Narrative
Fund: General Fund
Total Proposed: $2,418,200   FTEs: 18.5

STRATEGIC CONTEXT

The Parks and Recreation Department maintains 312 acres of parkland
across 14 sites, operates the Veterans Park Recreation Center, and
delivers more than 60 recreation programs and four signature community
events annually. The department supports Council Strategic Priority #2
(Quality of Life and Community Identity) and Priority #4 (Healthy and
Active Cedar Ridge).

The FY2027 operating environment is shaped by three forces. Park usage
has grown 18 percent since FY2023 as Cedar Ridge's population has
crossed 32,000. Annual rainfall in the Caddo County area was 22 percent
above the 10-year average in calendar year 2025, lengthening the mowing
season. And the Veterans Park playground, installed in 2008, has been
flagged by the city's risk pool inspector for end-of-useful-life
replacement.

The FY2027 proposed budget holds FTEs flat, funds the playground
replacement through a transfer from the Capital Projects Fund (shown
separately in the capital budget), and reallocates internal resources
to address the mowing cycle backlog.

FY2026 ACCOMPLISHMENTS

- Completed the Live Oak Trail extension (0.7 miles), opening in
  September 2025 ahead of schedule and $14,200 under budget.
- Grew youth recreation program enrollment from 612 to 784 registered
  participants (28 percent increase) by adding evening soccer and a
  Saturday adaptive recreation block.
- Reduced mowing cycle from a 14-day average in FY2025 to a 12-day
  average in FY2026 through route optimization, despite a wetter season.
- Hosted four signature events (July 4 Concert, Founders Day, Movies in
  the Park, Holiday Light Trail) with an estimated combined attendance
  of 21,400.
- Closed two open audit findings from the FY2024 management letter
  related to fee collection reconciliation at the Recreation Center.

FY2027 PRIORITIES

1. Replace the Veterans Park playground. Activity: capital replacement
   of the 2008 playground structure flagged at end-of-useful-life.
   Resource: $325,000 in Capital Projects Fund (shown in the capital
   budget). Outcome: TPRS-compliant play structure with shaded play
   area, open by spring 2027.

2. Address the mowing cycle backlog. Activity: replace two aging tractor-
   mowers (2014 and 2016 units, 2,400 and 2,180 hours respectively) that
   account for the bulk of unplanned downtime. Resource: $190,000 in
   capital outlay in the proposed operating budget. Outcome: target
   mowing cycle of 10 days during the growing season, down from 12 in
   FY2026.

3. Expand youth recreation participation. Activity: add a third spring
   soccer division, a Tuesday/Thursday Pickleball Open Play block, and
   a partnership with Cedar Ridge ISD on after-school programming.
   Resource: $34,000 within existing program revenue. Outcome: registered
   participants of 900+ in FY2027.

4. Implement digital reservation and check-in at the Recreation Center.
   Activity: deploy the IT-procured CivicRec platform module. Resource:
   funded through IT's enterprise software budget; Parks staff time only.
   Outcome: reduce check-in wait time and capture cleaner participation
   data for performance reporting.

PERFORMANCE MEASURES

| Measure                                | FY2025 Actual | FY2026 Projected | FY2027 Target |
|----------------------------------------|---------------|------------------|---------------|
| Mowing cycle (days, growing season)    | 14            | 12               | 10            |
| Registered program participants        | 612           | 784              | 900           |
| Acres maintained per FTE (parks ops)   | 28.1          | 28.4             | 29.2          |
| Customer satisfaction (1–5 scale)      | 4.2           | 4.3              | 4.4           |
| Volunteer hours contributed            | 3,840         | 4,100            | 4,400         |

SIGNIFICANT CHANGES FROM FY2026

- Capital outlay of $190,000 for two replacement tractor-mowers, above
  the city's $25,000 capitalization threshold. Replaces 2014 and 2016
  units that are projected to require $42,000 in repairs in FY2027 if
  retained.
- Personal services increase of $54,800 reflects the council-approved
  3.0 percent compensation adjustment and step movement; no FTE changes.
- Contractual services increase of $18,200 reflects the renegotiated
  port-a-let contract ($4,800), increased water and electric utility
  estimates ($9,400), and a $4,000 increase in tree care services tied
  to oak wilt monitoring.
- Recreation program revenue is budgeted at $312,000, an increase of
  $28,000 over FY2026 driven by program expansion. This is recorded as
  general fund revenue and offsets the general fund subsidy.

OUTYEAR CONSIDERATIONS (FY2028 AND FY2029)

The Veterans Park Recreation Center HVAC system reaches end of useful
life in FY2028 with an estimated replacement cost of $185,000; staff
recommends placing it in the next capital improvement plan update.
Equipment replacement for two pickup trucks (2018 and 2019 model years)
is scheduled in FY2029 at approximately $96,000. Outyear personnel
costs will reflect the city's compensation study refresh scheduled for
FY2028; preliminary indications suggest a market adjustment for the
recreation specialist class.

The department does not currently rely on grant funding for operations,
which limits outyear revenue risk.
```

## Notes for the User

- The capital request for tractor-mowers is presented in the operating
  budget because the city's capital improvement plan threshold is
  $50,000. Items between $25,000 (capitalization) and $50,000 (CIP
  threshold) appear in operating capital outlay.
- The Veterans Park playground replacement is in the Capital Projects
  Fund and would be carried in the capital budget narrative, not here.
- Performance measure baselines tie to the city's performance reporting
  dashboard. Confirm FY2025 actuals with Finance before submission.
- This draft was prepared with AI assistance and should be reviewed by
  the Parks Director and Finance Director Rivera before transmittal to
  the City Manager's proposed budget binder.
