# Example: FY2025 Transparency Summary — City of Cedar Ridge

This is a worked example for the transparency-report-generator skill. It uses the fictional City of Cedar Ridge, TX. The same city is referenced across the council-ops, finance, audit, and procurement examples in this library. FY2025 ended September 30, 2025.

## Input

- **Entity:** City of Cedar Ridge, TX (home-rule, council-manager, population 32,000)
- **FY:** FY2025 (Oct 1, 2024 – Sept 30, 2025)
- **General Fund revenue:** $23,810,000
- **General Fund expenditure:** $23,418,000
- **Water and Sewer Fund revenue:** $11,640,000
- **Water and Sewer Fund expenditure:** $10,985,000
- **Debt outstanding at 9/30/2025:** $34,200,000 (GO bonds $18,500,000; CO $9,700,000; revenue debt $5,200,000; capital leases $800,000)
- **TMRS funded ratio:** 89.4 percent (most recent valuation December 31, 2024)
- **Adopted tax rate FY2026:** $0.5732 per $100 (M&O $0.4198; I&S $0.1534)
- **Top contracts list available; check register posted monthly**
- **Two Ch. 380 agreements in effect:** retail-anchor development at 4500 Live Oak Drive; manufacturing investment incentive at 700 Industrial Parkway
- **GASB 77 disclosure:** total tax abatement value $84,000 (FY2025)
- **Bond ratings:** Moody's Aa3 (GO); S&P AA- (GO)

## Output

```
CITY OF CEDAR RIDGE, TEXAS
FY2025 FINANCIAL TRANSPARENCY
Last updated: March 12, 2026
Source documents: FY2025 Annual Comprehensive Financial Report
(unmodified opinion, dated February 28, 2026); FY2026 Adopted Budget;
FY2025 Q4 Investment Report (Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 2256); annual debt
obligation report posted under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 140.0045.

OVERVIEW

Cedar Ridge publishes this page to give residents a clear picture of
how the City raises and spends money. It mirrors the categories used
by the Texas Comptroller's Transparency Stars program: traditional
finances, contracts and procurement, economic development, public
pensions, and debt obligations. Every figure on this page ties to a
source document linked below.

1. TRADITIONAL FINANCES

In Fiscal Year 2025 (October 1, 2024 through September 30, 2025) the
General Fund — which pays for police, fire, parks, streets, and
general administration — received $23,810,000 in revenue and spent
$23,418,000 on operations and small capital, ending the year with a
$392,000 operating surplus. Revenue grew 5.3 percent over FY2024,
primarily from sales tax and property tax growth.

The Water and Sewer Fund (an enterprise fund) generated $11,640,000
in revenue and spent $10,985,000 on operations, debt service, and
capital investments, ending the year with a $655,000 operating
result. Water and sewer rates were last adjusted on October 1, 2024.

The General Fund's unassigned fund balance at year-end was equal to
90 days of operating expenditure, above the City Council's 75-day
policy floor.

Sources:
- FY2025 Annual Comprehensive Financial Report (full PDF):
  www.cedarridgetx.gov/finance/acfr-fy2025
- FY2026 Adopted Budget Book:
  www.cedarridgetx.gov/finance/budget-fy2026
- Monthly financial reports:
  www.cedarridgetx.gov/finance/monthly-reports

2. CONTRACTS AND PROCUREMENT

Cedar Ridge follows Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 252 for competitive
procurement and Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 2269 for alternative project
delivery on public works. The City posts active solicitations on its
Bonfire portal and posts the monthly check register through the
Finance Department page.

In FY2025 the City processed approximately 4,300 vendor payments
totaling $34.9 million. The 25 largest contracts active during
FY2025, with vendor, scope summary, and contract value, are listed
in the linked summary.

Vendors with a business relationship covered by Tex. Loc. Gov't Code
Ch. 176 file Form CIQ with the City Secretary. City officers file
the companion Form CIS. Both rosters are available on the City
Secretary page.

Links:
- Active solicitations: cedarridge.bonfirehub.com
- Monthly check register:
  www.cedarridgetx.gov/finance/check-register
- Top 25 contracts (FY2025):
  www.cedarridgetx.gov/finance/top-contracts-fy2025
- Form CIQ filings:
  www.cedarridgetx.gov/clerk/ciq

3. ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT

Two performance-based economic development agreements were active in
FY2025:

(a) Live Oak Center Retail Anchor (4500 Live Oak Drive). Tex. Loc.
Gov't Code Ch. 380 agreement adopted by City Council Resolution
2023-08. Performance-based sales tax rebate, maximum aggregate
$1,200,000 over 10 years, contingent on a $14 million capital
investment and an annual sales tax generation threshold. FY2025
rebate: $146,200.

(b) Cedar Ridge Manufacturing Investment (700 Industrial Parkway).
Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 380 agreement adopted by City Council
Resolution 2022-22. Performance-based property tax-equivalent
incentive, maximum aggregate $640,000 over 7 years, contingent on a
$9 million capital investment and a 45-FTE job retention threshold.
FY2025 incentive paid: $84,000.

In addition, the City participates in one tax abatement administered
by Caddo County (Cedar Ridge as an overlapping taxing unit) for a
distribution center expansion. Under GASB 77, the City discloses
$84,000 in reduced property tax revenue attributable to this
abatement in FY2025.

Links:
- Ch. 380 agreements (PDFs):
  www.cedarridgetx.gov/economic-development/agreements
- GASB 77 disclosure: ACFR Note 17, pp. 96–98.

4. PUBLIC PENSIONS

Cedar Ridge participates in the Texas Municipal Retirement System
(TMRS), a statewide multiple-employer defined benefit plan
established under Tex. Gov't Code Title 8, Subtitle G. TMRS is the
sole pension plan for general City employees. Sworn police personnel
also participate in TMRS; Cedar Ridge does not maintain a separate
local pension plan.

- Plan: Texas Municipal Retirement System
- Most recent actuarial valuation: December 31, 2024
- Funded ratio: 89.4 percent
- City contribution rate FY2025: 13.42 percent of payroll
- Member contribution rate: 7 percent
- Vesting: 5 years
- Retirement eligibility: age 60 with 5 years service, or any age
  with 20 years service

The annual TMRS report, the City's pension footnote (ACFR Note 11),
and the Texas Pension Review Board filings are linked below.

Links:
- TMRS annual report: www.tmrs.com
- Pension Review Board filings: www.prb.texas.gov
- ACFR Note 11: see Section 1 above.

5. DEBT OBLIGATIONS

At September 30, 2025 the City had $34,200,000 in outstanding debt
principal:

| Type                             | Outstanding (9/30/25) |
|----------------------------------|-----------------------|
| General Obligation Bonds         | $18,500,000           |
| Certificates of Obligation       |  $9,700,000           |
| Water and Sewer Revenue Bonds    |  $5,200,000           |
| Capital Leases (GASB 87)         |    $800,000           |
| TOTAL                            | $34,200,000           |

Per capita debt at FY2025 year-end is approximately $1,069. The
General Obligation debt service requirement is funded through the
Interest and Sinking (I&S) portion of the property tax rate, which
for FY2026 is set at $0.1534 per $100 of assessed valuation. Total
adopted FY2026 tax rate is $0.5732 per $100 (M&O $0.4198, I&S
$0.1534).

Current ratings on the City's General Obligation debt:

- Moody's Investors Service: Aa3 (stable outlook)
- S&P Global Ratings: AA- (stable outlook)

Water and Sewer revenue bond debt service is covered at 2.41× by net
revenue in FY2025, above the 1.25× rate covenant required by the
bond ordinances.

The City files continuing disclosure annually on the MSRB's EMMA
system as required by SEC Rule 15c2-12.

## Notes for the User

- The page should be re-published when the audit is finalized,
  when a new debt issue closes, or when a new Ch. 380/381 agreement
  is adopted.
- Confirm bond ratings against the most recent rating letters before
  publication.
- The GASB 77 disclosure number ties to ACFR Note 17 — confirm the
  page reference at publication.
- The TMRS funded ratio refreshes annually with the December 31
  valuation. The next refresh will be available in summer 2026.
- The Texas Comptroller Transparency Stars program criteria are
  refreshed periodically; verify against the current checklist.
```
