# Site Selection RFI Response — Section Checklist

A working checklist of what experienced site selectors look for in each section of an RFI response and the common failure modes that move a community to the "no" pile in the first reading.

## Cover Letter

**Include:** Project code name acknowledgment, point of contact and direct number, NDA acknowledgment, response date.

**Avoid:** Marketing prose, mayor's photo, glossy graphics that increase file size without adding information.

## Community Profile

**Include:**

- Population and 10-year growth trend
- Metropolitan statistical area context
- Distance to nearest port (Houston, Beaumont, Corpus, Brownsville)
- Distance to DFW, IAH, AUS, SAT international airports
- Major employer mix by sector
- Median age and labor force participation rate

**Common failure:** Burying the population number on page 3. Site selectors filter on population in the first 30 seconds.

## Workforce

**Include:**

- Total civilian labor force within 30-, 45-, and 60-minute drive isochrones
- Wage levels by occupation relevant to the project sector (BLS OES data is acceptable)
- Unemployment rate, 24-month trend
- Sector concentration ratios for the target sector
- Community college and university programs producing relevant talent
- Workforce board contact and program inventory (TWC Skills Development Fund eligibility)
- Recent comparable hires (anonymized) at existing employers

**Common failure:** Overstating wage competitiveness. Site selectors cross-check with BLS data within minutes.

## Real Estate

**Per site, include:**

- Parcel ID and ownership
- Acreage (gross and developable)
- Current zoning and any required rezoning
- Asking price or terms
- Frontage and access points
- Topography summary
- Environmental status (Phase I report status, known issues)
- Utility distance to mainlines
- Rail or interstate access

**Common failure:** Presenting marginal sites alongside the best site without filtering. Three excellent options beat seven mediocre options.

## Infrastructure

**Water:** Available pressure at the site, available capacity in gallons per day, source (surface, ground, purchased), treatment plant headroom, distance to fire hydrants.

**Wastewater:** Capacity in gallons per day at the receiving lift station and treatment plant, current loading, industrial pretreatment requirements.

**Electric:** Substation distance, available voltage, dual feed availability, recent capacity additions or constraints, provider (municipally owned, cooperative, IOU).

**Natural gas:** Distance to main, available capacity, provider.

**Telecommunications:** Fiber providers serving the site, dual-route availability, latency to major data hubs.

**Transportation:** Distance to interstate, weight-restricted bridges between site and interstate, rail spur availability, last-mile delivery patterns.

**Common failure:** Confusing maximum capacity with available capacity. Site selectors care about what is currently available, not theoretical buildout.

## Regulatory Environment

**Include:**

- Zoning compatibility for the proposed use
- Platting requirements and typical timeline
- Building permit timeline (from complete application to issuance)
- Environmental review obligations (TCEQ permits, USACE Section 404 if wetlands, endangered species considerations)
- Recent comparable permits issued (anonymized) with actual elapsed time
- Designated project manager or permit concierge

**Common failure:** Quoting the statutory minimum permitting timeline rather than the realistic timeline. Site selectors trust communities that disclose realistic timelines.

## Incentives Package

**Include:**

- Statutory authority (Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 380, Ch. 381, Ch. 504, Ch. 505, Tex. Tax Code Ch. 311, Ch. 312)
- Authorized framework or guidelines adopted by the governing body
- Specific components offered: property tax rebate, sales tax rebate, cash grant, fee waiver, infrastructure cost-share
- County participation if any
- Type A or Type B EDC participation if any
- School district position (informational; school district incentive programs are limited)
- State-level support (TEF, Skills Development Fund)
- Process and timeline for governing-body action

**Common failure:** Stating dollar amounts that have not been authorized. Use ranges and commit to a process where authorization is incomplete.

## Quality of Life

**Include:** Schools (district rating, recent bond program), parks and trails inventory, housing inventory by price band, healthcare access, cost of living, commute patterns, recent municipal investments.

**Common failure:** Listing every park and trail in the city. Two strong examples beat a comprehensive list.

## Implementation Team

**Include:** Named city manager, ED director, utility lead, planning director, workforce board contact, chamber lead. Direct phone numbers. Single point of contact identified.

**Common failure:** Naming a generic "economic development department." Site selectors want a name and a phone number.

## Confidentiality and Non-Binding Statement

Standard language: "This response is non-binding. Final incentive commitments require approval by the City Council in open meeting under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 380. Materials marked confidential by the requesting party are withheld under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.131 to the extent disclosure would harm the City's competitive position. Following project announcement or decline, most materials become public."
