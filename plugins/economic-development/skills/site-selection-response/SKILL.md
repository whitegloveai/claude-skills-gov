# Site Selection Response

> Drafts a coordinated RFI response that reads like the work of a serious community competing for a real project. Built for ED directors who get a 14-day deadline, a project code name, and a five-tab spreadsheet of questions.

## When to Use

- "Build a response to the Project Falcon distribution center RFI."
- "We have 14 days to respond to a confidential site selector RFI. Draft it."
- "Format the workforce, sites, and utilities sections for a 480,000-sf project."
- "Draft the incentives section using our adopted Ch. 380 framework."
- "Pull together the regulatory and permitting timeline section."

## Inputs Needed

Required:

- RFI questions, format requirements, and submission deadline
- Project parameters: sector, capital investment, job creation, square footage, utility loads, schedule, confidentiality posture
- Available sites with parcel size, ownership, zoning, frontage, utility availability
- Workforce data: regional labor draw, wage levels, training pipeline, unemployment, sector concentration
- Utility capacity: water, sewer, electric, gas, fiber, transportation access
- Regulatory environment: zoning entitlements, platting requirements, permitting timeline, environmental constraints
- Incentives package authorized: Ch. 380 framework, EDC participation, county participation, school district stance

Optional but recommended:

- Letters of support from utility providers, school district, workforce board, and regional partners
- Recent comparable projects sited locally
- Quality-of-life materials (parks, schools, housing inventory)
- Available state-level support: TEF, Skills Development Fund, Texas Workforce Commission

If the user has not provided the RFI questions or the confidentiality posture, ask before drafting. Confidentiality posture drives both the content and the public-records handling.

## Process

1. Confirm the confidentiality posture. Note whether the city has signed an NDA, whether a project code name has been assigned, and whether materials shared by the site selector are confidential under Tex. Gov't Code § 552.131. Plan the response with the assumption that sensitive content may be released after the project is announced or declined.
2. Mirror the RFI structure exactly. Site selectors compare responses tab-for-tab. Do not reorder the questions, even if the local logic would suggest otherwise.
3. Draft the Community Profile: population, demographics, growth trends, metropolitan context, distance to ports and major markets.
4. Draft Workforce: total labor draw within 30, 45, and 60 minutes; wage levels by occupation relevant to the project sector; training pipeline (community college, ISD CTE, workforce board programs); recent comparable hires by other employers.
5. Draft Real Estate: each candidate site with parcel ID, acreage, ownership, zoning, asking price or terms, frontage, topography, environmental status, and rail or interstate access. Include a parcel-level map reference.
6. Draft Infrastructure: water capacity and pressure at the site, wastewater capacity, electric service voltage and substation distance, natural gas availability, fiber providers and routes, road access, weight-restricted bridges, rail spurs.
7. Draft Regulatory Environment: zoning compatibility, platting requirements and timeline, building permit timeline, environmental review obligations (TCEQ, USACE if wetlands), endangered species considerations.
8. Draft Incentives Package: state the proposed Ch. 380/381 structure, EDC participation if any, county participation, school district position. Be specific about dollar values where authorized; otherwise use ranges.
9. Draft Quality of Life: schools, parks, housing inventory, healthcare, cost of living, commute patterns, recent municipal investments.
10. Draft Implementation Team: named city, EDC, utility, workforce, and chamber contacts who will work the project through site selection.
11. Close with a statement that the response is non-binding until a definitive agreement is approved by the governing body in open meeting.

## Output Format

Mirrors the RFI structure. When the RFI does not prescribe a format, use these sections:

1. Cover Letter
2. Community Profile
3. Workforce
4. Real Estate (with site profiles)
5. Infrastructure
6. Regulatory Environment
7. Incentives Package
8. Quality of Life
9. Implementation Team
10. Confidentiality and Non-Binding Statement

Length: typically 25 to 60 pages depending on RFI scope. Use tables for quantitative content. Avoid marketing prose; site selectors discount communities that lead with adjectives.

Tone: factual, candid about constraints and trade-offs. Site selectors trust communities that disclose limitations early. Do not overstate utility capacity or permitting timelines. Overstatement is the single most common cause of late-stage project failure.

## TRAIGA & Compliance Notes

**AI use disclosure — transparency best practice (not a § 552.051 trigger):** Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.051(b)–(d) requires disclosure when a citizen *interacts directly with an AI system* (e.g., a chatbot, automated voice assistant, or AI-driven service interface). The disclosure trigger is *interaction*, not *output*. This skill produces a static document drafted with AI assistance; it does not put a citizen in direct interaction with AI, so § 552.051 is not engaged.

However, transparency about AI assistance in document drafting is sound practice and should align with the city's adopted AI use policy and the GovAI Coalition's guidance on documenting AI tool use. Consider a brief notation on documents distributed publicly or to the governing body: *"AI-assisted draft, reviewed by [staff title and name]."* This is a policy choice, not a statutory mandate.

**Confidentiality:** Tex. Gov't Code § 552.131 exempts certain economic development information from public disclosure when release would harm the city's competitive position. This exception is narrow, time-limited, and project-specific. Materials marked confidential by a site selector remain subject to the Public Information Act; the city may withhold under § 552.131 only while the deal is active and only to the extent disclosure would harm the city's negotiating position. After the project is announced or declined, most materials become public.

**Binding vs. non-binding:** RFI responses are commonly framed as "expressions of interest." Authorize incentives only as the governing body or EDC board has formally authorized. A letter that states specific dollar grants without governing-body authorization may create reliance and litigation risk. Use ranges when authorization has not been completed; commit only to a process and timeline for board action.

**Authorization pathway:** Cities act through the council under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 380; counties through commissioners court under Ch. 381; Type A through Ch. 504; Type B through Ch. 505 (with § 505.158 hearing where required). RFI responses should be reviewed by the city attorney before submission.

**Retention:** RFI responses and supporting analysis retain consistent with the local government's adopted retention schedule, typically aligned to the Texas State Library Local Government Retention Schedule.
