## 1. Letter from the Executive Director

To the Cedar Ridge community, Mayor Castillo, the City Council, and the EDC Board:

FY2025 was a year of measured progress. The corporation closed four projects that together placed $52 million in capital investment into service and created 380 full-time jobs at an average wage of $23.40 per hour. Six active projects carried into FY2026, including one that triggered a § 505.158 hearing for an affordable workforce housing component.

The work was not without setbacks. The Riverbend Logistics project did not achieve its Year 1 jobs milestone and entered a 12-month cure period under our standard clawback. The board has received quarterly updates and will consider the Year 2 performance review in January.

I want to acknowledge the staff and partners who made the year work: City Manager Daniel Ahn, the Cedar Ridge EDC Board led by [Chair Name], Caddo Workforce Solutions, Caddo College, and the Cedar Ridge Chamber. The most consequential outcome of the year was not a single project; it was the deepening of the regional training partnership with Caddo College that produced 28 welders, 18 machinists, and 35 CDL drivers placed with local employers.

Looking ahead, the board has adopted three priorities for FY2026: complete the Cedar Ridge Business Park Phase 2 infrastructure, structure a workforce housing pilot under § 505.151(11), and finalize a county-city joint incentive framework with Caddo County under Ch. 380/381.

— Sofia Ramirez, ED Director

## 5. Workforce and Partnerships

The corporation invested $185,000 in partnership programs during FY2025:

- **Caddo College welding cohort:** 28 completers, 26 placed locally, average starting wage $22.10/hour.
- **CDL training partnership:** 35 completers, 30 placed locally.
- **First-line supervisor pilot with Texas A&M Engineering Extension Service:** 18 completers from 6 employers.
- **Cedar Ridge ISD CTE engagement:** EDC funded equipment for the high school machining program; 22 students earned NIMS credentials.

## 6. Community Impact

The 380 jobs created in FY2025 represent annualized payroll of approximately $18.5 million. Based on the BR&E spending-pattern survey, approximately 60% of that payroll is spent within Caddo County. The four completed projects added approximately $51 million in taxable value to the Cedar Ridge tax roll, increasing the city's tax base by approximately 1.8%.

## 8. Looking Ahead

The board has adopted three priorities for FY2026:

1. **Complete the Cedar Ridge Business Park Phase 2 infrastructure** to make three additional parcels site-ready by Q4 2026.
2. **Structure a workforce housing pilot under § 505.151(11)** to address the housing-supply constraint identified in the FY2025 BR&E cycle.
3. **Finalize a county-city joint incentive framework with Caddo County** under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 380 and Ch. 381 to streamline incentive coordination on regional projects.
