# EDC Annual Report — Required and Recommended Elements

A working checklist for Type A (Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 504) and Type B (Ch. 505) Texas economic development corporation annual reports, plus city EDC progress reports operated as a department.

## Statutory Anchors

| Citation | What it requires |
|---|---|
| Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 504.351 | Type A corporations file an annual report with the city. |
| Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 505.404 | Type B corporations file an annual report with the city. |
| Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 505.158 | Hearing requirement for certain Type B projects; summarize in annual report. |
| Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 505.159 | Council approval required for certain Type B actions; reference approvals obtained. |
| Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 551 | Adoption in open meeting. |
| Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552 | Annual report is a public record. |

## Required Elements (per most city-adopted reporting templates)

1. **Identification of the corporation:** Legal name, type (A or B), creating city, date of creation, sales tax rate authorized.
2. **Board of directors:** Names, terms, officers, meeting attendance summary.
3. **Financial position:**
   - Sales tax revenue actual vs. budget
   - Other revenue sources (interest, lease, grant)
   - Total expenditures by category (operations, project incentives, capital, debt service)
   - Beginning and ending fund balance
   - Any restricted reserves and the purpose of the restriction
4. **Project activity:**
   - Projects authorized during the year, with statutory category for Type B projects
   - Projects completed during the year
   - Projects discontinued or where performance milestones were not met
   - Status of agreements with active clawback exposure
5. **Compliance disclosures:**
   - § 505.158 hearings held and outcomes
   - § 505.159 council approvals obtained
   - Conflict of interest disclosures by board members
   - Required ethics filings status
6. **Outstanding obligations:** Multi-year incentive commitments and their future-year cash flow impact.

## Recommended Elements

1. **Letter from the executive director or board chair.** A one- to one-and-a-half-page voice piece, with named individuals and a candid acknowledgment of a challenge or unfinished priority.
2. **Year-in-review KPIs:**
   - Jobs created (new)
   - Jobs retained (BR&E)
   - Capital investment in completed projects
   - Capital investment in agreements executed
   - Workforce training cohorts supported
3. **Project portfolio narrative.** Beyond the statutory list, a short narrative for each material project: applicant, sector, capital, jobs, status.
4. **Workforce and partnership outcomes.** Specific outcomes from partnerships with the workforce board, community college, school district CTE, chamber, and regional EDO.
5. **Community impact.** Concrete examples of household-level or neighborhood-level impact: jobs at livable wages, infrastructure that opened sites, tax base expansion.
6. **Sales tax revenue trend.** Three- to five-year history with discussion of drivers.
7. **Strategic plan alignment.** Where the year's activity advanced the board-adopted strategic plan and where it did not.
8. **Looking ahead.** Three to five priorities adopted by the board for the year ahead, with the rationale and the resources allocated to each.

## Common Audit and Council Comments

- Failing to reconcile sales tax revenue reported in the annual report to the Comptroller's allocation reports.
- Listing completed projects without disclosing clawback status or performance variance.
- Omitting § 505.158 hearings from the compliance section.
- Reporting "jobs created" without distinguishing new from retained or full-time from part-time.
- Reporting capital investment commitments rather than capital investment actually placed in service during the year.
- Failing to disclose multi-year incentive commitments and their future-year cash flow impact.

## Distribution and Posting

- File with the city by the date and in the form prescribed.
- Present to the EDC board in open meeting for acknowledgment or adoption.
- Post on the city or EDC website.
- Provide a print version for council distribution if requested.
- Make available on request under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552.

## Format Recommendations

- Lead with the letter and KPIs. Save the financial detail for the middle.
- Use tables for quantitative content. Use plain prose for narrative.
- Anonymize or aggregate any BR&E findings that name specific at-risk businesses, unless the business has consented.
- Avoid superlatives. The reader is a council member, a journalist, or a resident — credibility matters more than enthusiasm.
- Keep total length manageable. A focused 15-page report reads better than a comprehensive 40-page report.
