# Annual Report Narrative

> Drafts the narrative sections of an EDC annual report or city ED progress report. Built for the executive director who needs the prose to drop into a designed report or be posted as a standalone document.

## When to Use

- "Draft the FY2025 annual report narrative for the EDC."
- "Build the year-in-review and project portfolio sections for the board chair."
- "Write the executive director's letter for this year's annual report."
- "Create the looking-ahead section tied to the FY2026 strategic plan."
- "Convert the project tracker into the project portfolio narrative."

## Inputs Needed

Required:

- Fiscal year covered (or calendar year, depending on adopted reporting cycle)
- KPIs for the year: jobs created, capital investment, retention success rate, projects assisted
- Project portfolio: completed projects (capex, jobs, status), active projects (stage, sector)
- Financial summary: revenues (sales tax allocation, grant revenue, interest), expenditures (operations, project incentives, capital), fund balance, beginning and ending
- Board composition: members, terms, officers
- Partnerships: workforce board, community college, chamber, regional EDO, county

Optional but recommended:

- Strategic plan or work plan in effect during the period
- Comparable prior-year KPIs for trend lines
- Sales tax revenue trend (3 to 5 years)
- Community impact stories or testimonials
- Forward-look priorities adopted by the board

If the user has not provided the financial summary or the project portfolio, ask before drafting. A narrative without numbers reads as marketing.

## Process

1. Confirm the entity type. Type A corporations operate under Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 504 and report under § 504.351. Type B corporations operate under Ch. 505 and report under § 505.404. Some cities operate the ED function as a city department rather than a corporation; the framework is similar but the statutory citations differ.
2. Draft the Letter from the Executive Director. One to one-and-a-half pages. Lead with the year's most consequential outcomes, acknowledge a challenge candidly, name the staff, board, and partners by role, and close with the year ahead.
3. Draft the Year in Review. State the headline KPIs as a table. Discuss what drove each KPI. Avoid the temptation to characterize every quarter as record-breaking.
4. Draft the Project Portfolio section. Group projects into Completed, Active, and Pipeline. For each completed project, state the applicant, sector, capital investment, jobs, status of agreement performance, and any clawback action.
5. Draft the Financial Summary. Present revenues, expenditures, and fund balance in a table. Discuss any material variance from the adopted budget. Disclose sales tax revenue trend over 3 to 5 years.
6. Draft the Workforce and Partnerships section. State the partnerships, their purpose, and what each produced in the reporting period.
7. Draft the Community Impact section. Frame impact at the household and neighborhood level when possible: jobs at livable wages, capital investment that expanded tax base, infrastructure that opened new sites.
8. Draft the Board and Leadership section. List board members and terms. Acknowledge outgoing members.
9. Draft the Looking Ahead section. State the board-adopted priorities for the year ahead, not a wish list.
10. Format for either a designed-report drop-in or a standalone web document. Use plain markdown headings that translate cleanly to either path.

## Output Format

Plain markdown annual report narrative. Sections in this order:

1. Letter from the Executive Director
2. Year in Review (with KPI table)
3. Project Portfolio (with subsections: Completed, Active, Pipeline)
4. Financial Summary (with revenue/expense table)
5. Workforce and Partnerships
6. Community Impact
7. Board and Leadership
8. Looking Ahead

Length: 12 to 25 pages depending on portfolio size.

Tone: factual, direct, and confident without superlatives. The reader is a council member or resident who wants to understand what the EDC did with public money. Avoid promotional language. State both achievements and unfinished business.

## TRAIGA & Compliance Notes

**AI use disclosure — transparency best practice (not a § 552.051 trigger):** Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.051(b)–(d) requires disclosure when a citizen *interacts directly with an AI system* (e.g., a chatbot, automated voice assistant, or AI-driven service interface). The disclosure trigger is *interaction*, not *output*. This skill produces a static document drafted with AI assistance; it does not put a citizen in direct interaction with AI, so § 552.051 is not engaged.

However, transparency about AI assistance in document drafting is sound practice and should align with the city's adopted AI use policy and the GovAI Coalition's guidance on documenting AI tool use. Consider a brief notation on documents distributed publicly or to the governing body: *"AI-assisted draft, reviewed by [staff title and name]."* This is a policy choice, not a statutory mandate.

**Statutory reporting obligations:**

- Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 505.404 — Type B EDCs file an annual report with the city in the form prescribed by the city. Typically covers project activity, financial position, and governance compliance.
- Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 504.351 — Type A EDCs have a parallel annual report obligation.
- Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 505.158 — certain Type B projects (outside the categories listed in § 505.151) require a separately noticed public hearing. The annual report is a logical place to summarize the prior year's § 505.158 hearings.
- Tex. Loc. Gov't Code § 505.159 — many Type B board actions require council approval. Reference council approvals obtained during the year.
- Many corporations post their annual report under Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 552 transparency expectations.

**Open meetings and open records:** EDC boards are governmental bodies under the Texas Open Meetings Act (Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 551) and subject to the Public Information Act (Ch. 552). The annual report is a public record. Drafts circulated internally before adoption may be eligible for the § 552.111 (agency memoranda) exception until finalized.

**Sales tax allocations:** Type A and Type B sales tax revenues are reported by the Comptroller. State the actual collections, not estimates, when finalizing the report.

**Retention:** Adopted annual reports retain permanently. Drafts retain consistent with the local government's adopted retention schedule, typically aligned to the Texas State Library Local Government Retention Schedule.
