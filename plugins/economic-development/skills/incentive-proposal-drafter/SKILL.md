# Incentive Proposal Drafter

> Drafts a Chapter 380/381 incentive proposal that a council or commissioners court can read, debate, and act on. Built for ED directors and EDC executives who need to convert a deal sheet into a defensible public document.

## When to Use

- "Draft a Chapter 380 proposal for the Cedar Ridge Advanced Manufacturing project."
- "Turn this deal sheet into something council can read on Tuesday."
- "Build the performance metrics and clawback section for a $28M capex deal."
- "The county judge wants a 381 narrative for the data center incentive."
- "Convert this EDC term sheet into a board-ready proposal."

## Inputs Needed

Required:

- Project name and applicant entity (legal name and parent if any)
- Capital investment amount and schedule
- Job creation: count, average wage level, benefits, full-time vs. part-time
- Project timeline: site control, construction start, certificate of occupancy, first hire, full ramp
- Requested incentive structure: cash grants (annual or lump), property tax rebates, sales tax rebates, fee waivers, infrastructure cost-share
- Public benefit narrative: workforce profile, tax base impact, supply chain, community fit

Optional but recommended:

- Retention target if part of an expansion or retention deal
- Wage floor and any benefits requirement
- Third-party economic impact analysis (if available)
- Identified clawback triggers and recapture method
- Confidentiality posture during pre-decision phase

If the user has not provided capital investment, job creation, or the requested incentive structure, ask before drafting. A proposal without numbers is not actionable.

## Process

1. Confirm legal authority. Cities use Ch. 380; counties use Ch. 381. Sales-tax-funded incentives flow through a Type A (Ch. 504) or Type B (Ch. 505) corporation. Property tax abatement is a separate path under Tex. Tax Code Ch. 312 and requires a reinvestment zone. Note whether more than one authority is layered.
2. Draft the Executive Summary in one page or less. State the applicant, capital investment, job count, average wage, requested incentive total, and policy rationale.
3. Write the Project Description: facility type, square footage, equipment investment, operations, hours, customer base. Council members test for specificity.
4. Draft Applicant Background: corporate history, ownership, locations, references, prior incentive performance. If the applicant remains confidential, use a project code name.
5. Build Capital Investment and Jobs in a table. Break out real property, business personal property, inventory. Break out jobs by year, average wage, benefits.
6. Write Public Benefit Analysis. Quantify: estimated incremental property tax, sales tax on construction and ongoing operations, induced workforce spending, supply chain spend with local vendors.
7. Draft Requested Incentives Structure. State each component with a dollar amount and schedule (property tax rebate, sales tax rebate, cash grant, fee waivers, infrastructure cost-share, or layered).
8. Draft Performance Metrics and Milestones. Tie each disbursement to a measurable milestone: capex by paid invoices, jobs by quarterly TWC filings, wage floor by payroll certification.
9. Draft Clawback Provisions. Specify recapture method (pro-rata, straight, or accelerated), cure periods, and trigger events (sale or relocation, jobs shortfall, wage floor breach, dissolution).
10. Draft Term and Reporting: agreement term, annual compliance report due date, audit rights, notice provisions.
11. Close with Recommended Council Action: the motion to approve in principle and direct staff to negotiate final terms.

## Output Format

Plain markdown proposal. Length: 6 to 12 pages depending on deal complexity. Sections in this order:

1. Executive Summary
2. Project Description
3. Applicant Background
4. Capital Investment and Jobs (with table)
5. Public Benefit Analysis
6. Requested Incentives Structure (with disbursement schedule table)
7. Performance Metrics and Milestones
8. Clawback Provisions
9. Term and Reporting
10. Recommended Council Action

Tone: factual, candid, public-records-cognizant. State both benefits and risks. Do not editorialize about the applicant. Avoid superlatives.

## TRAIGA & Compliance Notes

**AI use disclosure — transparency best practice (not a § 552.051 trigger):** Tex. Bus. & Comm. Code § 552.051(b)–(d) requires disclosure when a citizen *interacts directly with an AI system* (e.g., a chatbot, automated voice assistant, or AI-driven service interface). The disclosure trigger is *interaction*, not *output*. This skill produces a static document drafted with AI assistance; it does not put a citizen in direct interaction with AI, so § 552.051 is not engaged.

However, transparency about AI assistance in document drafting is sound practice and should align with the city's adopted AI use policy and the GovAI Coalition's guidance on documenting AI tool use. Consider a brief notation on documents distributed publicly or to the governing body: *"AI-assisted draft, reviewed by [staff title and name]."* This is a policy choice, not a statutory mandate.

**Incentive authority — Texas:**

- Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 380 — municipal authority to make loans or grants of public money to promote economic development.
- Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 381 — county authority paralleling Ch. 380.
- Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 504 — Type A economic development corporations funded by dedicated sales tax. Industrial focus, narrower eligible uses.
- Tex. Loc. Gov't Code Ch. 505 — Type B corporations. Broader eligible uses including quality of life. § 505.158 requires a public hearing for certain projects.
- Tex. Tax Code Ch. 311 — Tax Increment Reinvestment Zones (TIRZ). Captures incremental property tax to fund public improvements.
- Tex. Tax Code Ch. 312 — Property tax abatement in a reinvestment zone. Requires a separately adopted abatement policy and a reinvestment zone designation.

**Confidentiality:** Tex. Gov't Code § 552.131 exempts certain economic development information from public disclosure when release would harm the governmental body's competitive position. The exception is narrow and time-limited; once an agreement is executed, the substantive terms are generally public.

**Public hearing and posting:** Ch. 380 and Ch. 381 agreements are adopted in open meeting. Type B § 505.158 projects require a separate notice and hearing. TIRZ creation requires its own statutory process under Ch. 311.

**Retention:** Executed incentive agreements retain permanently. Drafts retain for the period set by the local government's adopted retention schedule, typically aligned to the Texas State Library Local Government Retention Schedule.
