# Cedar Ridge Advanced Manufacturing — Chapter 380 Incentive Proposal

**Prepared by:** Sofia Ramirez, Economic Development Director, City of Cedar Ridge
**Prepared for:** Mayor Castillo and Cedar Ridge City Council
**Date:** May 10, 2026
**Project code name during pre-decision phase:** Project Anvil
**AI-assisted draft, reviewed by Sofia Ramirez, ED Director**

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Project Anvil is a fictional advanced manufacturing facility proposed by Anvil Precision Manufacturing, LLC, a tier-2 supplier to the automotive and industrial robotics sectors. The project would site a 220,000-square-foot facility on a 32-acre parcel within the Cedar Ridge Business Park, representing $28.0 million in capital investment and 145 full-time jobs at an average wage of $24.00 per hour with employer-paid health benefits. The applicant requests a structured Chapter 380 grant package totaling an estimated $3.85 million over an 8-year term, including a property tax rebate, a one-time infrastructure cost-share, and limited permit fee waivers. Staff recommends approval in principle subject to negotiation of the final agreement, with performance milestones tied to capex, jobs, and wages, and a pro-rata clawback for non-performance.

## 2. Project Description

The facility will manufacture precision-machined components for electric vehicle drivetrain and industrial automation customers. Operations will include CNC machining, heat treatment, assembly, and quality testing. The facility will run two shifts, five days per week initially, ramping to three shifts within 36 months. Anvil expects 60% of finished output to ship to two anchor customers in the Texas Triangle. Construction is expected to begin Q4 2026 with certificate of occupancy in Q3 2027 and first hires within 60 days of CO.

## 3. Applicant Background

Anvil Precision Manufacturing, LLC was formed in 2014 and operates two facilities in the Midwest. The applicant has no prior incentive defaults known to staff. References from two prior host jurisdictions confirm timely performance on capex and job commitments. Ownership is a closely held family partnership with no public reporting obligations.

## 5. Public Benefit Analysis

Over the proposed 8-year term, staff estimates the project will generate:

- **Incremental city property tax (before rebate):** approximately $2.96 million on real and business personal property at the current $0.5285/$100 rate, assuming standard depreciation.
- **One-time sales tax on construction materials and equipment:** approximately $185,000 to the city general fund.
- **Annual induced workforce spending in Cedar Ridge:** estimated $4.2 million in payroll spent locally based on the BR&E spending-pattern survey conducted with existing Cedar Ridge manufacturers.
- **Supply chain spend with local vendors:** Anvil estimates $1.1 million annually in maintenance, logistics, and packaging services sourced from Cedar Ridge and Caddo County vendors.

## 6. Requested Incentives Structure

| Component | Authority | Mechanism | Amount | Schedule |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Property tax rebate | Ch. 380 | 75% of incremental city ad valorem tax | ~$2.22M | Annual, Years 1–8 |
| Infrastructure cost-share | Ch. 380 | One-time reimbursement of off-site water and street extensions | $1.50M | Paid in two tranches on milestone completion |
| Permit fee waiver | Ch. 380 | Waiver of building permit and plan review fees | ~$130K | At permit issuance |
| **Estimated total package value** | | | **~$3.85M** | |

A separate Tex. Tax Code Ch. 312 abatement is not proposed; the Ch. 380 rebate accomplishes the equivalent economic effect with simpler administration and clearer clawback enforceability.

## 7. Performance Metrics and Milestones

| Milestone | Evidence | Disbursement gated |
|---|---|---|
| Capex of at least $22M by end of Year 1 | Paid invoices and certified investment report | Year 1 property tax rebate |
| 60 FTE by end of Year 1, 145 FTE by end of Year 3 | Quarterly TWC C-3 filings, payroll certification | Each year's rebate |
| Average wage at or above $24.00/hour in Years 1–2, $25.00 by Year 4 | Payroll certification | Each year's rebate |
| Employer-paid health benefits maintained | Plan documentation | Each year's rebate |

If Anvil falls short on jobs by more than 10% in any year, the rebate for that year is reduced pro-rata. If the wage floor is breached, the rebate for that year is forfeited.

## 8. Clawback Provisions

- **Sale, relocation, or cessation of operations within the term:** Full pro-rata recapture of all disbursed grants, calculated on the unfulfilled portion of the term.
- **Jobs shortfall greater than 25% in any year:** Pro-rata reduction in that year's payment and a 12-month cure period before recapture.
- **Wage floor breach for two consecutive quarters:** Forfeiture of that year's payment.
- **Material default on any agreement covenant:** Acceleration of recapture after a 30-day cure period.

Recapture is secured by a recorded performance covenant on the real property and by a personal guaranty of the parent entity capped at the disbursed grant amount.

## 9. Term and Reporting

- **Term:** 8 years from the date of certificate of occupancy.
- **Annual compliance report:** Due each March 1, covering the prior calendar year.
- **Audit rights:** City may audit payroll, capex, and TWC filings upon 30 days' notice.
- **Notice:** All notices to City Manager Daniel Ahn with copy to the City Attorney.

## 10. Recommended Council Action

> Motion that the City Council approve in principle the Chapter 380 incentive structure for Project Anvil as set forth in this proposal, direct the City Manager and City Attorney to negotiate a definitive agreement consistent with these terms, and return the executed draft agreement to Council for final consideration at a regular meeting.
