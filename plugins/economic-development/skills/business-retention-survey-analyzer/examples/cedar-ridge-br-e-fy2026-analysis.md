## 1. Reporting Period and Universe

The FY2026 BR&E cycle targeted 78 businesses across two sectors: manufacturing (NAICS 31–33), 52 businesses, and professional services (NAICS 54), 26 businesses. Targeting was based on a combined list of property-tax-roll BPP filers with 10+ employees and Cedar Ridge EDC contact records. Sole proprietorships and businesses with fewer than 10 employees were excluded.

## 3. Sector Health Indicators

**Manufacturing (42 respondents):** Sector health appears stable to growing. Sixty-eight percent of manufacturing respondents reported capacity utilization at or above 80%. Forty-five percent reported committed capital investment in the next 12 months. Twenty-six percent reported active hiring difficulty for at least one critical occupation, most often welders, machinists, or first-line supervisors.

**Professional services (18 respondents):** Sector health appears stable. The most consistent theme is hybrid work changing real estate needs. Six respondents are considering smaller footprints at lease renewal; two are considering larger footprints to support hiring.

## 4. At-Risk Businesses (Internal — Do Not Distribute)

**Tier 1 — Imminent risk (2 businesses):**

- **[Business A] (precision metals, 38 FTE):** Capacity constraint cited as material. Owner reports that the current 18,000-sf facility cannot accommodate a new product line. Has been approached by a neighboring jurisdiction. Lease expires Q2 2027. Recommended action: site visit by ED Director and EDC Chair within 14 days; surface available expansion options on the EDC-controlled inventory.
- **[Business B] (industrial services, 22 FTE):** Ownership transition signal. Founder is 67, has not designated a successor, and indicated openness to selling to an out-of-state strategic. Recommended action: confidential conversation with succession planning resources; introduction to Texas Workforce Commission and SBDC succession resources.

## 5. Expansion Opportunities

**Tier 1 — Imminent (3 businesses):**

- **[Business C] (electrical components, 56 FTE):** Active site search for a 25,000-sf expansion. Has identified Parcel D-3 in the business park as a potential target. Recommended action: EDC board to consider an expansion incentive proposal at the June meeting.
- **[Business D] (food and beverage manufacturing, 75 FTE):** Adding a second shift in Q3 2026; will require approximately 30 new FTE. Recommended action: Caddo Workforce Solutions to develop a hiring cohort partnership; explore TWC Skills Development Fund eligibility.
- **[Business E] (logistics services, 42 FTE):** Recent customer win is expected to expand operations 40% in 18 months. Recommended action: utility coordination review for electrical service upgrade.

## 6. Supply Chain Insights

Three patterns emerged. First, six manufacturing respondents source specialty fasteners from out-of-state suppliers; one Cedar Ridge respondent (Business F) reports that it could supply this category if it adds capacity. Recommended action: facilitated introduction. Second, four respondents identified packaging as a category where they would prefer a local supplier and currently source from a Houston-area provider. Third, three respondents cited rising freight costs as a structural concern.

## 7. Workforce and Training Insights

Three themes warrant action:

**Theme 1 — Skilled trades pipeline.** Nine manufacturing respondents reported sustained hiring difficulty for welders, machinists, or HVAC technicians. Caddo College currently graduates approximately 35 trades program completers annually; the EDC should explore a 50-completer target through a Skills Development Fund cohort with three to five anchor employers.

**Theme 2 — CDL drivers and dispatchers.** Four logistics and manufacturing respondents reported sustained CDL hiring difficulty. Caddo College's existing CDL program graduates 90 drivers annually; placement data suggests opportunity for a Cedar Ridge-specific employer signing event.

**Theme 3 — First-line supervisors.** Seven respondents across both sectors reported difficulty hiring supervisors. The Texas A&M Engineering Extension Service has a leadership development curriculum that could be offered as a cohort with subsidized seats for Cedar Ridge employers.

## 10. Limitations

This analysis reflects what respondents disclosed in good faith. Non-response was concentrated in two sectors that historically respond at lower rates (small machine shops, professional services firms with fewer than 15 employees). The absence of signals from non-respondents is not evidence of stability. Year-over-year comparison is constrained because the FY2025 cycle used a different sectoral cut.
