# BR&E Signal Taxonomy

A working catalog of expansion signals, retention risk indicators, supply chain signals, and workforce themes, with the underlying survey questions that surface each. Designed for the ED director triaging 40 to 80 survey responses in a single cycle.

## Expansion Signal Tiers

### Tier 1 — Imminent (act within 30 days)

| Signal | Underlying survey question |
|---|---|
| Active site search underway | "Are you currently evaluating sites for expansion?" |
| Capital purchase committed in next 12 months | "What capital investments have you committed to in the next 12 months?" |
| Hiring 10+ FTE in next 6 months | "How many positions do you expect to add in the next 6 months?" |
| Lease or facility decision in next 12 months | "When does your current lease expire?" |

### Tier 2 — Planning (act within 90 days)

| Signal | Underlying survey question |
|---|---|
| Capacity utilization above 90% | "What is your current capacity utilization?" |
| New customer wins changing footprint | "Have you signed new customers that change your space or staffing needs?" |
| Equipment replacement cycle imminent | "When is your next major equipment replacement?" |
| Adding a shift in next 12 months | "Do you plan to add a shift?" |

### Tier 3 — Exploring (track, no immediate action)

| Signal | Underlying survey question |
|---|---|
| General growth optimism with no specific plan | Open-ended "How would you describe your growth outlook?" |
| Interest in workforce training to enable growth | "What training resources would help you grow?" |
| Interest in incentive programs (informational) | "Are you familiar with local incentive programs?" |

## Retention Risk Indicators

### Tier 1 — Imminent (act within 30 days)

| Indicator | Underlying survey question |
|---|---|
| Active relocation evaluation underway | "Are you currently evaluating relocation outside Cedar Ridge?" |
| Ownership transition without succession plan within 12 months | "What is your succession or transition plan?" |
| Primary customer loss of 30%+ revenue | "Have you experienced significant customer concentration changes?" |
| Lease expiration in next 12 months with no renewal commitment | "When does your current lease expire? Have you committed to renew?" |

### Tier 2 — Concern (act within 90 days)

| Indicator | Underlying survey question |
|---|---|
| Utility or facility constraint cited as growth blocker | "What constraints are limiting your growth here?" |
| Hiring inability sustained over 6+ months for critical roles | "What positions have you been unable to fill?" |
| Regulatory friction cited as material concern | "Have you encountered regulatory or permitting issues that affect your operations?" |
| Workforce attrition above sector norm | "How does your turnover compare to your sector?" |

### Tier 3 — Watch (track, no immediate action)

| Indicator | Underlying survey question |
|---|---|
| General concerns about long-term competitiveness | Open-ended "What are your top three concerns about operating here?" |
| Sector headwinds (industry-wide, not business-specific) | Sector context |
| Owner age and lifestyle signals | Demographic survey questions |

## Supply Chain Signals

| Signal | Underlying survey question |
|---|---|
| Top suppliers sourced out of state | "Where are your top three suppliers located?" |
| Specific component or service the business wishes were available locally | "Are there suppliers you wish were closer?" |
| Logistics cost as a growing share of cost of goods | "How have your logistics costs trended?" |
| Local supplier-development opportunity | Cross-reference: another local respondent could supply this |

## Workforce Themes

Cluster individual workforce signals into 3 to 5 themes per cycle:

| Theme cluster | Source signals |
|---|---|
| Skilled trades pipeline | Hiring difficulty for welders, machinists, electricians, HVAC techs, plumbers |
| CDL and logistics | Hiring difficulty for drivers, dispatchers, warehouse leads |
| Production technicians | Hiring difficulty for operators, assemblers, QC technicians |
| Professional and technical | Hiring difficulty for engineers, accountants, software roles |
| First-line supervisors | Hiring difficulty for leads, supervisors, managers |

For each theme, identify the workforce board or community college partner most aligned to act on it, and the specific program design (cohort training, work-based learning, paid pre-apprenticeship) that fits.

## Limitations to Disclose in Every Analysis

- Self-disclosure bias: respondents may understate retention risk to avoid telegraphing weakness or overstate expansion to access incentives.
- Non-response bias: at-risk businesses are more likely to decline a BR&E interview. The absence of a signal is not evidence of stability.
- Single-cycle data: signals are most reliable when compared to a prior baseline.
- Confidentiality: BR&E findings are shared in confidence. Aggregate where the audience extends beyond the ED office.
